Texte 10 : Quotidien gratuit

Trois nouveaux projets de quotidien gratuit sont en chantier. 
Arrivés en Belgique il y a dix ans, les gratuits ont fragilisé

gratuits ; la sixième chaîne de télévision va investir dans 
les mensuels « Homme en ville » et « Femme en ville ». De 
quoi fragiliser encore un peu plus la presse traditionnelle, 
et surtout ses circuits de distribution.

🔍 STEP 2: HOW TO APPROACH IT

Why this text is your FOURTH challenge

In Projects 1–3, you dealt with events (toast), reviews (album), and history (Elle magazine). The logic was: something happened → the text describes it → you find the description.

This text introduces BUSINESS LOGIC. It doesn't just describe — it explains WHY things happen, shows HOW actors react, and projects WHAT comes next. You need to track a chain of causation:

Chapter 1-3: EVENT → DESCRIPTION → FIND IT
                         ↓
Chapter 4:   SITUATION → CAUSE → REACTION → CONSEQUENCE → FUTURE

And for the FIRST TIME, you face a "best title" question — a question type that tests whether you understand the MAIN IDEA of the entire text, not just one detail.

The anatomy of THIS specific text

TYPE:        Article économique / médias (business/media article)
LENGTH:      ~130 words (short)
STRUCTURE:   Paragraph 1 = SITUATION (free papers multiply, paid press reacts)
             Paragraph 2 = CAUSE (why free papers succeed)
             Paragraph 3 = FUTURE (next developments, further impact)
DIFFICULTY:  ★★☆☆☆ (2/5)
TRAP LEVEL:  ★★★☆☆ (3/5) — requires understanding business dynamics

🧩 STEP 3: HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

QUESTION 14: Quel titre convient le mieux à ce texte?

This is a TITLE question — first time encountering this type.

A title question is FUNDAMENTALLY different from other questions. It doesn't ask about one fact, one person, or one moment. It asks: What is this ENTIRE text about?

The Title-Matching Method (NEW — your most important tool for title questions)

TITLE-MATCHING METHOD:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

STEP 1: Identify the MAIN SUBJECT of the text
        → What appears in EVERY paragraph?

STEP 2: Identify the MAIN ANGLE
        → Is the text positive, negative, neutral, alarming?

STEP 3: Test each title against TWO criteria:
        CRITERION A — SCOPE: Does it cover the WHOLE text?
        CRITERION B — ANGLE: Does it match the text's tone/direction?

STEP 4: Eliminate titles that are:
        → Too NARROW (cover only one paragraph)
        → Too BROAD (cover topics not in the text)
        → Wrong ANGLE (positive when text is negative, or vice versa)
        → MISLEADING (suggest something the text doesn't say)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Let me apply this method:

STEP 1: What is the MAIN SUBJECT?

Paragraph 1: Free papers grow → paid press weakened
Paragraph 2: Free papers profit → paid papers lose money
Paragraph 3: Free papers expand to magazines → paid press further weakened

RECURRING THREAD: The IMPACT of free newspapers on PAID press
Every paragraph mentions BOTH free papers AND paid press
The text is about the THREAT free papers pose to paid press

STEP 2: What is the MAIN ANGLE?

Key vocabulary:
"fragilisé" (weakened) → NEGATIVE for paid press
"perdent de l'argent" (losing money) → NEGATIVE for paid press
"fragiliser encore un peu plus" (weaken even more) → GROWING threat
"De quoi fragiliser..." → WARNING tone

ANGLE: The text is a WARNING about the growing threat to paid press

STEP 3: Test each title

Choice A: "Ils sont fous, ces gratuits"

SCOPE: About free papers only → misses the paid press dimension
ANGLE: "Fous" (crazy) suggests irrationality or excess → 
       but the text says free papers are PROFITABLE, not crazy
VERDICT: Wrong scope AND wrong angle

❌ ELIMINATED. The text describes free papers as SUCCESSFUL and rational business ventures. "Fous" (crazy) contradicts this. Also, the text's main story is about the IMPACT on paid press, not about free papers being crazy.

Choice B: "Les gratuits se font concurrence"

SCOPE: Suggests free papers compete AGAINST EACH OTHER
TEXT: Free papers compete against PAID press, not against each other
The Habas example avoids "concurrence frontale avec les autres quotidiens"
by distributing in the evening — but this is ONE detail, not the main story
VERDICT: Wrong focus — the competition is free vs paid, not free vs free

❌ ELIMINATED. This is a Focus Misdirection trap. The word "concurrence" IS in the text, but it describes Habas avoiding competition with OTHER free papers — it's a minor strategic detail, NOT the main story. The main conflict is free papers vs. paid press.

🆕 NEW TRAP TYPE: Focus Misdirection

The title uses a word or concept from the text but applies it to the WRONG relationship or the WRONG conflict. The text is about X vs Y, but the title suggests X vs X.

Choice C: "Les quotidiens gagnent des lecteurs"

SCOPE: Suggests paid dailies are GAINING readers
TEXT: Paid dailies are LOSING money and being "fragilisés"
VERDICT: OPPOSITE of what the text says

❌ ELIMINATED. Direct contradiction. The text says paid papers are weakened, not growing. This is a Direction Reversal trap (Project 3) applied to a title.

Choice D: "Nouvelle alerte pour la presse payante"

SCOPE: "Presse payante" = paid press → the main victim in ALL three paragraphs ✓
       "Nouvelle alerte" = new warning → matches the escalating threat ✓
       
COVERAGE CHECK:
P1: Free papers multiply → paid press weakened → ALERT ✓
P2: Free papers profit while paid papers lose → ALERT ✓
P3: Free papers expand to magazines → paid press "encore un peu plus" weakened → ALERT ✓

ANGLE: "Alerte" (alert/warning) → matches the WARNING tone of the text ✓

VERDICT: Covers ALL three paragraphs. Matches the angle. Perfect fit.

✅ CORRECT. "Nouvelle alerte pour la presse payante" captures both the SCOPE (the entire text is about threats to paid press) and the ANGLE (the text has an escalating warning tone: weakened → losing money → even more weakened).

✅ Answer 14: D

💡 KEY INSIGHT: The Best Title Test

THE BEST TITLE:
✓ Covers ALL paragraphs, not just one
✓ Matches the text's TONE (warning, celebration, criticism, etc.)
✓ Names the MAIN subject or victim, not a secondary detail
✓ Captures the text's DIRECTION (growing, declining, changing, etc.)

THE WRONG TITLE:
✗ Covers only ONE paragraph or detail
✗ Contradicts the text's tone
✗ Focuses on a secondary actor or detail
✗ Reverses the text's direction

QUESTION 15: La presse payante réagit en...

Layer targeted: REACTION (Paragraph 1)

Critical word: "réagit" (reacts)

The question asks: HOW does the paid press respond to the free paper threat?

Hunt in paragraph 1:

"Les éditeurs et les investisseurs s'inclinent devant leur 
succès, en tentant de RÉPLIQUER."

"Répliquer" = to replicate, to respond, to fight back

But HOW do they replicate? The next sentence gives the specific example:

"le président du groupe de communication Habas a décidé de 
LANCER SON QUOTIDIEN GRATUIT avant l'été."

So the reaction is: launching their OWN free newspaper. The paid press responds by entering the free paper market themselves.

ELIMINATE:

Choice A: "diversifiant leurs ressources financières"

"diversifiant leurs ressources financières" = diversifying their financial resources

The text doesn't mention financial diversification. It mentions launching a free paper — which is a PRODUCT strategy, not a financial strategy. Nothing about seeking new funding, selling assets, or finding new revenue streams.

❌ ELIMINATED. Invention — the concept of financial diversification appears nowhere in the text. The reaction is about creating a new PRODUCT, not restructuring finances.

Choice B: "investissant dans la distribution"

"distribution" is mentioned in two places:
P2: "large distribution" → about free papers' success factor
P3: "circuits de distribution" → about future impact on paid press

But NEITHER describes the paid press's REACTION.

❌ ELIMINATED. Word Reuse trap (Project 1). "Distribution" appears in the text but in contexts describing free papers' advantage and future impact — NOT as the paid press's reaction strategy.

Choice C: "s'alliant avec d'autres quotidiens"

"s'alliant" = forming alliances with other dailies

The text never mentions alliances between newspapers. Habas launches his OWN paper. No partnerships, no coalitions, no joint ventures mentioned.

❌ ELIMINATED. Pure invention. No alliance is described anywhere in the text.

Choice D: "publiant son propre gratuit"

Text: "le président du groupe [...] a décidé de LANCER 
SON quotidien GRATUIT"

Choice: "publiant SON PROPRE GRATUIT"

LANCER          →    PUBLIANT (launching → publishing)
SON quotidien   →    SON PROPRE (his own)
GRATUIT         →    GRATUIT (free)

And more broadly:

"Les éditeurs [...] s'inclinent devant leur succès, 
en tentant de RÉPLIQUER"

RÉPLIQUER = to replicate/respond
HOW? → By launching their own free paper
= PUBLIANT SON PROPRE GRATUIT ✓

✅ CORRECT. The paid press reacts by publishing its own free newspaper. The Habas example is the concrete illustration of this strategy.

✅ Answer 15: D

QUESTION 16: Dans un avenir proche, il y aura...

Layer targeted: FUTURE/PROJECTION (Paragraph 3)

Critical words: "Dans un avenir proche" = in the near future

This anchors you in the LAST PARAGRAPH, which starts with "Prochaine étape" (next step).

Hunt in paragraph 3:

"Prochaine étape, le développement d'HEBDOS et de MENSUELS 
gratuits ; la sixième chaîne de télévision va investir dans 
les mensuels « Homme en ville » et « Femme en ville »."

Let me decode the key vocabulary:

HEBDOS    = hebdomadaires = WEEKLY publications
MENSUELS  = MONTHLY publications
GRATUITS  = free (no charge)

Current situation: free DAILY newspapers (quotidiens)
Next step: free WEEKLY and MONTHLY publications
         = free publications at MAGAZINE frequency

Now a critical vocabulary connection:

QUOTIDIEN = daily → NEWSPAPER format
HEBDO     = weekly → can be newspaper OR magazine format
MENSUEL   = monthly → typically MAGAZINE format

"Hebdos et mensuels" = publications that come out weekly and monthly
= MAGAZINE-format publications (as opposed to daily newspapers)

ELIMINATE:

Choice A: "un gratuit sur les médias"

"sur les médias" = ABOUT the media (content = media topics)
Text says: "Homme en ville" and "Femme en ville" 
→ These are LIFESTYLE titles, not media-focused titles

❌ ELIMINATED. The text mentions a TV channel (sixième chaîne) INVESTING in magazines — but the magazines are not ABOUT media. "Homme en ville" and "Femme en ville" are lifestyle/urban living titles. This is a Subject Swap trap (Project 1) — the actor (TV channel) is confused with the content (about media).

Choice C: "une émission sur la sixième chaîne"

"une émission" = a TV show/program
Text says: "la sixième chaîne va INVESTIR dans les MENSUELS"
→ The TV channel is INVESTING in PRINT publications
→ NOT creating a TV show

❌ ELIMINATED. The sixth channel is an INVESTOR, not a broadcaster in this context. It's putting money into MAGAZINES, not producing TV content. This is a Role Confusion trap — the actor's usual function (broadcasting TV shows) is assumed to be their action here, but the text says they're doing something DIFFERENT (investing in print).

🆕 NEW TRAP TYPE: Role Confusion

An actor is known for one role (TV channel → makes TV shows), but the text describes them doing something DIFFERENT (investing in magazines). The answer assumes the actor's DEFAULT role instead of reading what they ACTUALLY do in the text.

Choice D: "de nouvelles formes de distribution"

Text mentions: "circuits de distribution" being affected
But this is a CONSEQUENCE, not a future product
The question asks what there WILL BE, not what will be affected

❌ ELIMINATED. The text mentions distribution circuits being further weakened — but that's the IMPACT, not the new development. The question asks "il y aura" (there will be) — meaning what NEW THING will exist. Layer Confusion (Project 2) — consequence information is presented as product information.

Choice B: "des gratuits au format magazine"

Text: "développement d'HEBDOS et de MENSUELS gratuits"
     = development of free WEEKLIES and MONTHLIES

Choice: "des gratuits au FORMAT MAGAZINE"
       = free publications in MAGAZINE format

HEBDOS + MENSUELS = publications with weekly/monthly frequency
                  = MAGAZINE format (not daily newspaper format)
GRATUITS = free

"Gratuits au format magazine" perfectly captures 
"hebdos et mensuels gratuits" ✓

✅ CORRECT. The text announces free weeklies and monthlies as the "prochaine étape." These are magazine-format publications (as opposed to the daily newspaper format that currently dominates the free press market).

✅ Answer 16: B

🎯 STEP 4: WHAT COULD BE DONE

Strategy 1: The Title-Matching Method (NEW — essential for all title questions)

You learned this during Q14. Let me formalize it as a permanent tool:

TITLE-MATCHING METHOD — PERMANENT CHECKLIST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

□ SCOPE TEST: Does the title cover ALL paragraphs?
  → If it covers only P1 → too narrow → eliminate
  → If it covers topics not in the text → too broad → eliminate

□ ANGLE TEST: Does the title match the text's tone?
  → Text is a warning? Title should suggest alert/danger
  → Text is positive? Title should suggest success/progress
  → Text is neutral? Title should be descriptive, not emotional

□ SUBJECT TEST: Does the title name the MAIN subject?
  → Who/what is the text PRIMARILY about?
  → A good title names this main subject

□ DIRECTION TEST: Does the title capture the text's movement?
  → Growing, declining, changing, threatening, celebrating?

GOLDEN RULE: The best title is the one you'd see in a 
newspaper that makes you think "Yes, that's exactly 
what this article is about."

✅ STEP 5: WHAT SHOULD YOU DO

Rules for Business/Economy Texts

1. Map the CAUSE-EFFECT CHAIN before answering

Business texts follow logic: situation → cause → reaction → consequence → future. Identify each link. Questions will target specific links.

2. For TITLE questions, test SCOPE first

The most common error on title questions is picking a title that's true but TOO NARROW. A good title covers the WHOLE text, not just one paragraph.

3. Distinguish ACTORS from ACTIONS

When the text says "the TV channel invests in magazines," the ACTOR is the TV channel and the ACTION is investing in print. Don't assume the actor is doing their default job (making TV shows).

4. Track TEMPORAL markers in each paragraph

P1: "Arrivés il y a dix ans" → PAST (10 years ago)
    "a décidé de lancer" → PRESENT decision
P2: "Cette multiplication" → CURRENT situation
P3: "Prochaine étape" → FUTURE

When a question says "dans un avenir proche," go DIRECTLY to the paragraph with future markers.

5. Build vocabulary bridges between the text and answer choices

The text says "mensuels," the answer says "format magazine." You need to know these refer to the same thing. Build your vocabulary:

ESSENTIAL VOCABULARY FOR MEDIA TEXTS:
quotidien → daily newspaper
hebdomadaire (hebdo) → weekly magazine
mensuel → monthly magazine
bimensuel → bi-monthly
trimestriel → quarterly
diffusion → circulation
tirage → print run
lectorat → readership
parution → publication/issue

❌ STEP 6: WHAT SHOULD YOU NOT DO

❌ DON'T pick a title based on one paragraph

"Les gratuits se font concurrence" (Choice B) describes ONE detail from ONE paragraph. A title must capture the WHOLE text.

❌ DON'T assume an actor does their "default" job

A TV channel investing in magazines ≠ a TV channel making TV shows. Read what the text SAYS the actor does, not what you EXPECT them to do.

❌ DON'T confuse CONSEQUENCE with PRODUCT

"Distribution circuits being weakened" is a CONSEQUENCE. "Free magazines being launched" is a PRODUCT. When the question asks "what will there be," it asks about products/developments, not consequences.

❌ DON'T confuse the CONTENT of a publication with its INVESTOR

The sixth TV channel INVESTS in magazines. The magazines are not ABOUT television or media. The investor's identity ≠ the product's content.

❌ DON'T pick "sounds right" titles — TEST them

"Les quotidiens gagnent des lecteurs" (Choice C) SOUNDS like a plausible newspaper headline. But it CONTRADICTS the text. A title must be TRUE and COMPREHENSIVE, not just plausible-sounding.
