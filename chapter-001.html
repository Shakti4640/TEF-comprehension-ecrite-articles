CHAPTER 1: BROKEN WINDOWS AT SAINT-JEAN

📰 Texte 89 — Crime Report | Phase 1: Foundation Thinking

📄 THE COMPLETE ARTICLE

Deux vitrines brisées : Les auteurs interpellés

De nouvelles dégradations ont été commises, au cours de la soirée de mercredi, dans le centre-ville de Saint-Jean. Entre 21 heures et 22 heures, un groupe de jeunes passablement éméchés a brisé la vitrine de la Banque Royale et la porte de la pharmacie, rue de Nice. Appelés aussitôt par des témoins, les gendarmes ont interpellé les auteurs en flagrant délit : trois jeunes de la ville et des environs, âgés d'une vingtaine d'années. Ils ont été placés en garde à vue à la gendarmerie, où l'enquête se poursuit, car il semble que ces nouvelles dégradations se situent dans le prolongement des vols et dégradations effectués ces dernières semaines.

D'après La Nouvelle République

🔍 THE THREE QUESTIONS

Question 15 : Les auteurs du délit ont été

  • A — arrêtés sur les lieux du délit.
  • B — libérés.
  • C — arrêtés chez eux.
  • D — blessés.

Question 16 : Ils étaient tous originaires

  • A — de Nice.
  • B — de la même région.
  • C — de Saint-Jean.
  • D — d'une région différente.

Question 17 : L'enquête se poursuit parce que

  • A — d'autres coupables sont recherchés.
  • B — le montant des dégâts n'est pas connu.
  • C — ce ne sont pas les premières dégradations commises.
  • D — des témoins manquent à l'appel.

🧠 HOW TO APPROACH IT

This is your very first article. Everything you learn here becomes the permanent method for every chapter that follows. We are not just solving three questions — we are building a reading machine in your brain.

STEP 0: Understand what "Compréhension Écrite" actually tests

It does NOT test:

  • Whether you know every word
  • Whether you can translate the article into your language
  • Whether you can write a summary

It DOES test:

  • Can you find where in the text the answer hides?
  • Can you tell the difference between what the text SAYS and what it ALMOST says?
  • Can you avoid traps designed to catch fast, careless readers?

STEP 1: Read the QUESTIONS first — ALWAYS

This is the single most important rule in Compréhension Écrite.

Why? Because the text is a forest. The questions are your map. If you enter the forest without a map, you wander. If you read the map first, you walk straight to the treasure.

Before touching the article, read all three questions (just the questions, not yet the answer choices):

Q15: "Les auteurs du délit ont été..." → I need to find WHAT HAPPENED TO THEM
Q16: "Ils étaient tous originaires..." → I need to find WHERE THEY COME FROM
Q17: "L'enquête se poursuit parce que..." → I need to find WHY the investigation continues

Now your brain has three search targets. When you read the text, these targets will light up like beacons.

STEP 2: Read the TITLE — it's a free gift

Deux vitrines brisées : Les auteurs interpellés

The title already tells you:

  • What happened: Two windows were broken
  • What resulted: The perpetrators were caught ("interpellés")

You haven't even read the article yet and you already know the outcome. This is not cheating — this is strategic reading.

STEP 3: Now read the text — ONE time, actively

Read the text with your three search targets in mind. As you read, mentally tag the information:

Text segmentWhat it tells youWhich question it serves
"De nouvelles dégradations"This is NOT the first timeQ17
"un groupe de jeunes passablement éméchés"They were somewhat drunkBackground info
"a brisé la vitrine de la Banque Royale et la porte de la pharmacie"What they didBackground info
"rue de Nice"Location of the crime — it's a STREET nameQ16 (trap alert!)
"les gendarmes ont interpellé les auteurs en flagrant délit"Caught in the act = on the spotQ15
"trois jeunes de la ville et des environs"From the city AND surroundingsQ16
"placés en garde à vue"Detained, not releasedQ15 (confirms)
"ces nouvelles dégradations se situent dans le prolongement des vols et dégradations effectués ces dernières semaines"There were PREVIOUS incidentsQ17

One read. All three answers located. This is the power of reading questions first.

✅ HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

QUESTION 15: Les auteurs du délit ont été...

The key phrase in the text:

"les gendarmes ont interpellé les auteurs en flagrant délit"

Let's understand this crucial legal expression:

FrenchMeaning
en flagrant délitcaught in the act — meaning they were caught AT THE SCENE, WHILE committing or just after committing the crime

Now let's test each answer:

ChoiceAnalysisVerdict
A — arrêtés sur les lieux du délit"en flagrant délit" = caught at the scene of the crime. This matches perfectly.✅ CORRECT
B — libérésThe text says "placés en garde à vue" (detained). The opposite of released.❌ Contradicts text
C — arrêtés chez eux"en flagrant délit" means at the scene, NOT at home.❌ Wrong location
D — blessésNothing in the text mentions injuries to anyone.❌ Not mentioned

✅ Answer: A

The method revealed: When you see "en flagrant délit," it ALWAYS means caught at the scene. This is a fixed legal expression. Learn it once, use it forever.

QUESTION 16: Ils étaient tous originaires...

The key phrase in the text:

"trois jeunes de la ville et des environs"

Let's break this down word by word:

FrenchMeaning
de la villefrom the city (Saint-Jean)
et des environsAND from the surrounding area

This means: NOT all from Saint-Jean. Some from the city, some from nearby. But they're all from the same general region.

Now let's test each answer:

ChoiceAnalysisVerdict
A — de Nice"Rue de Nice" is the NAME of a STREET in Saint-Jean. It is NOT the city of Nice. This is a classic trap!❌ TRAP — confusing street name with city
B — de la même région"De la ville et des environs" = the same region (city + surroundings). This matches.✅ CORRECT
C — de Saint-JeanOnly SOME are from the city itself. Others are from "les environs." So "tous de Saint-Jean" is too narrow.❌ Too specific
D — d'une région différenteThey're from the same city/area, not different regions.❌ Contradicts text

✅ Answer: B

The trap revealed: "Rue de Nice" is a street name inside Saint-Jean. The test deliberately places "de Nice" as option A to catch students who:

  1. Read too fast
  2. See "Nice" in the text and jump to the answer
  3. Don't understand the difference between a street name and a city name

This is THE foundational trap type you will encounter in every exam: a word from the text appears in a wrong answer to lure you.

🚨 GOLDEN RULE #1: Just because a word from the text appears in an answer choice does NOT make it correct. In fact, it often makes it a TRAP.

QUESTION 17: L'enquête se poursuit parce que...

The key phrase in the text:

"l'enquête se poursuit, car il semble que ces nouvelles dégradations se situent dans le prolongement des vols et dégradations effectués ces dernières semaines"

Let's decode this sentence layer by layer:

ExpressionMeaning
"ces nouvelles dégradations"THESE new acts of vandalism — meaning there were OLD ones
"dans le prolongement"as a continuation of / following up on
"des vols et dégradations effectués"thefts and damage that were carried out
"ces dernières semaines"in recent weeks

The logic chain: The investigation continues BECAUSE this incident appears to be connected to PREVIOUS crimes committed in recent weeks.

Now let's test each answer:

ChoiceAnalysisVerdict
A — d'autres coupables sont recherchésThe text doesn't say they're looking for more suspects. Three were caught.❌ Not stated
B — le montant des dégâts n'est pas connuThe text never discusses the cost of damage.❌ Not mentioned
C — ce ne sont pas les premières dégradations commises"Nouvelles dégradations" + "dans le prolongement des vols... ces dernières semaines" = there were PREVIOUS incidents. PERFECT match.✅ CORRECT
D — des témoins manquent à l'appelThe text says witnesses CALLED the police ("Appelés aussitôt par des témoins"). They were present and responsive.❌ Contradicts text

✅ Answer: C

The method revealed: The word "car" (because) is your signpost. Whenever you see a question asking "pourquoi" or "parce que," search the text for causal connectors:

Causal connectorEnglish
carbecause / for
parce quebecause
puisquesince
en raison dedue to
à cause debecause of
en effetindeed / in fact
c'est pourquoithat's why

Find the connector → the answer is right next to it.

🎯 WHAT COULD BE DONE (Strategic Options)

When facing a short crime-report article like this, you have three possible strategies:

Strategy A: Question-First Scan (RECOMMENDED)

Read questions → Read title → Read text once with targets → Answer
Time: 3-4 minutes | Accuracy: Very High

Strategy B: Full-Read-Then-Answer

Read entire text → Read questions → Re-read text for answers
Time: 5-6 minutes | Accuracy: Medium (memory fades)

Strategy C: Question-by-Question

Read Q1 → Scan text for Q1 answer → Read Q2 → Scan for Q2 → etc.
Time: 6-7 minutes | Accuracy: Medium (fragmented understanding)

For this exam, Strategy A is ALWAYS superior. You read the text only once, but with purpose. Every sentence you read is being filtered through your question targets.

✅ WHAT YOU SHOULD DO

During the exam — for EVERY article:

  1. Read ALL questions first (just the question stems, not the choices yet)
  2. Read the title — extract the theme in 5 seconds
  3. Read the text ONCE, actively hunting for your targets
  4. Go to each question, NOW read the answer choices
  5. Eliminate impossible answers first (usually 2 are clearly wrong)
  6. Choose between the remaining 2 by going back to the EXACT phrase in the text
  7. Never leave a question blank — even a guess has 25% chance

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL #1: The "Familiar Word" Trap 🪤

Text says: "rue de Nice"
Answer A says: "de Nice"
Student thinks: "I saw 'Nice' in the text! That must be the answer!"

Reality: "Rue de Nice" is a street in Saint-Jean. The perpetrators are not from Nice.

How to avoid it: When you see a text-word in an answer choice, be EXTRA suspicious. Ask yourself: "Is this word being used the SAME WAY in the text and in the answer?"

PITFALL #2: The "Too Specific" Trap 🎯

Text says: "de la ville et des environs"
Answer C says: "de Saint-Jean"
Student thinks: "The city IS Saint-Jean, so C must be right!"

Reality: "De la ville ET DES ENVIRONS" means some are from the city and some from nearby areas. Saying they're ALL from Saint-Jean is too narrow.

How to avoid it: Watch for the word "tous" (all) in the question. If the question says "tous originaires," your answer must cover ALL of them, not just some.

PITFALL #3: The "Sounds Reasonable" Trap 🤔

Question: "Why does the investigation continue?"
Answer A: "Other suspects are being searched for"
Student thinks: "That sounds like a reasonable reason for continuing an investigation!"

Reality: It sounds reasonable, but the text NEVER says this. The text gives a SPECIFIC reason: connection to previous crimes.

How to avoid it: Your answer must be PROVABLE from the text. If you can't point to a specific line, the answer is wrong — no matter how logical it sounds.

PITFALL #4: The "Contradiction Blindness" Trap 🙈

Text says: "Appelés aussitôt par des témoins"
Answer D says: "des témoins manquent à l'appel"

The text says witnesses CALLED police. The answer says witnesses are MISSING. These are opposites. Students who read too fast miss this contradiction.

How to avoid it: For each answer choice, ask: "Does the text say the OPPOSITE of this?" If yes, eliminate immediately.
