📄 STEP 1: THE COMPLETE ARTICLE

Texte 20 : Les Koguis, gardiens de notre planète

À la fin du XVIIe siècle, après soixante-quinze années 
d'affrontements avec les conquistadores espagnols, les Koguis 
ont décidé de se retirer du monde "civilisé." Aujourd'hui, ils 
n'ont plus rien à craindre des Espagnols, mais leur vie n'est 
guère plus facile et leur civilisation semble être condamnée à 
s'éteindre. Menacés par différents groupes de guérilleros qui 
sévissent en Colombie, les narcotrafiquants, les pilleurs de 
tombes et même les paysans sans terre qui s'installent sur leur 
territoire, ils n'ont cessé de fuir toujours plus haut, dans 
les vallées de Sierra Nevada ou leur survie devient de plus en 
plus difficile.

Au XVIIe siècle, il y avait environ 500 000 Koguis. 
Aujourd'hui, on n'en dénombre pas plus de 25 000. Pour eux, la 
Sierra Nevada représente le centre du monde. C'est la Terre 
mère qui leur a transmis le code moral et spirituel qui régit 
leur vie sur la Terre. Peuple de sages, les Koguis pratiquent 
une vie spirituelle intense. Leurs enfants sont initiés très 
tôt aux mystères de leur religion, en pratiquant des rites qui 
leur apprennent qu'ils ne sont qu'un élément parmi d'autres 
dans la nature.

Les Koguis considèrent que les hommes ne sont pas propriétaires 
de la Terre, mais que c'est la Terre qui les possède et qui 
prend soin d'eux. Au centre de cet univers spirituel se 
trouvent les Mamus, prêtres et philosophes, qui veillent à 
l'ordre spirituel de la communauté. A l'écoute des phénomènes 
naturels, ce sont eux qui président les cérémonies et les rites 
de leur village, destinés à évoquer les ancêtres ou à remercier 
le dieu du Bien.

Si les Koguis venaient à disparaître, nous perdrions avec eux 
un exemple de sagesse particulièrement précieux pour notre 
époque. Après toutes les erreurs commises à l'encontre des 
éléments, nous nous voyons en effet contraints, à l'aube de ce 
nouveau siècle, de reconsidérer le rapport entre l'économie et 
la sauvegarde de la nature.

🔍 STEP 2: HOW TO APPROACH IT

Why this text is a PHASE 4 challenge

Every text before this one described things you can OBSERVE: flea markets (Project 16), noise effects (Project 17), albums (Project 2), toast recipes (Project 1). You could verify the answers against concrete, material facts.

This text asks you to enter a worldview you do not share. The Koguis believe:

  • The Earth POSSESSES humans (not the reverse)
  • Humans are just ONE element among many in nature
  • A spiritual code transmitted by the Earth governs all human life

You may agree or disagree. It doesn't matter. The exam tests whether you can ACCURATELY REPORT what the text says about their beliefs — without distorting, amplifying, or projecting your own perspective.

🆕 NEW CONCEPT: WORLDVIEW COMPREHENSION
When a text describes a belief system, your job is to be a FAITHFUL REPORTER, not a JUDGE. You must answer "What do the Koguis believe?" — not "What is true?" or "What do I believe?" This is the hardest form of reading because it requires suspending your own framework.

The anatomy of THIS specific text

TYPE:        Article anthropologique / essai engagé 
             (anthropological article with editorial conclusion)
LENGTH:      ~280 words (medium-long)
STRUCTURE:   
  Paragraph 1 = HISTORICAL CONTEXT + CURRENT THREATS
  Paragraph 2 = DEMOGRAPHICS + SPIRITUAL BELIEFS
  Paragraph 3 = CORE WORLDVIEW + ROLE OF MAMUS
  Paragraph 4 = AUTHOR'S EDITORIAL CONCLUSION (what if they 
                disappear?)
DIFFICULTY:  ★★★★☆ (4/5)
TRAP LEVEL:  ★★★★★ (5/5) — worldview inversion traps

What makes this text STRUCTURALLY unique

This is the first text that operates on two temporal planes simultaneously:

TEMPORAL PLANE 1: PAST → PRESENT (historical narrative)
  XVIIe siècle → Aujourd'hui → threats → fleeing higher

TEMPORAL PLANE 2: BELIEF SYSTEM (timeless, spiritual)
  Terre mère → code moral → rites → Mamus → harmony

TEMPORAL PLANE 3: HYPOTHETICAL FUTURE (conditional)
  "Si les Koguis venaient à disparaître..." → consequences

Each question targets a DIFFERENT temporal plane:

  • Q18 → Plane 1 (current situation)
  • Q19 → Plane 2 (belief system)
  • Q20 → Plane 2 (spiritual structure)
  • Q21 → Plane 3 (hypothetical future)

🧩 STEP 3: HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

QUESTION 18: Dans les conditions du monde moderne, la tribu des Koguis...

This question asks about the Koguis' CURRENT SITUATION — Temporal Plane 1.

The key phrase in the question is: "dans les conditions du monde moderne" — meaning TODAY, not in the past.

Locate the relevant passage:

"Aujourd'hui, ils n'ont plus rien à craindre des Espagnols, 
MAIS leur vie n'est guère plus facile et leur civilisation 
semble être condamnée à s'éteindre. Menacés par différents 
groupes de guérilleros qui sévissent en Colombie, les 
narcotrafiquants, les pilleurs de tombes et même les paysans 
sans terre qui s'installent sur leur territoire, ils n'ont 
cessé de FUIR toujours plus HAUT, dans les vallées de Sierra 
Nevada ou leur survie devient de plus en plus difficile."

Extract the core facts about their CURRENT situation:

FACT 1: No longer threatened by SPANISH conquistadors
        ("n'ont plus rien à craindre des Espagnols")

FACT 2: Still threatened by MODERN forces
        (guerrillas, drug traffickers, grave robbers, 
         landless peasants)

FACT 3: They keep FLEEING HIGHER into the mountains
        ("n'ont cessé de fuir toujours plus haut")

FACT 4: Survival is increasingly DIFFICULT
        ("leur survie devient de plus en plus difficile")

SYNTHESIS: They are FORCED to RETREAT, ABANDONING their 
traditional lands, under modern threats

Now eliminate:

Choice A: "mène une guerre perpétuelle et sans merci contre l'univers « civilisé »"

Text says: "les Koguis ont décidé de se RETIRER du monde civilisé"
                                        ^^^^^^^^^^
           "ils n'ont cessé de FUIR toujours plus haut"
                                ^^^^

❌ ELIMINATED — Direct Inversion trap (from Projects 9+). The Koguis are NOT waging war. They are FLEEING. They are not ATTACKING — they are RETREATING. "Se retirer" and "fuir" are the OPPOSITE of "mener une guerre." The text explicitly describes a people in FLIGHT, not in COMBAT.

⚠️ CRITICAL DISTINCTION: The text mentions 75 years of "affrontements" (confrontations) — but that was in the PAST (XVIIe siècle). The question asks about "le monde moderne" — and in modern times, they FLEE, they don't fight. This is a Timeline trap (from Project 1): a true fact from the WRONG time period.

Choice B: "n'a plus aucune raison valable de craindre son entourage le plus proche"

Text says: "ils n'ont plus rien à craindre des ESPAGNOLS"
                                               ^^^^^^^^^
           BUT:
           "Menacés par différents groupes de guérilleros...
           les narcotrafiquants, les pilleurs de tombes et 
           même les paysans sans terre"

❌ ELIMINATED — Partial Truth + Amplification trap (from Project 17). It IS true that they no longer fear the SPANISH. But Choice B says they have "aucune raison de craindre" (NO reason to fear) their "entourage" (surroundings). The text lists FOUR modern threats! The "mais" after the Spanish sentence immediately introduces new dangers. This choice captures only the FIRST half of a "no longer X, BUT now Y" structure.

This is the "MAIS...AUSSI" pattern from Project 16 in a new form:

"N'ont plus rien à craindre des Espagnols" (SIDE A — positive)
"MAIS leur vie n'est guère plus facile"    (SIDE B — negative)

Choice B captures only Side A and ignores Side B entirely.

Choice C: "continue à être l'objet des persécutions violentes des conquérants espagnols"

Text says: "ils n'ont PLUS RIEN à craindre des Espagnols"
                      ^^^^^^^^^

❌ ELIMINATED — Direct Contradiction trap. The text EXPLICITLY states the Spanish threat is OVER. "N'ont plus rien à craindre des Espagnols" = have nothing more to fear from the Spanish. Choice C says the EXACT OPPOSITE: Spanish persecution CONTINUES. The words "plus rien" are absolute — the Spanish chapter is closed.

🆕 NEW CONCEPT: HISTORICAL LAYER CONFUSION
Anthropological texts describe MULTIPLE time periods. The exam tests whether you can assign facts to the CORRECT period:

XVIIe SIÈCLE: Spanish conquistadors → affrontements
AUJOURD'HUI:  Guerrillas, narcos, etc. → fuir plus haut

Choice C takes a PAST threat and presents it as CURRENT. Choice A takes PAST combat and presents it as CURRENT behavior. Both fail the timeline test.

Text evidence — MULTIPLE sentences:

QUESTION 19: La vision du monde des Koguis est basée sur la croyance selon laquelle...

🆕 NEW QUESTION STRUCTURE: "SELON LAQUELLE" (according to which)
This question asks you to identify a SPECIFIC BELIEF — not what is objectively true, but what the KOGUIS believe. You must report their worldview accurately, without adding to it or distorting it.

This question targets Temporal Plane 2 — the timeless belief system.

Locate the belief passages:

BELIEF PASSAGE 1 (Paragraph 2):
"C'est la Terre mère qui leur a TRANSMIS LE CODE MORAL ET 
SPIRITUEL qui RÉGIT leur vie sur la Terre."

BELIEF PASSAGE 2 (Paragraph 2):
"des rites qui leur apprennent qu'ils ne sont QU'UN ÉLÉMENT 
PARMI D'AUTRES dans la nature"

BELIEF PASSAGE 3 (Paragraph 3):
"Les Koguis considèrent que les hommes NE SONT PAS 
PROPRIÉTAIRES de la Terre, mais que c'est LA TERRE QUI LES 
POSSÈDE et qui prend soin d'eux."

Synthesize the three belief statements:

BELIEF 1: The Earth TRANSMITTED the moral/spiritual code 
          → The Earth DICTATES the laws
          
BELIEF 2: Humans are ONE element AMONG OTHERS 
          → Humans are NOT privileged or unique

BELIEF 3: Humans DON'T OWN the Earth; the Earth OWNS humans
          → The relationship is REVERSED from Western thinking

Now eliminate:

Choice A: "les humains ont la Terre en leur complète et définitive possession"

Text says: "les hommes NE SONT PAS propriétaires de la Terre"
            ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^
           "c'est la Terre qui LES possède"

❌ ELIMINATED — Direct Inversion trap — and the most EXTREME inversion in all 25 projects. Choice A states the EXACT OPPOSITE of the Koguis' core belief. The text says humans DON'T own the Earth. Choice A says humans have COMPLETE possession. This is a 180-degree reversal.

🆕 NEW CONCEPT: THE "WORLDVIEW INVERSION" TRAP
In anthropological texts, the exam deliberately offers an answer that represents the OPPOSITE worldview — typically the Western/modern perspective. Choice A represents how MODERN civilization views the Earth (as a resource to possess). The Koguis believe the reverse. If you unconsciously default to the Western view, you'll pick the wrong answer.

This is the most philosophically sophisticated trap we've encountered. It tests not just reading skill but intellectual flexibility — can you set aside your cultural default and report what ANOTHER culture believes?

Choice C: "les enfants, plus proches de la nature, en comprennent mieux les mystères"

Text says: "Leurs enfants sont initiés très tôt aux mystères 
de leur religion, en pratiquant des rites qui leur apprennent 
qu'ils ne sont qu'un élément parmi d'autres"

❌ ELIMINATED — Subtle Distortion trap. The text DOES mention children being initiated into mysteries. But it does NOT say children understand nature BETTER than adults BECAUSE they are "closer to nature." The text says children are TAUGHT through rites — they LEARN, they don't have innate superior understanding. Moreover, the core BELIEF of the Koguis is not about children — it's about the relationship between ALL humans and the Earth.

This choice takes a DETAIL about education and inflates it into the CORE belief. As learned in Project 16 (Detail Hijack), a supporting detail is not the main thesis.

Choice D: "la nature attribue aux humains une place privilégiée et unique dans l'univers"

Text says: "ils ne sont QU'UN ÉLÉMENT PARMI D'AUTRES dans la nature"
                        ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

❌ ELIMINATED — Worldview Inversion trap (same type as Choice A, different angle). "Privilégiée et unique" means privileged and unique — suggesting humans have a SPECIAL status. The text says the EXACT OPPOSITE: humans are "qu'un élément parmi d'autres" — JUST one element AMONG OTHERS. The restrictive "ne...que" and "parmi d'autres" explicitly DENY any privileged status.

PATTERN ALERT: Both Choice A and Choice D represent the WESTERN worldview (humans are special, humans own things). The Koguis' worldview is the REVERSE. The exam offers TWO inversions as wrong answers — testing whether you've truly understood the alternative worldview.

QUESTION 20: Dans la civilisation des Koguis, le pouvoir des Mamus...

This question tests your ability to characterize the SCOPE and NATURE of the Mamus' role.

Isolate the Mamus passage:

"Au centre de cet univers spirituel se trouvent les Mamus, 
PRÊTRES ET PHILOSOPHES, qui VEILLENT À L'ORDRE SPIRITUEL de 
la communauté. A l'écoute des phénomènes naturels, ce sont 
eux qui PRÉSIDENT LES CÉRÉMONIES et les rites de leur village, 
destinés à évoquer les ancêtres ou à remercier le dieu du Bien."

Extract the Mamus' characteristics:

TITLE:     "prêtres et philosophes" (priests AND philosophers)
FUNCTION:  "veillent à l'ordre spirituel" (watch over spiritual order)
DOMAIN:    "cet univers SPIRITUEL" (spiritual universe)
ACTIVITIES: "président les cérémonies et les rites" (preside over 
            ceremonies and rites)
PURPOSE:   "évoquer les ancêtres" + "remercier le dieu du Bien"
           (invoke ancestors + thank the god of Good)

Key insight: The Mamus' power is SPIRITUAL and MORAL, not administrative or all-encompassing.

Now eliminate:

Choice B: "est cantonné aux arts divinatoires et à l'interprétation des phénomènes naturels"

Text says: "A l'écoute des phénomènes naturels"

❌ ELIMINATED — Reduction + Distortion trap. The text says the Mamus are "à l'écoute des phénomènes naturels" (attentive to natural phenomena) — but this is ONE aspect of their role, not the WHOLE thing. More critically, "arts divinatoires" (divination/fortune-telling) is NEVER mentioned. Being attentive to nature ≠ practicing divination.

Furthermore, "cantonné" means "limited to" or "restricted to" — suggesting this is ALL they do. But the text shows they do MUCH more: they maintain spiritual order, preside over ceremonies, invoke ancestors, and thank the divine. "Cantonné" contradicts the text's portrait of broad spiritual authority.

🆕 NEW TRAP TYPE — REDUCTION: The wrong answer takes ONE aspect of a role and presents it as the ENTIRE role, using a word like "cantonné" (limited to), "se réduit à" (reduces to), or "seulement" (only). When you see these LIMITING words, check whether the text actually LIMITS the role or describes it more broadly.

Choice C: "est purement administratif et se réduit à l'organisation des cérémonies du village"

Text says: "veillent à l'ORDRE SPIRITUEL"
                         ^^^^^^^^^
           "prêtres et PHILOSOPHES"
                        ^^^^^^^^^^^

❌ ELIMINATED — Double Reduction trap. Two problems:

Reduction 1: "purement administratif" (purely administrative) directly contradicts "ordre SPIRITUEL" (spiritual order) and "prêtres et philosophes" (priests and philosophers). Administrative ≠ spiritual. The Mamus' role is ethical/moral/spiritual, not bureaucratic.

Reduction 2: "se réduit à l'organisation des cérémonies" (reduces to organizing ceremonies). Yes, they preside over ceremonies — but they ALSO maintain spiritual order. "Se réduit" falsely shrinks their role. Same mechanism as Choice B's "cantonné."

⚠️ PATTERN ALERT: Both Choice B ("cantonné") and Choice C ("se réduit") use LIMITING language to shrink the Mamus' role. When TWO wrong answers both use the same strategy (reduction), the correct answer likely describes a BROADER role.

QUESTION 21: L'extinction de la tribu des Koguis aurait pour conséquences...

🆕 NEW QUESTION TYPE: "PROJECTIVE CONSEQUENCE"
This question asks you to project INTO THE FUTURE based on a CONDITIONAL statement in the text. The text says "SI les Koguis venaient à disparaître" (IF the Koguis were to disappear) — and you must identify the consequence the AUTHOR envisions.

This targets Temporal Plane 3 — the hypothetical future.

Isolate the relevant passage (Paragraph 4 — the FINAL paragraph):

"Si les Koguis VENAIENT À DISPARAÎTRE, nous PERDRIONS avec 
eux UN EXEMPLE DE SAGESSE particulièrement précieux pour 
notre époque."

"Après toutes les erreurs commises à l'encontre des éléments, 
nous nous voyons en effet contraints, à l'aube de ce nouveau 
siècle, de RECONSIDÉRER LE RAPPORT ENTRE L'ÉCONOMIE ET LA 
SAUVEGARDE DE LA NATURE."

Decode the author's argument step by step:

STEP 1: IF they disappear...
STEP 2: ...we LOSE an example of wisdom
STEP 3: This wisdom = harmony between humans and nature
        (from paragraphs 2-3: Earth possesses humans, 
         humans are one element among others)
STEP 4: We NEED this wisdom NOW because we've made errors 
        against nature
STEP 5: We must reconsider the relationship between 
        economy and nature

SYNTHESIS: Their disappearance = loss of a WAY OF LIFE 
based on HARMONY WITH NATURE — a model we desperately need

Now eliminate:

Choice A: "des problèmes écologiques considérables dans la région de la Sierra Nevada"

❌ ELIMINATED — Scope Reduction + Invention trap. The author's argument is about a UNIVERSAL loss ("pour notre époque" = for our era, meaning ALL of humanity). Choice A reduces this to a LOCAL ecological problem in one specific region. Furthermore, the text never says the Koguis' disappearance would cause ecological damage in the Sierra Nevada. The text says we'd lose their WISDOM — a philosophical/spiritual loss, not an environmental one.

🆕 NEW CONCEPT: UNIVERSAL VS LOCAL SCOPE
When the author writes "pour notre époque" and "nous nous voyons contraints," the "nous" (we) means ALL OF HUMANITY, not just the Sierra Nevada region. The correct answer must match this UNIVERSAL scope. Any answer that localizes the consequence to one region is too narrow.

Choice B: "la dégradation des conditions de vie des autres peuples indigènes en Colombie"

❌ ELIMINATED — Invention trap. The text never mentions other indigenous peoples in Colombia. The text's concern is about HUMANITY'S loss of wisdom, not about neighboring tribes' living conditions. This choice invents a chain of consequences the text doesn't describe.

Choice D: "des conséquences économiques importantes pour le reste de la population de la région"

❌ ELIMINATED — Worldview Inversion trap (same type as Q19). This choice frames the consequence in ECONOMIC terms — the very framework the text CRITICIZES. The author says we need to "reconsidérer le rapport entre l'ÉCONOMIE et la sauvegarde de la nature" — meaning the economic perspective has been OVERVALUED and needs to be BALANCED with nature. Framing the Koguis' disappearance as an "economic consequence" applies exactly the thinking the text argues AGAINST.

⚠️ DEEP INSIGHT: This is the most intellectually sophisticated trap in the entire exam. The text argues that we've prioritized economy over nature (our "errors"). The Koguis represent the OPPOSITE priority (nature over economy). Framing their loss in economic terms would mean applying the very mindset that the text identifies as the PROBLEM. It's like measuring a spiritual teacher's value by their salary — fundamentally missing the point.

Choice C: "la disparition irrémédiable d'un mode de vie basé sur l'harmonie avec la nature"

Text evidence:

"nous perdrions avec eux un EXEMPLE DE SAGESSE"
→ "disparition d'un mode de vie" 
   (losing an example = disappearance of a way of life)

"particulièrement précieux"
→ "irrémédiable" (precious + lost = irreplaceable/irremediable)

"le rapport entre l'économie et la sauvegarde de la nature"
+ "les hommes ne sont pas propriétaires de la Terre"
+ "un élément parmi d'autres dans la nature"
→ "basé sur l'harmonie avec la nature"
   (all their beliefs = harmony with nature)

✅ CORRECT. The paraphrase chain synthesizes the ENTIRE text:

  • "exemple de sagesse" + "perdrions" → "disparition irrémédiable d'un mode de vie" (irreversible loss of a way of life)
  • ALL of paragraphs 2-3 (Earth possesses humans, one element among others, spiritual code from nature) → "basé sur l'harmonie avec la nature" (based on harmony with nature)
  • The author's universal concern ("notre époque") is captured by the weight of "irrémédiable" (irremediable = gone forever, affecting everyone)

✅ Answer 21: C

For consequence questions, verify the SCOPE:

AUTHOR'S SCOPE: "pour notre époque" = ALL HUMANITY
                "nous perdrions" = WE (universal)
                "reconsidérer" = RETHINK (civilizational)

CORRECT SCOPE:  Universal, philosophical, civilizational
WRONG SCOPE:    Local (Sierra Nevada), regional (Colombia),
                economic (financial consequences)

If the author speaks in universal terms, the correct answer must be universal too.

Strategy 3: The "Temporal Plane" Assignment

Before answering, assign each question to its temporal plane:

Q18 → PRESENT: What is happening NOW?
      → Look in Paragraph 1 (current threats, fleeing)

Q19 → TIMELESS: What do they BELIEVE?
      → Look in Paragraphs 2-3 (spiritual worldview)

      → Look in Paragraph 4 (author's conclusion)

Each paragraph answers exactly ONE question. This is not accidental — well-structured texts often follow this pattern. Map the structure, and you'll know WHERE to look.

✅ STEP 5: WHAT SHOULD YOU DO

The 6 Rules for Anthropological Texts

1. Suspend your own worldview when answering "belief" questions

The exam doesn't ask what YOU believe. It asks what the TEXT says the Koguis believe. You are a reporter, not a philosopher. (New concept introduced in this project.)

2. Watch for INVERSION traps that represent the OPPOSITE worldview

When two cultures are contrasted, wrong answers often represent the OTHER culture's perspective. If the Koguis believe Earth owns humans, expect a wrong answer saying humans own Earth. (Worldview Inversion trap — new in this project.)

3. For "consequence" questions, match the SCOPE of the author's concern

If the author says "pour notre époque" (for our era), the consequence is UNIVERSAL. Don't choose a LOCAL answer. (Universal vs Local Scope — new concept.)

4. For "role" questions, calibrate the BREADTH — neither too narrow nor too broad

The Mamus are not JUST ceremony organizers (too narrow) and not managers of ALL daily life (too broad). They maintain spiritual/moral order. Use the Scope Spectrum technique introduced in Q20.

5. Assign each question to its TEMPORAL PLANE before searching

Present → Paragraph 1. Belief system → Paragraphs 2-3. Hypothetical future → Paragraph 4. This prevents you from searching in the wrong part of the text.

6. When the text CRITICIZES a value system, wrong answers will APPLY that system

The text criticizes economic thinking ("erreurs commises"). Wrong answers will frame the Koguis' loss in economic terms. Recognize this pattern: the text's critique becomes the trap's framework.

❌ STEP 6: WHAT SHOULD YOU NOT DO

❌ SIN 1: Defaulting to the Western worldview

If your first instinct for Q19 is "humans possess the Earth," you're applying YOUR worldview, not reading the text. The text says the OPPOSITE. As established in Project 11 (editorial stance), report the TEXT's position, not yours.

❌ SIN 2: Confusing PAST threats with PRESENT threats

Spanish conquistadors = PAST. Guerrillas and drug traffickers = PRESENT. The question says "dans les conditions du monde moderne" — you MUST answer about TODAY. Timeline precision, established in Project 1, is crucial here.

❌ SIN 3: Choosing an answer with LIMITING language when the text describes a BROAD role

"Cantonné à" (limited to), "se réduit à" (reduces to), "purement" (purely) — these words SHRINK a role. If the text describes a BROAD spiritual authority, answers with these words are wrong. This is the Reduction trap, new in this project.

❌ SIN 4: Measuring a spiritual civilization by economic standards

Choice D of Q21 is wrong precisely because it applies economic measurement to a spiritual reality. The text explicitly asks us to move BEYOND economic thinking. Don't fall into the trap of evaluating everything in financial terms. (Connects to Project 17's Mechanism vs Trend — different analytical frameworks applied to the wrong context.)

❌ SIN 5: Confusing a DETAIL about children with the CORE belief

Children are initiated into rites — that's a DETAIL about education. The CORE belief is that the Earth governs human life and humans are not special. Don't elevate a supporting detail to the level of the central thesis. (Connects to Project 16's Detail Hijack.)
