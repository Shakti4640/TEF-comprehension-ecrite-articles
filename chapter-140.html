This text is about a real BUSINESS — a Quebec company that employs retired grandmothers to knit children's clothes. It's not just describing a product; it's ARGUING that this business model is clever, efficient, and popular.

Each gap fills a different ARGUMENT FUNCTION:

GAP 1: RESULT of the business concept (how is it going?)
GAP 2: HOW the business operates (production process)
GAP 3: ADVANTAGE of the local production model
GAP 4: WORKING CONDITIONS (how the grandmothers are treated)
GAP 5: POPULARITY/SUCCESS (evidence of the concept's appeal)

The Core Challenge: ARGUMENTATIVE COHERENCE

In Chapter 9, the text flowed in ONE direction: problem → solution → features → benefits. Here, the text builds an ARGUMENT through multiple steps:

THESIS:      Mamy Factory employs retirees to knit children's clothes
EVIDENCE 1:  The concept is successful (Gap 1)
CONTEXT:     Two trends exploited (retirees + responsible consumption)
PROCESS:     How production works (Gap 2 + Gap 3)
CONDITIONS:  How workers are treated (Gap 4)
PROOF:       Evidence of popularity (Gap 5)

Each gap must advance the ARGUMENT, not just fill space. A sentence that's topically related but doesn't advance the argument will break the text's logical flow.

Building on Chapter 9's Method

Your B.G.A. + P.C.T. method (Chapter 9) still applies, but now you add a new layer:

Chapter 9 method:
  B.G.A. (Before-Gap-After) + P.C.T. (Pronoun-Connector-Tone)

Chapter 10 ADDS:
  A.L.C. (Argument-Logic-Consistency)
  → Does the gap advance the text's ARGUMENT?
  → Does the gap maintain logical CONSISTENCY with stated facts?
  → Does the gap's claim fit the EVIDENCE provided nearby?

🧩 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

GAP 1: After "employs retirees to make children's clothes" — First sentence's conclusion

Step 1: Apply B.G.A.

BEFORE:

"Depuis un an, l'entreprise québécoise, Mamy Factory fait appel à des retraitées pour confectionner des vêtements pour enfants sur leur temps libre."

Content: For one year, the Quebec company Mamy Factory has been employing retired women to make children's clothes in their free time.

This is the OPENING — it introduces the concept. What naturally follows an introduction?

AFTER:

"La fondatrice a réussi à exploiter deux tendances actuelles ; un nombre de retraités qui ne cesse de croître et la consommation responsable qui séduit de plus en plus de monde."

Content: The founder successfully exploited two current trends: growing number of retirees + responsible consumption.

The AFTER explains the STRATEGY. It moves to HOW the founder conceived the idea.

Step 2: Identify the gap's function.

The text opens with the concept (employing retirees). Before moving to the strategic explanation (how the founder conceived it), there should be a sentence about HOW THE CONCEPT IS GOING — the result, the reception, the status.

[Concept introduced] → [GAP: How is it going?] → [Strategy explanation]

The gap should tell us: Is this working? Is it successful?

Also, this is the FIRST paragraph — the opening hook. Newspaper/magazine articles typically follow:

Hook (what the company does) → Success statement → Explanation of why it works

Step 3: Check each option.

Option A — "L'âge des employées n'est pas une garantie de qualité."

"The age of the employees is not a guarantee of quality."

This is NEGATIVE — it CRITICIZES the concept. But the rest of the text is entirely POSITIVE about Mamy Factory. The AFTER says the founder "a réussi à exploiter" (successfully exploited) — success language. A criticism here breaks the positive flow.

Also, this contradicts the text's overall thesis. The article is PROMOTING the concept, not questioning it.

❌ ELIMINATED — Negative tone contradicts the text's positive framing; "réussi" in AFTER implies success, not doubt

Option B — "En employant ces cadres, l'usine a dopé l'emploi dans la région."

"By employing these executives, the factory has boosted employment in the region."

Two factual problems:

  1. "Cadres" (executives/managers): The text says the company employs "retraitées" (retired women) who are "tricoteuses" (knitters). These are NOT executives. They're grandmothers knitting at home. "Cadres" completely mischaracterizes who they are.

  2. "L'usine" (the factory): The text describes a decentralized model — wool is sent to knitters' HOMES ("au domicile des tricoteuses, aux quatre coins de la Province"). This is NOT a factory model. There's no central factory.

❌ ELIMINATED — "Cadres" contradicts "retraitées/tricoteuses"; "usine" contradicts the home-based model

This is a CHARACTERIZATION CONTRADICTION TRAP — the gap option describes the workers and workplace in terms that directly contradict the text's descriptions. This is a more advanced version of Chapter 9's Contradiction Trap — here, the contradiction is about the NATURE of the business, not just a specific fact.

Option C — "Le savoir-faire des aînées est plébiscité et les commandes se multiplient."

"The know-how of the elderly is widely acclaimed and orders are multiplying."

Let's test the flow:

BEFORE: "Mamy Factory employs retirees to make children's clothes."
GAP C:  "The know-how of the elderly is acclaimed and orders are multiplying."
AFTER:  "The founder successfully exploited two trends..."

  • "Le savoir-faire des aînées" (the know-how of the elderly) — directly relates to "retraitées" (retirees). "Aînées" (elderly women) ≈ "retraitées" (retired women). Their SKILL is valued. ✓

  • "Plébiscité" (widely acclaimed) — indicates SUCCESS. This matches the positive tone and the "réussi" (succeeded) in the AFTER. ✓

  • "Les commandes se multiplient" (orders are multiplying) — CONCRETE evidence of success. Business is growing. ✓

The flow:

[Company employs retirees to knit]
→ [Their expertise is acclaimed, orders are growing]  ← SUCCESS STATEMENT
→ [The founder successfully exploited two trends...]  ← STRATEGIC EXPLANATION

This follows the classic journalistic pattern: Concept → Success → Explanation.

✅ CORRECT — Provides the success statement between concept introduction and strategy explanation; "plébiscité" matches the positive tone; "commandes se multiplient" gives concrete evidence

Option D — "La dernière collection propose des tenues adaptées aux goûts des séniors."

"The latest collection offers outfits adapted to seniors' tastes."

The company makes children's clothes ("vêtements pour enfants"), NOT clothes for seniors. This directly contradicts the first sentence.

❌ ELIMINATED — "Séniors" contradicts "pour enfants"; the target market is children, not elderly

This is a TARGET CONFUSION TRAP — confusing WHO MAKES the clothes (elderly women) with WHO WEARS them (children). The grandmothers are the PRODUCERS, not the CONSUMERS.

✅ ANSWER: C

🔬 THE OPENING PARAGRAPH PATTERN:

JOURNALISTIC OPENING PATTERN:
  Sentence 1: WHAT the company does (concept)
  Sentence 2: HOW it's going (success/reception) ← GAP 1
  Sentence 3: WHY it works (strategy/explanation)

This pattern appears in many CE texts about businesses/projects.
The gap between "what" and "why" is almost always "how it's going."

IDENTIFYING THIS PATTERN helps you predict what the gap needs
BEFORE you even read the options.

GAP 2: After "two trends exploited" — Before "Collections designed in-house"

Step 1: Apply B.G.A.

BEFORE:

"La fondatrice a réussi à exploiter deux tendances actuelles ; un nombre de retraités qui ne cesse de croître et la consommation responsable qui séduit de plus en plus de monde."

The founder exploited: (1) growing retiree population + (2) responsible consumption trend.

AFTER:

"Les collections sont dessinées en interne. Lorsque le produit est validé, la laine est envoyée au domicile des tricoteuses, aux quatre coins de la Province."

The collections are designed in-house. When the product is validated, wool is sent to knitters' homes across the Province.

Step 2: Identify the gap's function.

The BEFORE describes the STRATEGIC INSIGHT (two trends). The AFTER describes the PRODUCTION PROCESS (design → validation → send wool).

The gap must BRIDGE from strategy to process. It should introduce HOW the founder translated her insight into a concrete business model.

[Strategic insight: retirees + responsible consumption]
→ GAP: [How she built the business model]
→ [Concrete production process: design in-house, send wool to knitters]

The gap should introduce the CONCEPT of grandmothers knitting with natural materials — the concrete realization of the two trends.

Step 3: Check each option.

Option A — "Elle imagine alors les mamies tricoteuses de matières naturelles."

"She then imagines the grandma knitters of natural materials."

Let's test the flow:

BEFORE: "She exploited two trends: retirees + responsible consumption"
GAP A:  "She then imagines the grandma knitters of natural materials."
AFTER:  "Collections are designed in-house. Wool is sent to knitters' homes."
  • "Elle" = the founder (la fondatrice, feminine singular). ✓ Pronoun consistency.
  • "Imagine alors" (then imagines) — "alors" (then) creates a TEMPORAL sequence: first she identified the trends, THEN she conceived the model. Logical flow. ✓
  • "Les mamies tricoteuses" (grandma knitters) — this IS the business model: retired grandmothers who knit. It concretizes the "retraités" trend. ✓
  • "De matières naturelles" (of/with natural materials) — this connects to "consommation responsable" (responsible consumption). Natural materials = responsible sourcing. It also anticipates "la laine" (wool) in the AFTER. ✓

The bridge works perfectly:

[Trend 1: retirees] → "mamies tricoteuses" (retirees who knit) ✓
[Trend 2: responsible consumption] → "matières naturelles" (natural materials) ✓
→ [Production: design → send WOOL to knitters] ← "laine" connects to "matières naturelles" ✓

✅ CORRECT — Bridges both trends to the concrete business model; introduces "mamies tricoteuses" and "matières naturelles" which anticipate the production process

Option B — "Afin de réduire les coûts, la production est délocalisée à l'étranger."

"In order to reduce costs, production is outsourced abroad."

The ENTIRE TEXT contradicts this. The text says:

  • Wool is sent to knitters "aux quatre coins de la Province" (across the Province = LOCAL)
  • "Faire appel à un fournisseur à l'autre bout du monde" is presented as the NEGATIVE alternative

The business model is LOCAL, not foreign. "Délocalisée à l'étranger" (outsourced abroad) is the OPPOSITE of what the company does.

❌ ELIMINATED — Directly contradicts the text's LOCAL production model

This is a 180-DEGREE CONTRADICTION TRAP — the answer states the exact OPPOSITE of the text's core argument. The text celebrates LOCAL production; the option describes FOREIGN outsourcing. This should be caught immediately through your Contradiction Cross-Check.

Option C — "Cette créatrice a ainsi permis à ces chômeuses de reprendre une activité."

"This creator has thus allowed these unemployed women to resume activity."

Problem: "Chômeuses" (unemployed women) ≠ "retraitées" (retired women). Retired people are NOT unemployed — they have CHOSEN to stop working (or reached retirement age). Unemployment implies wanting to work but being unable to find a job.

RETRAITÉE:  Has worked, now retired by choice/age → receives pension
CHÔMEUSE:   Wants to work but can't find employment → receives unemployment benefits

This is an important SOCIAL distinction in French. The text consistently uses "retraitées" and "grands-mères" — never "chômeuses."

❌ ELIMINATED — "Chômeuses" mischaracterizes "retraitées"; retired ≠ unemployed

This is a STATUS MISCHARACTERIZATION TRAP — describing people with the wrong social category. Similar to Chapter 5's Sympathetic Inference Trap (assuming "children in difficulty" = orphans), here the answer assumes "retired women working" = "unemployed women finding jobs."

Option D — "Pour garantir plus de souplesse, les stylistes sont extérieurs à l'entreprise."

"To guarantee more flexibility, the designers are external to the company."

The AFTER says: "Les collections sont dessinées en interne." (Collections are designed IN-HOUSE.)

"En interne" (in-house) DIRECTLY contradicts "extérieurs à l'entreprise" (external to the company). The designers work INSIDE the company, not outside.

❌ ELIMINATED — "Extérieurs" directly contradicts "en interne" in the AFTER sentence

This is the clearest contradiction in this exercise — the gap option says one thing, and the VERY NEXT SENTENCE says the opposite. This is why reading the AFTER sentence first is so important.

✅ ANSWER: A

The correct gap must CONNECT BOTH trends to the business model:
  Trend 1 → "mamies tricoteuses" (retirees who knit)
  Trend 2 → "matières naturelles" (natural materials = responsible)

Then the AFTER describes the PROCESS:
  → "laine" (wool) = a natural material ← connects to "matières naturelles"

GAP 3: After "wool sent to knitters across the Province" — Before "Using a foreign supplier would require months"

Step 1: Apply B.G.A.

BEFORE:

"Lorsque le produit est validé, la laine est envoyée au domicile des tricoteuses, aux quatre coins de la Province."

When the product is validated, wool is sent to knitters' homes across the Province. = LOCAL production model.

AFTER:

"Faire appel à un fournisseur à l'autre bout du monde exigerait au contraire des mois de production."

Using a supplier at the other end of the world would on the contrary require months of production.

Step 2: Identify the gap's function using the CONNECTOR.

The CRITICAL connector is "au contraire" (on the contrary). This signals a CONTRAST:

[GAP sentence] ← describes an ADVANTAGE of local production
"Au contraire" → foreign production would take MONTHS

"Au contraire" means: "By contrast, the alternative is worse." So the gap must state something POSITIVE about the local model — specifically something about TIME/SPEED — that foreign production would contrast with negatively.

The AFTER says foreign suppliers would need "mois de production" (months of production). The CONTRAST implies the local model is FASTER. So the gap should state that the local model REDUCES production time.

[LOCAL model: advantage about SPEED] → "Au contraire" → [FOREIGN model: months needed]
SPEED advantage ← → SPEED disadvantage

Step 3: Check each option.

Option A — "Le bilan carbone de l'opération est ainsi positif."

"The carbon footprint of the operation is thus positive."

This is about ENVIRONMENTAL impact, not SPEED. The AFTER contrasts with "mois de production" (months of production) — a TIME issue. "Au contraire" needs a contrast about TIME, not environment.

GAP A:  "Carbon footprint is positive" (ENVIRONMENT)
AFTER:  "Foreign supplier would need MONTHS" (TIME)
→ "Au contraire" connects ENVIRONMENT to TIME? 
→ That's not a proper contrast — different categories

❌ ELIMINATED — "Bilan carbone" (environment) doesn't contrast logically with "mois de production" (time)

This is a CATEGORY MISMATCH IN CONTRAST TRAP — the gap introduces one category (environment) but the contrast connector "au contraire" requires the SAME category (time) to create a meaningful opposition.

VALID CONTRAST:   "Production is fast" ← au contraire → "Foreign would take months"
                   (TIME)                                 (TIME)

INVALID CONTRAST: "Carbon footprint is good" ← au contraire → "Foreign would take months"
                   (ENVIRONMENT)                              (TIME)

Option B — "Le cycle de fabrication est ainsi largement réduit."

"The manufacturing cycle is thus greatly reduced."

Let's test the flow:

BEFORE: "Wool is sent to knitters' homes across the Province."
GAP B:  "The manufacturing cycle is thus greatly reduced."
AFTER:  "Using a foreign supplier would on the contrary require months."
  • "Cycle de fabrication" (manufacturing cycle) = TIME-related concept. ✓
  • "Largement réduit" (greatly reduced) = the local model is FAST. ✓
  • "Au contraire" → foreign model takes MONTHS = the local model is faster BY CONTRAST. ✓
  • "Ainsi" (thus) → because wool is sent locally (BEFORE), the production cycle is reduced (CONSEQUENCE). ✓

The contrast is perfect:

LOCAL:    "Manufacturing cycle greatly REDUCED" (fast)
           ↕ "au contraire" ↕
FOREIGN:  "Would require MONTHS of production" (slow)

Same category (TIME), opposite values (fast vs. slow). The "au contraire" connector works perfectly.

✅ CORRECT — "Cycle de fabrication réduit" contrasts perfectly with "mois de production"; "ainsi" logically follows from local production; same category (time)

Option C — "Ce choix permet de faire des économies substantielles."

"This choice allows for substantial savings."

This is about MONEY, not TIME. "Au contraire" + "mois de production" creates a TIME contrast, not a COST contrast. If the gap were about cost, the AFTER would need to say "foreign suppliers would cost more" — not "would require months."

❌ ELIMINATED — "Économies" (cost) doesn't contrast with "mois de production" (time)

Same trap as Option A: wrong category for the contrast.

Option D — "Cette gestion locale est la marque d'un commerce engagé."

"This local management is the hallmark of an engaged/ethical business."

This is about ETHICS/VALUES, not TIME. Same problem as A and C — wrong category for the "au contraire" + "mois" contrast.

Also, "commerce engagé" (ethical business) is a VALUE JUDGMENT that doesn't lead to a time comparison.

❌ ELIMINATED — "Commerce engagé" (ethics) doesn't contrast with "mois de production" (time)

✅ ANSWER: B

🔬 THE CONTRAST CATEGORY MATCHING LESSON:

When a gap is followed by "au contraire" (on the contrary):

RULE: The gap and the AFTER must discuss the SAME CATEGORY 
      but with OPPOSITE values.

VALID:
  GAP:   "Production cycle is REDUCED" (time: fast)
  AFTER: "Foreign would need MONTHS"   (time: slow)
  → Same category (time), opposite values (fast vs. slow) ✓

INVALID:
  GAP:   "Carbon footprint is positive" (environment: good)
  AFTER: "Foreign would need MONTHS"    (time: slow)
  → Different categories (environment vs. time) ✗

CHECKLIST for "au contraire" gaps:
  1. Identify the CATEGORY in the AFTER (time? cost? quality? environment?)
  2. The gap must be in the SAME category
  3. The gap must have the OPPOSITE value

GAP 4: Before "Each works at her own pace, brand wants to minimize stocks"

Step 1: Apply B.G.A.

BEFORE (end of previous paragraph):

"Faire appel à un fournisseur à l'autre bout du monde exigerait au contraire des mois de production."

Foreign production = slow and impractical.

AFTER:

"Chacune œuvre à son rythme, la marque souhaitant limiter les stocks au minimum."

"Each one works at her own pace, the brand wanting to minimize stocks."

Step 2: Analyze the AFTER carefully.

Two key elements:

  1. "Chacune œuvre à son rythme" — each grandmother works at her OWN PACE. This implies FLEXIBILITY, no pressure, individual rhythm. The tone is GENTLE, RESPECTFUL of the workers.

  2. "La marque souhaitant limiter les stocks au minimum" — the brand wants minimal inventory. This is a BUSINESS RATIONALE: they don't overproduce, they produce on demand.

These two elements together paint a picture: the grandmothers are not pressured; the brand is flexible.

What should the gap say? It should INTRODUCE this idea — that despite being a business, Mamy Factory doesn't put pressure on its workers. There should be a sentence that TRANSITIONS from the production efficiency discussion (Paragraph 2) to the working conditions discussion (Paragraph 3).

Step 3: Consider the logical transition.

PARAGRAPH 2 ended with: Production efficiency (fast cycle, local model)
PARAGRAPH 3 begins with: Working conditions (own pace, no overproduction)

The gap must BRIDGE from "efficiency" to "human treatment."
The implicit question: "But does this efficiency come at a cost to the workers?"
The answer: "No — they work at their own pace."

This suggests the gap should ACKNOWLEDGE the potential concern (pressure on workers) and then REASSURE (no pressure). OR it should simply state the policy: the grandmothers aren't pressured.

Step 4: Check each option.

Option A — "Pour autant, pas question de mettre la pression aux mamies."

"However, no question of putting pressure on the grandmothers."

Let's test the flow:

BEFORE: [Production efficiency discussion, fast cycle]
GAP A:  "However, no question of putting pressure on the grandmothers."
AFTER:  "Each works at her own pace, brand wants to minimize stocks."

  • "Pour autant" (however/nonetheless) — a CONCESSIVE connector. It means: "Despite the efficiency discussed above, we must note that..." This bridges PERFECTLY from efficiency to human treatment. ✓

  • "Pas question de mettre la pression" (no question of pressuring) — directly introduces the NO-PRESSURE policy. ✓

  • The AFTER then CONFIRMS this: "chacune œuvre à son rythme" (each at her own pace) = EVIDENCE of no pressure. ✓

The logical chain:

[Production is efficient and fast]
→ "Pour autant" (however/nonetheless)
→ [No pressure on the grandmothers]
→ [Each works at her own pace] = PROOF of no pressure

"Pour autant" creates a CONCESSION: "We've been talking about efficiency, but let's be clear — efficiency doesn't mean exploitation."

✅ CORRECT — "Pour autant" bridges from efficiency to working conditions; "pas question de pression" directly introduces the no-pressure policy confirmed by AFTER

"This system is nevertheless incompatible with tight deadlines."

❌ ELIMINATED — Contradicts the speed advantage just established; negative framing clashes with the text's positive tone

"A bonus is given to grandmothers who knit more than requested."

This introduces a PRODUCTIVITY INCENTIVE — a reward for producing MORE. But the AFTER says "chacune œuvre à son rythme" (each at her own pace) and the brand wants to "limiter les stocks au minimum" (minimize inventory).

If the brand wants MINIMUM stocks, why would it incentivize grandmothers to produce MORE than requested? A bonus for overproduction contradicts the minimum-stock policy.

❌ ELIMINATED — Productivity bonus contradicts "limiter les stocks au minimum" in AFTER

This is a INTERNAL LOGIC CONTRADICTION — the gap option creates a logical inconsistency with the AFTER sentence. If you incentivize overproduction, you can't also claim to minimize stocks. The two policies are incompatible.

GAP D:  "Grandmothers are OVERWHELMED" (crushed by work)
AFTER:  "Each works at HER OWN PACE"  (relaxed, flexible)

🔬 THE CONCESSIVE BRIDGE LESSON:

CONCESSIVE CONNECTORS signal a shift from one aspect to another:
  "Pour autant" = however/nonetheless
  "Néanmoins" = nevertheless  
  "Cependant" = however
  "Toutefois" = yet

FUNCTION: "We've discussed X, but let's also consider Y"

In this gap:
  X = Production efficiency (previous paragraph)
  Y = Working conditions (this paragraph)
  
  "Pour autant, pas question de mettre la pression"
  = "Despite the efficiency, no pressure on workers"
  
This BRIDGES from one theme to another while maintaining the 
POSITIVE tone of the text.

TRAP: Concessive connectors with NEGATIVE content that contradicts 
      either the BEFORE or the AFTER.
      Option B: "néanmoins incompatible" → negates speed advantage ❌

GAP 5: Before "En effet, 3500+ grandmothers have contacted Mamy Factory"

Step 1: Apply B.G.A.

BEFORE (end of previous paragraph):

"Chacune œuvre à son rythme, la marque souhaitant limiter les stocks au minimum."

Each works at her own pace, brand minimizes stocks.

AFTER:

"En effet, plus de 3500 grands-mères ont contacté Mamy Factory pour devenir les petites mains de la marque."

"Indeed, more than 3500 grandmothers have contacted Mamy Factory to become the brand's helping hands."

Step 2: Analyze the CRITICAL connector.

"En effet" (indeed) — this is a CONFIRMATION/EVIDENCE connector. It means: "What I just said is confirmed by the following evidence."

[GAP sentence: makes a CLAIM] → "En effet" → [EVIDENCE: 3500 grandmothers contacted]

So the gap must make a CLAIM that "3500 grandmothers contacted us" would serve as EVIDENCE for.

What claim would 3500 grandmothers contacting the company PROVE?

  • That the concept is POPULAR? ✓ (many people want to participate)
  • That word of mouth works? ✓ (people are hearing about it and responding)
  • That the concept is inspiring? ✓ (people want to be part of it)

The evidence (3500 contacts) proves POPULARITY and ORGANIC SPREAD.

Step 3: Check each option.

Option A — "Le concept fait des émules et le bouche à oreille fonctionne à merveille."

"The concept inspires imitators and word of mouth works wonderfully."

Let's test the flow:

BEFORE: "Each works at her own pace, brand minimizes stocks."
GAP A:  "The concept inspires imitators and word of mouth works wonderfully."
AFTER:  "Indeed, 3500+ grandmothers have contacted Mamy Factory."

  • "Le concept fait des émules" (the concept inspires imitators/followers) — makes a CLAIM about the concept's popularity and influence. ✓

  • "Le bouche à oreille fonctionne à merveille" (word of mouth works wonderfully) — makes a CLAIM about organic spread. ✓

  • "En effet" → "Indeed, 3500+ grandmothers contacted us" — this CONFIRMS both claims:

    • 3500 contacts = the concept IS popular (fait des émules) ✓
    • 3500 people heard about it and reached out = word of mouth IS working ✓

The "en effet" connection is PERFECT: the claim (popularity + word of mouth) is directly confirmed by the evidence (3500 contacts).

✅ CORRECT — Both claims are confirmed by the "en effet" evidence; logical flow is seamless

Option B — "Galvanisées avec ce succès, les mamies revendiquent une revalorisation."

"Galvanized by this success, the grandmothers demand a pay raise."

This introduces LABOR CONFLICT — grandmothers demanding more money. The text has been entirely HARMONIOUS about the relationship between the brand and the workers. Introducing demands/conflict at the very end breaks the positive tone.

Also, "en effet, 3500 grandmothers contacted us" doesn't CONFIRM a pay raise demand. It confirms POPULARITY, not labor disputes.

GAP B:  "Grandmothers demand a raise" (CLAIM)
AFTER:  "Indeed, 3500 contacted us" (EVIDENCE)
→ 3500 contacts don't prove workers want more money
→ They prove the CONCEPT is popular
→ "En effet" connection BREAKS

❌ ELIMINATED — Labor conflict breaks positive tone; "en effet" evidence doesn't confirm pay raise demands

Option C — "D'anciennes couturières limogées ont désormais une perspective d'avenir."

"Former laid-off seamstresses now have a future perspective."

The text describes "retraitées" (retired women), not "couturières limogées" (laid-off seamstresses). Retired ≠ laid off. Also, this introduces a new group of people (seamstresses) that the text never mentions.

And "en effet, 3500 grandmothers contacted us" doesn't confirm that laid-off seamstresses have a future. It confirms that grandmothers WANT TO PARTICIPATE.

❌ ELIMINATED — "Couturières limogées" ≠ "retraitées"; orphan topic; "en effet" connection doesn't work

Option D — "Les débuts restent modestes mais devraient bientôt toucher le grand public."

"The beginnings remain modest but should soon reach the general public."

"Les débuts restent modestes" (beginnings are still modest) — this claims the company is SMALL and not yet widely known. But "en effet, 3500+ grandmothers contacted us" suggests the OPPOSITE — 3500 is a LOT of contacts. That's not "modest."

GAP D:  "Beginnings are MODEST" (small, limited)
AFTER:  "Indeed, 3500+ contacted us" (large number = NOT modest)
→ "En effet" would need to CONFIRM modesty
→ But 3500 contacts CONTRADICTS modesty
→ "En effet" connection BREAKS

❌ ELIMINATED — "Modestes" (modest) contradicted by "3500+" (large number); "en effet" would confirm the claim, but the evidence refutes it

This is a CLAIM-EVIDENCE MISMATCH TRAP — the gap makes a claim that the "en effet" evidence actually DISPROVES rather than confirms. If you claim something is modest, your evidence should show smallness, not 3500 contacts.

✅ ANSWER: A

🔬 THE "EN EFFET" CONFIRMATION PATTERN:

"EN EFFET" STRUCTURE:
  [CLAIM] → "En effet," → [EVIDENCE that confirms the claim]

The gap must contain a CLAIM.
The AFTER must CONFIRM that claim.

TEST: "Does the evidence in the AFTER actually PROVE the gap's claim?"

GAP A: "Concept is popular + word of mouth works" 
  → En effet: 3500 contacted us = PROVES popularity ✓

GAP B: "Workers demand a raise"
  → En effet: 3500 contacted us = doesn't prove labor demands ✗

GAP D: "Beginnings are modest"
  → En effet: 3500 contacted us = DISPROVES modesty ✗

RULE: When "en effet" follows a gap, the gap's claim must be 
      CONFIRMABLE by the evidence that "en effet" introduces.
      If the evidence CONTRADICTS the claim, the option is wrong.

New Strategy: The Argument Function Map

For business/argumentative texte à trous, identify each gap's ARGUMENT FUNCTION before solving:

GAP FUNCTIONS IN ARGUMENTATIVE TEXTS:

FUNCTION 1: SUCCESS STATEMENT
  → Follows concept introduction
  → States how well the concept is received

  → Follows process description

FUNCTION 4: CONDITION/TREATMENT STATEMENT
  → Bridges efficiency to human concerns
  → Uses concessive connector ("pour autant," "cependant")

FUNCTION 5: POPULARITY CLAIM
  → Precedes evidence (introduced by "en effet")
  → Makes a claim the evidence will confirm
  → Example: Gap 5 ("fait des émules + bouche à oreille")

FACTS ESTABLISHED IN THE TEXT:

CHECK EVERY OPTION against this list:
  "Tenues pour séniors" → contradicts "pour enfants" ❌

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL 1: The Claim-Evidence Mismatch Trap (NEW — Core concept of Chapter 10)

MECHANISM:  The gap makes a claim that the "en effet" evidence 
            actually DISPROVES or fails to support
EXAMPLE:    Gap: "Beginnings are modest" → En effet: 3500 contacts
            3500 contacts = NOT modest → claim disproved
DEFENSE:    Always test: "Does the 'en effet' evidence CONFIRM 
            or CONTRADICT the gap's claim?"

PITFALL 2: The Category Mismatch in Contrast Trap (NEW)

MECHANISM:  A contrast connector ("au contraire") connects two 
            statements from DIFFERENT categories
EXAMPLE:    Gap: "Carbon footprint positive" (environment)
            After: "Foreign would need months" (time)
            → Environment vs. Time = not a valid contrast
DEFENSE:    Identify the CATEGORY in the AFTER, then ensure the 
            gap is in the SAME category with the OPPOSITE value

PITFALL 3: The 180-Degree Contradiction Trap (NEW)

MECHANISM:  The gap states the EXACT OPPOSITE of the text's core argument
EXAMPLE:    Text argues for LOCAL production
            Gap: "Production is outsourced ABROAD"
            → Directly reverses the entire thesis
DEFENSE:    Build the contradiction network check — compare each 
            option against ALL stated facts

PITFALL 4: The Status Mischaracterization Trap (NEW)

MECHANISM:  People are described with the wrong social category
EXAMPLE:    "Retraitées" (retired) → called "chômeuses" (unemployed)
            "Retraitées" (retired) → called "cadres" (executives)
DEFENSE:    Verify that the gap option uses the SAME social category 
            as the text for the same people

PITFALL 5: The Target Confusion Trap (NEW — builds on Ch.5 Group Attribution)

MECHANISM:  Confusing who PRODUCES with who CONSUMES
EXAMPLE:    Grandmothers PRODUCE children's clothes
            Trap: "Clothes for seniors" (assumes producers = consumers)
DEFENSE:    Track: WHO makes? WHO buys? WHO uses? 
            These are often different people.

PITFALL 6: The Internal Logic Contradiction (NEW)

MECHANISM:  The gap creates a logical inconsistency with the AFTER sentence
EXAMPLE:    Gap: "Bonus for overproduction" + After: "Minimize stocks"
            → Incentivizing MORE production while wanting LESS stock
            → Logically incompatible
DEFENSE:    Read the gap + AFTER together as a unit. 
            Do they make sense together? Or do they contradict?
