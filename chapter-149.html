📖 CHAPTER 19: UN TAHITIEN NOMMÉ GAUGUIN — ART CRITICISM & EXHIBITION REVIEWS

Phase 4 | Hard | Advanced Patterns

📰 COMPLETE ARTICLE

Un tahitien nommé Gauguin

L'exposition « Gauguin Tahiti » au Grand Palais a quelque chose de frappant. Rares sont ceux qui ne connaissent pas l'œuvre du peintre mais voir rassemblées les pièces représentatives de sa période tahitienne est tout de même niveau.

Le musée du Luxembourg a déjà présenté « La période bretonne de Gauguin ». C'est à présent une facette totalement différente du maître qui est valorisée. Mais surtout une autre inspiration avec d'autres rencontres, d'autres paysages. Omniprésence des femmes dévêtues, couleurs vives, enivrent le regard. Gauguin bouscule les formes artistiques en témoignant dans une recherche énergique et presque sauvage de la culture polynésienne.

Cette rétrospective serait incomplète sans la présentation d'une facette artistique moins connue du peintre : la sculpture. Aux côtés de pièces traditionnelles des îles Marquises, les œuvres de Gauguin s'imprègnent totalement de cette influence. Il est alors aisé de voir comment l'artiste s'est progressivement emparé de cette culture jusqu'à en devenir l'un des représentants les plus actifs.

Une pièce maîtresse de cette exposition « D'où venons-nous ? Que sommes-nous ? Où allons-nous ? » une sorte de testament pictural revient pour la première fois en France depuis 50 ans. Gauguin a entrepris la réalisation de ce tableau avant de mourir. Durant un mois, il a travaillé jour et nuit dans une fièvre sur cette œuvre exemplaire de celui qui revendique le droit de tout oser.

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

Why This Text Demands New Skills

In Chapter 18 (Potins), you navigated an argumentative texte à trous with statistical data and logical chains. That was about LOGIC — connecting pieces through cause-effect reasoning and mathematical coherence.

This text is fundamentally different. It's an art criticism / exhibition review — a genre where:

  1. Vocabulary is aesthetic, not logical: words like "enivrent le regard," "testament pictural," "revendique le droit de tout oser" carry emotional and artistic weight
  2. The text EVALUATES, not just describes: the author doesn't merely list what's in the exhibition — they judge its significance, its novelty, its relationship to Gauguin's broader career
  3. Multiple SECTIONS of the exhibition correspond to multiple PARAGRAPHS of the text — each paragraph covers a different room/theme
  4. "Known vs Unknown" is the central axis: the text constantly distinguishes what the public already knows about Gauguin from what this exhibition newly reveals

This is your first encounter with a text where cultural knowledge is referenced but NOT required. You don't need to know anything about Gauguin, Tahiti, or Polynesian art. Everything you need is IN the text. But the text ASSUMES a culturally literate reader and uses sophisticated vocabulary that can intimidate.

The Structural Map: Exhibition as Architecture

This text mirrors the physical layout of the exhibition — each paragraph is a "room":

PARAGRAPH 1: THE ENTRANCE — Overall impression

├── BUT seeing the Tahitian period assembled is remarkable (new)
└── FUNCTION: Sets expectations — familiar artist, unfamiliar angle

PARAGRAPH 2: ROOM 1 — THE PAINTINGS (Tahitian period)
├── CONTRAST: Luxembourg showed Breton period / This shows Tahitian
├── "Facette totalement différente" = completely different facet
├── CONTENT: Nude women, vivid colors, intoxicating visual experience

🧩 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

QUESTION 17: L'exposition « Gauguin - Tahiti » au Grand Palais...

This is a "global characterization" question — it asks you to define WHAT the exhibition IS, overall. This requires synthesizing Paragraphs 1 and 2 primarily.

Step 1: What does the text say the exhibition IS?

From Paragraph 1:

"voir rassemblées les pièces représentatives de sa période tahitienne"
= seeing assembled the representative pieces of his Tahitian period

From Paragraph 2:

"C'est à présent une facette totalement différente du maître qui est valorisée"
= a completely different facet of the master is being showcased

"une autre inspiration avec d'autres rencontres, d'autres paysages"
= another inspiration, other encounters, other landscapes

The exhibition focuses on ONE SPECIFIC PERIOD (Tahiti) of ONE ARTIST (Gauguin). It's a period-specific retrospective.

Step 2: Check each option.

Option A — "présente de nombreuses œuvres inédites de l'artiste"

"Œuvres inédites" = previously unseen/unpublished works.

The text says: "Rares sont ceux qui ne connaissent pas l'œuvre du peintre" = few people DON'T know the painter's work. This means the works ARE known. The novelty isn't that the works are unseen — it's that they're ASSEMBLED together for the first time in this way.

Also, the masterpiece in Paragraph 4 has been away from France for 50 years but is not described as "inédite" — it EXISTS, it's KNOWN, it just hasn't been in France recently.

❌ ELIMINATED — The text emphasizes the works are known, not unseen

This is a subtle but critical distinction:

TEXT:     Known works, assembled in a new way = novel PRESENTATION
OPTION A: Unknown works, never seen before = novel CONTENT

The novelty is in the CURATION, not in the WORKS themselves.

Option B — "retrace une des périodes de vie et de création du maître"

  • "retrace" = traces, follows, chronicles ✓
  • "une des périodes" = one of the periods ✓ (the Tahitian period, not his whole career)
  • "de vie et de création" = of life and creative work ✓ (the text describes both the cultural context — encounters, landscapes — and the artistic output — paintings, sculptures)
  • "du maître" = of the master ✓ (the text calls him "le maître")

Let's verify: does the exhibition cover ONE period? Yes — "sa période tahitienne." Does it cover both life and creation? Yes — the cultural immersion (life) AND the paintings/sculptures (creation).

✅ CORRECT

Option C — "fait double emploi avec celle du musée du Luxembourg"

"Fait double emploi" = duplicates / is redundant with.

The text says the EXACT OPPOSITE:

"Le musée du Luxembourg a déjà présenté « La période bretonne de Gauguin ». C'est à présent une facette totalement différente du maître qui est valorisée."

The Luxembourg showed the BRETON period. The Grand Palais shows the TAHITIAN period. These are "totalement différente" — completely different. No duplication.

❌ ELIMINATED — Direct contradiction: "totalement différente" ≠ "double emploi"

Option D — "met en valeur les premiers travaux de jeunesse du peintre"

"Premiers travaux de jeunesse" = early youthful works.

The text never mentions Gauguin's youth or early works. The Tahitian period was NOT Gauguin's youth — it was a later period of his career (he went to Tahiti as a mature artist). The text mentions a painting he made "avant de mourir" (before dying), which is the OPPOSITE of youthful work.

❌ ELIMINATED — No mention of youth; text actually describes late-career work

✅ ANSWER: B

The Lesson Here: "Known Works, New Arrangement" vs "Unknown Works"

Art exhibitions can be novel in TWO ways:

  1. Novel CONTENT: showing works nobody has seen before (inédites)
  2. Novel CURATION: assembling known works in a new thematic arrangement

This text describes type 2. The works are known ("rares sont ceux qui ne connaissent pas"), but the ASSEMBLY is striking ("quelque chose de frappant"). Option A confuses these two types of novelty.

RULE: In exhibition reviews, distinguish between the novelty of the WORKS (content) and the novelty of the PRESENTATION (curation). The text will signal which type applies.

QUESTION 18: Les toiles présentées à l'exposition...

This question targets ROOM 1 — THE PAINTINGS = Paragraph 2. The word "toiles" (canvases/paintings) tells you to look specifically at the painting descriptions.

Step 1: Locate the painting descriptions in Paragraph 2.

"Omniprésence des femmes dévêtues, couleurs vives, enivrent le regard."

Key: "couleurs vives" = VIVID colors. "Enivrent le regard" = intoxicate the gaze (overwhelming visual impact).

"Gauguin bouscule les formes artistiques en témoignant dans une recherche énergique et presque sauvage de la culture polynésienne."

Key: "bouscule les formes artistiques" = DISRUPTS artistic forms/conventions. "Recherche énergique et presque sauvage" = energetic and almost wild exploration.

Step 2: What do these descriptions tell us about the artistic style?

VIVID COLORS → not delicate, not pastel
INTOXICATING → overwhelming, powerful
DISRUPTS ARTISTIC FORMS → breaks conventions
ENERGETIC AND ALMOST WILD → raw, unconventional
NUDE WOMEN → human figures dominate

The overwhelming message: Gauguin's Tahitian paintings BREAK RULES. They're vivid, powerful, unconventional, almost savage.

Step 3: Check each option.

Option A — "perpétuent la tradition de la peinture académique"

"Perpétuent la tradition" = continue the tradition. "Peinture académique" = academic painting (conventional, rule-following).

The text says Gauguin "bouscule les formes artistiques" = DISRUPTS artistic forms. Disrupting is the OPPOSITE of perpetuating. Academic painting follows rules; Gauguin breaks them.

❌ ELIMINATED — Direct contradiction: "bouscule" (disrupts) ≠ "perpétuent" (continue)

Option B — "déclinent une palette tendre et délicate de tons pastel"

"Palette tendre et délicate" = soft and delicate palette. "Tons pastel" = pastel tones.

The text says: "couleurs vives" = VIVID colors. Vivid is the OPPOSITE of pastel. Pastel is soft, muted, gentle. Vivid is bold, bright, intense.

❌ ELIMINATED — "couleurs vives" (vivid) ≠ "tons pastel" (soft/muted)

This is a direct sensory contradiction. The text describes bold, intoxicating colors; the option describes gentle, soft tones. These are incompatible visual experiences.

Option C — "s'éloignent des conventions esthétiques et artistiques"

  • "s'éloignent des conventions" = move away from conventions ✓
  • "esthétiques et artistiques" = aesthetic and artistic ✓

Let's map:

  • Text: "bouscule les formes artistiques" → disrupts artistic forms
  • Option: "s'éloignent des conventions esthétiques et artistiques" → move away from conventions

"Bouscule les formes" (disrupts forms) ↔ "s'éloignent des conventions" (depart from conventions)

This is a perfect paraphrase. Both describe the same thing: breaking away from established artistic rules.

✅ CORRECT

Option D — "privilégient les paysages sauvages aux figures humaines"

"Privilégient les paysages aux figures humaines" = favor landscapes OVER human figures.

The text says: "Omniprésence des femmes dévêtues" = omnipresence of nude women. "Omniprésence" means they are EVERYWHERE — human figures DOMINATE. This is the opposite of privileging landscapes over figures.

❌ ELIMINATED — "Omniprésence des femmes" = figures dominate, not landscapes

✅ ANSWER: C

The Lesson Here: The Synonym-Antonym Axis in Art Criticism

Art criticism questions often hinge on whether the answer is a SYNONYM or ANTONYM of the text's description:

TEXT WORD          SYNONYM (✓)              ANTONYM (✗)
"bouscule"      → "s'éloigne de" (C)    → "perpétue" (A)
"couleurs vives" → "palette intense"     → "tons pastel" (B)
"omniprésence"  → "domination"          → "absence" / "privilégie X aux figures" (D)

Two out of four wrong options (A and B) are ANTONYMS disguised as descriptions. This is a Phase 4 pattern: the wrong answer says the OPPOSITE of what the text says, using art vocabulary that sounds plausible.

RULE: In art criticism texts, check whether each option's key adjective/verb is a SYNONYM or ANTONYM of the text's descriptor. If it's an antonym, it's wrong — no matter how artistically sophisticated it sounds.

QUESTION 19: Le volet de l'exposition consacré à la sculpture...

This question targets ROOM 2 — THE SCULPTURES = Paragraph 3. The word "sculpture" directs you precisely.

Step 1: Re-read Paragraph 3 carefully.

"Cette rétrospective serait incomplète sans la présentation d'une facette artistique moins connue du peintre : la sculpture."

Key: Gauguin's sculpture is a "facette moins connue" — a LESSER-KNOWN facet.

"Aux côtés de pièces traditionnelles des îles Marquises, les œuvres de Gauguin s'imprègnent totalement de cette influence."

Key: Gauguin's sculptures are shown ALONGSIDE traditional Marquesan pieces. His works are TOTALLY IMBUED with this influence.

"Il est alors aisé de voir comment l'artiste s'est progressivement emparé de cette culture jusqu'à en devenir l'un des représentants les plus actifs."

Key: Gauguin PROGRESSIVELY APPROPRIATED Polynesian culture → became one of its most active REPRESENTATIVES.

Step 2: Synthesize the paragraph's argument.

STRUCTURE:
1. Gauguin also sculpted (lesser-known fact)
2. His sculptures are shown NEXT TO traditional Marquesan pieces
3. His work is DEEPLY INFLUENCED by Marquesan tradition
4. He PROGRESSIVELY ABSORBED the culture
5. He BECAME one of its most active representatives

ARGUMENT: Gauguin's sculptures are INSCRIBED within the Polynesian
          artistic tradition — he absorbed it so deeply that he became
          one of its representatives.

Step 3: Check each option.

Option A — "se limite aux pièces traditionnelles des îles Marquises"

"Se limite aux" = is limited to / only shows.

The text says Gauguin's sculptures are shown "aux côtés de" (alongside) traditional pieces. "Alongside" means BOTH are present — Gauguin's works AND traditional works. The exhibition is NOT limited to traditional pieces only; it includes Gauguin's sculptures too.

❌ ELIMINATED — "aux côtés de" (alongside) ≠ "se limite aux" (limited to)

This trap removes Gauguin from his own exhibition section. The text shows a DIALOGUE between traditional pieces and Gauguin's work. Option A erases Gauguin's contribution.

Option B — "met en valeur l'activité de collectionneur d'art de Gauguin"

"Collectionneur d'art" = art collector.

The text never mentions Gauguin as a COLLECTOR. He is described as a CREATOR — he made sculptures influenced by Polynesian tradition. There's a fundamental difference:

COLLECTOR = acquires others' works (passive relationship to art)
CREATOR = makes his own works influenced by others (active relationship)

The text says Gauguin "s'est emparé de cette culture" (seized this culture) and produced works that "s'imprègnent de cette influence" (are imbued with this influence). He CREATED, not collected.

❌ ELIMINATED — Confuses creator with collector

Option C — "fait ressortir l'influence bretonne dans l'œuvre de Gauguin"

"Influence bretonne" = Breton influence.

The text explicitly SEPARATES the Breton period from the Tahitian period:

"Le musée du Luxembourg a déjà présenté « La période bretonne »... C'est à présent une facette totalement différente"

The sculpture section shows POLYNESIAN influence, not Breton influence. Brittany belongs to a different exhibition at a different museum.

❌ ELIMINATED — Wrong cultural influence: Polynesian, not Breton

This is a cross-paragraph contamination trap. The word "bretonne" DOES appear in the text — but in Paragraph 2, referring to the Luxembourg exhibition. If you don't track which paragraph corresponds to which exhibition section, you'll import information from the wrong "room."

Option D — "inscrit l'œuvre de Gauguin dans la tradition artistique des îles"

  • "inscrit" = inscribes, places within ✓
  • "l'œuvre de Gauguin" = Gauguin's work ✓
  • "dans la tradition artistique des îles" = within the artistic tradition of the islands ✓

Let's map to the text:

  • "s'imprègnent totalement de cette influence" → totally imbued with this (island) influence ✓
  • "s'est progressivement emparé de cette culture" → progressively took hold of this culture ✓
  • "devenir l'un des représentants les plus actifs" → became one of its most active representatives ✓

"Inscrit dans la tradition des îles" is a paraphrase of the entire paragraph's argument: Gauguin's sculpture is so deeply rooted in Polynesian tradition that he became one of its representatives.

✅ CORRECT

✅ ANSWER: D

The Lesson Here: The "Progressive Appropriation" Trajectory

Paragraph 3 describes a trajectory — a process over time:

STAGE 1: Gauguin encounters Marquesan art (alongside traditional pieces)
STAGE 2: His work absorbs this influence (s'imprègnent)
STAGE 3: He progressively seizes the culture (s'est emparé)
STAGE 4: He becomes one of its representatives (devenir un représentant)

The correct answer captures the END POINT of this trajectory: Gauguin's work is INSCRIBED within the island tradition. It doesn't describe the starting point (encounter) or the middle (absorption) — it describes the CONCLUSION (full integration).

RULE: When a text describes a progressive trajectory (encounter → absorption → integration), the correct answer usually captures the CONCLUSION of that trajectory, not an intermediate step.

QUESTION 20: La pièce principale de l'exposition...

This question targets ROOM 3 — THE MASTERPIECE = Paragraph 4. "Pièce principale" = main piece = "pièce maîtresse."

Step 1: Re-read Paragraph 4 with forensic precision.

"Une pièce maîtresse de cette exposition « D'où venons-nous ? Que sommes-nous ? Où allons-nous ? » une sorte de testament pictural revient pour la première fois en France depuis 50 ans."

Key facts:

  • It's a "testament pictural" = pictorial testament (final artistic statement)
  • It "revient pour la première fois en France depuis 50 ans"

Let's parse this EXTREMELY carefully: "revient pour la première fois en France depuis 50 ans"

  • "revient" = COMES BACK (implies it was in France before)
  • "pour la première fois" = for the first time
  • "depuis 50 ans" = in 50 years

Combined: it COMES BACK to France for the first time IN 50 years = it's been AWAY from France for 50 years and is now RETURNING.

This means:

  • It WAS in France before (at some point in the past)
  • It LEFT France (went elsewhere)
  • It has been ABSENT for 50 years
  • It is now RETURNING

"Gauguin a entrepris la réalisation de ce tableau avant de mourir."

Key: painted BEFORE DYING (one of his last works).

"Durant un mois, il a travaillé jour et nuit dans une fièvre"

Key: ONE MONTH of feverish, day-and-night work.

"cette œuvre exemplaire de celui qui revendique le droit de tout oser"

Key: the work of someone who CLAIMS THE RIGHT TO DARE EVERYTHING.

Step 2: Decode "testament pictural"

"Testament" in French has TWO meanings:

  1. Legal document distributing property after death
  2. Figurative: final statement, legacy, last word

"Testament pictural" = the painter's FINAL ARTISTIC STATEMENT — a painting that encapsulates everything he believed, his life philosophy, his artistic credo. It's his last word to the world.

The TITLE of the painting reinforces this: "D'où venons-nous ? Que sommes-nous ? Où allons-nous ?" — these are the ULTIMATE questions of human existence. This is not a casual painting; it's a philosophical meditation on life itself.

Step 3: Check each option.

Option A — "a résumé la pensée du peintre sur la vie"

  • "a résumé" = summarized ✓
  • "la pensée du peintre" = the painter's thought/philosophy ✓
  • "sur la vie" = about life ✓

Let's verify:

  • "testament pictural" = final artistic statement → summarizes his artistic philosophy ✓
  • Title: "Where do we come from? What are we? Where are we going?" → questions about LIFE ✓
  • "revendique le droit de tout oser" → claims the right to dare everything → artistic philosophy ✓

"Résumé la pensée du peintre sur la vie" is a paraphrase of "testament pictural" — a testament summarizes one's final thoughts, and the painting's title is explicitly about life's fundamental questions.

✅ STRONG CANDIDATE

Option B — "a requis 50 ans de travail fébrile de l'artiste"

"A requis 50 ans de travail" = required 50 years of work.

The text says TWO different things with the number 50:

  1. The painting RETURNS to France for the first time in 50 years (absence duration)
  2. The painting was made in one month ("durant un mois")

Option B CONFLATES these two facts: it takes "50 ans" from fact 1 and "travail fébrile" from fact 2, and creates a CHIMERA that says "50 years of feverish work." But the painting took ONE MONTH, not 50 years. And the 50 years refers to its ABSENCE from France, not its creation time.

❌ ELIMINATED — Chimera: merges "50 ans d'absence" with "un mois de travail fébrile"

This is one of the most sophisticated traps you'll encounter in Phase 4. Let's name it: the "Number Migration" trap. A number from one context (50 years away from France) migrates to a different context (50 years of work). Both "50 ans" and "fièvre/fébrile" are IN the text — but they belong to DIFFERENT FACTS.

FACT 1: Painting returns after 50 YEARS absence from France
FACT 2: Painting created in ONE MONTH of feverish work
TRAP:   50 YEARS of feverish work (combines number from Fact 1 + description from Fact 2)

Option C — "n'a pas quitté la France depuis 50 ans"

"N'a pas quitté la France" = has NOT LEFT France.

The text says: "revient pour la première fois en France depuis 50 ans" = COMES BACK to France. "Revient" means it LEFT and is now RETURNING. Option C says it NEVER LEFT — this is the exact opposite.

TEXT:     The painting LEFT France and is RETURNING after 50 years
OPTION C: The painting NEVER LEFT France for 50 years

❌ ELIMINATED — Reverses the direction of movement

This is a directional reversal trap: the painting's movement is FROM abroad BACK TO France. Option C reverses this to FROM France NOWHERE (it stayed).

Option D — "est exposée en France pour la première fois"

"Pour la première fois" = for the first time (ever).

The text says: "revient pour la première fois depuis 50 ans" = for the first time IN 50 YEARS. This means it HAS been in France before (otherwise it couldn't "come back"). It's not the absolute FIRST time — it's the first time in a LONG WHILE.

TEXT:     First time IN 50 YEARS (has been in France before, long ago)
OPTION D: First time EVER (has never been in France)

"Pour la première fois" + "depuis 50 ans" ≠ "pour la première fois" alone.

The phrase "depuis 50 ans" is CRUCIAL — it limits "la première fois" to a specific timeframe. Without it, the meaning changes completely:

  • "Pour la première fois" = first time EVER (absolute)
  • "Pour la première fois depuis 50 ans" = first time IN 50 YEARS (relative)

❌ ELIMINATED — Drops the critical qualifier "depuis 50 ans," changing the meaning from relative to absolute

✅ ANSWER: A

The Lesson Here: Three Traps in One Question

Question 20 contains THREE distinct trap types, each targeting a different reading failure:

TRAP TYPE 1: NUMBER MIGRATION (Option B)
├── Takes "50 ans" from one fact
├── Combines it with "fébrile" from another fact
└── Creates a false hybrid: "50 ans de travail fébrile"

TRAP TYPE 2: DIRECTIONAL REVERSAL (Option C)
├── Text says painting RETURNS to France (came back)
├── Option says painting NEVER LEFT France (stayed)
└── Reverses the direction of movement

TRAP TYPE 3: QUALIFIER DELETION (Option D)
├── Text says "pour la première fois DEPUIS 50 ANS"
├── Option says "pour la première fois" (drops "depuis 50 ans")
└── Changes meaning from RELATIVE first time to ABSOLUTE first time

RULE: When a text contains numbers, time expressions, and qualifiers, the wrong answers will either MIGRATE numbers between facts, REVERSE directions of movement, or DELETE crucial qualifiers. Verify that EVERY element of your chosen answer matches the text EXACTLY — including numbers, directions, and limiting phrases.

Strategy 2: The Synonym-Antonym Quick Test

✅ WHAT SHOULD YOU DO

DO #1: Build your paragraph-section map BEFORE reading questions

The moment you finish reading an exhibition review, create the mental map:

Para 1 = overview
Para 2 = paintings/main section
Para 3 = secondary section (sculpture)
Para 4 = highlight/masterpiece

This takes 10 seconds and saves minutes of confusion later.

DO #2: Decode art vocabulary through CONTEXT, not memory

You don't need to know what "testament pictural" means from prior knowledge. The text EXPLAINS it:

  • It's a painting made "avant de mourir" (before dying) → it's a FINAL work
  • It asks "D'où venons-nous? Que sommes-nous? Où allons-nous?" → it addresses LIFE's questions
  • Therefore: "testament pictural" = final artistic statement about life

Always let the CONTEXT define unfamiliar terms.

DO #3: Read time expressions as COMPLETE UNITS

"Pour la première fois depuis 50 ans" is ONE expression. Don't split it:

  • ✅ "for the first time in 50 years" (complete)
  • ❌ "for the first time" (truncated — changes meaning)

Time expressions in French are often multi-word units where removing any part changes the meaning.

DO #4: Verify the DIRECTION of movement/change

When a text describes something coming, going, returning, or changing:

  • "revient" = COMES BACK (direction: toward France)
  • "a quitté" = LEFT (direction: away from France)
  • "n'a pas quitté" = DID NOT LEAVE (direction: stayed in France)

The verb's direction must match the answer's direction.

DO #5: Distinguish CONTRAST from DUPLICATION between exhibitions

The text establishes:

Luxembourg = Breton period
Grand Palais = Tahitian period
RELATIONSHIP: "totalement différente" = NO overlap

Any answer suggesting similarity or duplication between the two is wrong.

❌ WHAT SHOULD YOU NOT DO

DON'T #1: Import information from the wrong paragraph

Option C of Q19 mentions "influence bretonne" — which IS in the text, but in Paragraph 2, referring to the Luxembourg exhibition. The sculpture section (Paragraph 3) discusses POLYNESIAN influence. Importing "bretonne" from Para 2 into a Para 3 question is cross-paragraph contamination.

As established in Chapter 16 (Stakeholder Law, now Section Law): each question targets ONE section. Stay within its paragraph.

DON'T #2: Assume art vocabulary is literal

"Bouscule les formes" doesn't mean Gauguin physically pushed anything. "Testament pictural" doesn't mean a legal document. "Enivrent le regard" doesn't mean anyone is drunk. Art criticism is METAPHORICAL. Read for the figurative meaning, not the literal one.

DON'T #3: Merge numbers from different facts

The "50 ans de travail fébrile" chimera in Option B is created by merging:

  • "50 ans" (from the absence-from-France fact)
  • "fébrile" (from the one-month-creation fact)

Always ask: does this number BELONG to this fact in the TEXT?

DON'T #4: Drop qualifiers from time expressions

"Pour la première fois" ≠ "pour la première fois depuis 50 ans." The qualifier "depuis 50 ans" changes the meaning from ABSOLUTE (never before) to RELATIVE (not in recent history). Never accept an answer that drops a qualifier.

DON'T #5: Confuse the artist's ROLE

Gauguin is a CREATOR (painter, sculptor), not a COLLECTOR. He MADE works influenced by Polynesian art; he didn't COLLECT Polynesian art. Option B of Q19 confuses these roles. Always check: is the text describing what the artist CREATED or what they ACQUIRED?

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL 1: The "Cross-Paragraph Contamination" Error

YOUR BRAIN: "The text mentions 'bretonne' → Option C says 'influence bretonne' → match!"
REALITY:    "Bretonne" appears in Paragraph 2 (about the Luxembourg exhibition)
            Question 19 asks about Paragraph 3 (sculptures at Grand Palais)
            Paragraph 3 discusses POLYNESIAN influence, not Breton
LESSON:     Each question targets ONE paragraph/section.
            Find the right paragraph FIRST, then answer ONLY from it.

PITFALL 2: The "Number Migration" Chimera

YOUR BRAIN: "50 ans is in the text, fébrile is in the text → 50 ans de travail fébrile!"
REALITY:    50 ans = duration of ABSENCE from France
            fébrile/fièvre = description of ONE MONTH of creation
            These belong to DIFFERENT facts — combining them creates fiction
LESSON:     Every number in the text is BOUND to a specific fact.
            Never allow a number to migrate from its original fact
            to a different context.

PITFALL 3: The "Directional Reversal" Error

YOUR BRAIN: "The painting and France and 50 years → it stayed in France!"
REALITY:    "REVIENT pour la première fois" = COMES BACK
            "Revient" = returns (it was AWAY, now it's BACK)
            "N'a pas quitté" = never left (STAYED)
            These are OPPOSITE movements.
LESSON:     Verify the DIRECTION implied by the verb.
            "Revient" = was away, returns
            "N'a pas quitté" = never left, stayed

PITFALL 4: The "Qualifier Deletion" Trap

YOUR BRAIN: "'Pour la première fois' → Option D says 'pour la première fois' → match!"
REALITY:    Text: "pour la première fois DEPUIS 50 ANS" = first time IN 50 YEARS
            Option D: "pour la première fois" (alone) = first time EVER
            The qualifier "depuis 50 ans" changes ABSOLUTE to RELATIVE
LESSON:     Time expressions are COMPLETE UNITS.
            Dropping any part changes the meaning fundamentally.
            Always read the FULL expression before matching.

PITFALL 5: The "Antonym-as-Synonym" Misread

YOUR BRAIN: "'Perpétuent la tradition' → Gauguin is traditional → makes sense!"
REALITY:    Text: "BOUSCULE les formes artistiques" = DISRUPTS conventions
            "Perpétuent" = CONTINUE/MAINTAIN
            Disrupting ≠ Continuing → these are ANTONYMS
LESSON:     In art criticism, the key verb defines the artist's relationship
            to tradition. "Bouscule" = breaks rules. "Perpétue" = follows rules.
            Check: is the option's verb a SYNONYM or ANTONYM of the text's?

PITFALL 6: The "Creator vs Collector" Role Confusion

YOUR BRAIN: "Gauguin is shown alongside Marquesan pieces → he collected them!"
REALITY:    "Aux côtés de" = alongside (side by side for comparison)
            His works "s'imprègnent de cette influence" = are influenced by
            He CREATED sculptures influenced by island art
            He did NOT COLLECT island art
LESSON:     "Alongside" means COMPARISON, not COLLECTION.
            An artist shown next to traditional pieces is being
            COMPARED to them, not presented as their collector.

LAW 1: THE SECTION-PARAGRAPH LAW — In exhibition reviews, each paragraph describes a different section. Each question targets ONE section. Map paragraphs to sections FIRST, then answer only from the targeted paragraph. Never import information from the wrong section.
