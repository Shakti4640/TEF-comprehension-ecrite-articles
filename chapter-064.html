📄 COMPLETE ARTICLE

Comment un baron futé rentabilisa un objet qu'il n'avait pas inventé en proposant son stylo bille au prix d'une cartouche d'encre. En 1973, lorsque le baron Marcel Bich introduit la société Bic en bourse, peu de gens savent que le baron n'a pas inventé le stylo à bille. Mais les actionnaires comprennent que le brave Marcel a eu un coup de génie. L'inventeur s'appelle John Loud, comme le prouve une licence déposée aux USA en 1888. À l'origine, il cherchait à fabriquer un objet capable de laisser des traces sur des matériaux rugueux comme le cuir. Son stylo n'intéressa personne car il faisait trop de taches.

Cinquante ans après, le journaliste hongrois Laszlo Biro aidé de son frère chimiste, perfectionne l'invention en Argentine. Une copie de son Birolen fut, aux USA, le premier stylo à bille jamais vendu. Mais là où Marcel Bich frappe fort, c'est sous l'introduction du concept « jetable ». En ces heures dorées de la reconstruction d'après-guerre, le baron propose son stylo-bille au prix d'une cartouche d'encre. Jusqu'alors il s'occupait avec un dénommé Édouard Buffard d'une usine parisienne produisant des pièces détachées de stylo-plumes et de porte-mines.

La licence permet à l'objet de supplanter le vieux stylo-plume Sergent Major. Fortune est faite lorsque l'Éducation nationale accepte l'utilisation du Bic en classe, en 1965.

Depuis, l'art d'écrire s'est révélé très lucratif pour l'entreprise. Aujourd'hui, elle vend, chaque jour, plus de 21 millions d'articles de papeterie à travers le monde, auxquels s'ajoutent quatre millions de briquets et neuf millions de rasoirs.

QUESTION 20: L'idée de génie du baron Bich a été...

What the question really asks: What was Bich's SPECIFIC stroke of genius?

The text's subtitle already tells us:

"Comment un baron futé rentabilisa un objet qu'il n'avait pas inventé en proposant son stylo bille au prix d'une cartouche d'encre."

This sentence contains the COMPLETE answer in compressed form:

  • "N'avait pas inventé" → He did NOT invent it
  • "Rentabilisa" → He made it PROFITABLE
  • "Au prix d'une cartouche d'encre" → By making it CHEAP (price of an ink cartridge)

And the body text confirms:

"là où Marcel Bich frappe fort, c'est sous l'introduction du concept « jetable »"
"le baron propose son stylo-bille au prix d'une cartouche d'encre"

Bich's genius = making the pen DISPOSABLE and CHEAP. Not inventing it. Not speculating on it. Making it affordable.

Now let's test:

Option A: "d'inventer le stylo à bille"
(Inventing the ballpoint pen)

The text says EXPLICITLY — three times:

  1. "rentabilisa un objet qu'il n'avait pas inventé" (commercialized something he had NOT invented)
  2. "peu de gens savent que le baron n'a pas inventé le stylo à bille" (few people know he did NOT invent it)
  3. "L'inventeur s'appelle John Loud" (the inventor is John Loud)

THREE explicit denials. This is the most heavily signaled trap in the entire text. The text practically SCREAMS: "He did NOT invent it!"

This is the ACHIEVEMENT MISATTRIBUTION trap — assigning the inventor's achievement to the marketer.

❌ ELIMINATED — The text explicitly and repeatedly denies this (triple negation)

Option B: "de spéculer sur le stylo Bic"
(Speculating on the Bic pen)

The text mentions the stock exchange: "introduit la société Bic en bourse"

"En bourse" (stock exchange) relates to financial speculation. But the stock listing happened in 1973 — LONG AFTER Bich's genius move (the disposable concept in the post-war era).

The stock listing was a CONSEQUENCE of success, not the cause. Going public is what happens AFTER you've already succeeded — it's not the genius idea itself.

Bich's geniusStock listing
Making pens cheap and disposable (CAUSE)Going public in 1973 (CONSEQUENCE)

This is a Cause-Consequence Confusion — presenting a RESULT as the CAUSE of success.

❌ ELIMINATED — Stock listing was a consequence of success, not the genius idea

(Selling the ballpoint pen cheaply)

The text says:

  • "proposant son stylo bille au prix d'une cartouche d'encre" (at the price of an ink cartridge)
  • "l'introduction du concept « jetable »" (the disposable concept)
  • "rentabilisa" (made profitable)

THE LESSON FROM Q22: The "Jusqu'alors" Temporal Pivot

"Jusqu'alors" (until then) is a TEMPORAL PIVOT — similar to "En fait" from Project 11, but operating in TIME rather than in expectation/reality.

TEMPORAL PIVOT STRUCTURE:

AFTER "jusqu'alors" in the text = BEFORE the event chronologically
→ What Bich did BEFORE the cheap pen = factory with Buffard

The text says: "He proposes his pen... Jusqu'alors he ran a factory"
Chronologically: First he ran the factory, THEN he launched the pen

"Jusqu'alors" reverses the narrative order — the text mentions the LATER event first, then goes BACK to describe the EARLIER situation. You must mentally reorder:

NARRATIVE ORDER (as written):
1. Bich proposes cheap pen (post-war)
2. "Jusqu'alors" → he ran a factory (pre-pen)

CHRONOLOGICAL ORDER (as happened):
1. Bich ran a factory with Buffard (late 1940s)
2. Then he launched the cheap pen (post-war innovation)

🏆 GOLDEN RULE #42: Watch for REVERSE-CHRONOLOGY PIVOTS like "jusqu'alors" (until then), "auparavant" (previously), "avant cela" (before that). These words signal that the text is going BACKWARD in time. The sentence AFTER the pivot describes an EARLIER period than the sentence before it. Mentally reorder to avoid confusion.

QUESTION 23: Le premier événement décisif pour la société Bic a été...

What the question really asks: What was the FIRST decisive/turning-point event for Bic?

Notice: "premier événement décisif" — the FIRST DECISIVE event. This has TWO requirements:

  1. It must be DECISIVE (a turning point, not just an event)
  2. It must be the FIRST such event (chronologically earliest decisive moment)

The key passage:

"Fortune est faite lorsque l'Éducation nationale accepte l'utilisation du Bic en classe, en 1965."

"Fortune est faite" = fortune is made. This is the text's explicit marker of the DECISIVE MOMENT — the point where real success/wealth was achieved.

But let me map ALL significant events to check which is truly FIRST and DECISIVE:

EVENTS EVALUATION:

1888: Loud's patent → NOT about Bic (different company/person)
~1938: Biro's improvement → NOT about Bic
Post-war: Bich's cheap pen concept → IMPORTANT but not marked as "fortune"
1965: Schools accept Bic → "FORTUNE EST FAITE" ← EXPLICIT MARKER

Now let's test:

Option A: "sa cotation en bourse"
(Its stock exchange listing)

The stock listing happened in 1973. The fortune was already made in 1965 ("Fortune est faite...en 1965").

Chronologically: 1965 (decisive) → 1973 (stock listing). The stock listing came AFTER the decisive moment — it's a FORMALIZATION of existing success, not the turning point itself.

The question asks for the FIRST decisive event. 1973 comes after 1965.

❌ ELIMINATED — Stock listing (1973) came AFTER the decisive moment (1965)

Option B: "la vente du stylo aux USA"
(The sale of the pen in the USA)

The text mentions a pen sold in the USA: "Une copie de son Birolen fut, aux USA, le premier stylo à bille jamais vendu"

But this was BIRO's pen (the Birolen), not BIC's pen. The sale happened before Bich's involvement. It's about the HISTORY of the ballpoint pen, not about the Bic company.

This is Achievement Misattribution (Rule #41) — attributing Biro's USA sale to the Bic company.

❌ ELIMINATED — This was Biro's pen sale, not Bic's (achievement misattribution)

The text says: "La licence permet à l'objet de supplanter le vieux stylo-plume Sergent Major"

"Supplanter" = to supplant/replace — to take the place of. This means Bic REPLACED Sergent Major in the market. It does NOT mean Bic BOUGHT Sergent Major.

This is a CRITICAL VOCABULARY TRAP — "supplanter" sounds business-like and could be confused with "racheter" by a non-precise reader. But they describe fundamentally different events.

THE LESSON FROM Q23: Finding the "Fortune Marker"

Business narrative texts often contain EXPLICIT SUCCESS MARKERS — phrases that tell you EXACTLY when success arrived:

COMMON SUCCESS MARKERS IN FRENCH:

"Fortune est faite"          → Fortune is made (this is THE moment)
"Le succès est assuré"       → Success is assured
"C'est le tournant"          → This is the turning point
"Tout change lorsque..."     → Everything changes when...
"Le déclic se produit"       → The trigger occurs
"La consécration arrive"     → Recognition arrives

When the text contains such a marker, the "decisive event" question is ANSWERED by whatever event the marker is attached to. Don't overthink — follow the marker.

4. Find the SUCCESS MARKER.

6. Check entity assignments obsessively.

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL 35: Achievement Misattribution (NEW)

First introduced here. Will reappear in Projects 19, 20, 24.

What it is: An answer assigns one person's achievement (invention, sale, discovery) to a different person mentioned in the text.

How to detect: Before answering any "who did what?" question, consult your Actor-Achievement Map. Verify: "Does the TEXT say THIS person did THIS thing?"

In this text: Option A (Q20) attributes invention to Bich. The text explicitly attributes invention to Loud. Option B (Q23) attributes the USA pen sale to Bic. The text attributes it to Biro.

Defense: Build the Actor-Achievement Map as your first step.

PITFALL 36: The Reverse Chronology Confusion (NEW)

First introduced here. Will reappear in Projects 19, 20.

What it is: The text mentions a LATER event first, then goes back to an EARLIER event using "jusqu'alors" or similar pivots. The reader confuses the temporal order.

How to detect: Watch for reverse-chronology markers: "jusqu'alors," "auparavant," "avant cela," "jusque-là."

In this text: "Jusqu'alors il s'occupait d'une usine" — the factory came BEFORE the cheap pen, even though the text mentions the cheap pen first.

Defense: Build the timeline in CHRONOLOGICAL order, not in narrative order.

PITFALL 37: Competition vs. Transaction Vocabulary Confusion (NEW)

First introduced here. Will reappear in Projects 16, 23.

What it is: Market competition verbs (supplanter, rivaliser) are confused with corporate transaction verbs (racheter, acquérir).

How to detect: When you see a business action word, ask: "Is this about winning in the MARKET or making a financial DEAL?"

In this text: "Supplanter" (outcompete) ≠ "rachat" (acquisition).

Defense: Maintain the vocabulary distinction (Rule #43).
