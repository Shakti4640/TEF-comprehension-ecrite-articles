CHAPTER 8: PARAPHRASE RECOGNITION — WHEN THE ANSWER RESTATES THE TEXT (TEXTE 101, Q25)

Core Patterns | Phase 2 of 5

📄 THE ARTICLE

We return to Texte 101 (Paroles de techno prophète). Refer back to Chapter 1 for full text. In this chapter, we focus on the fundamental paradox of Compréhension Écrite:

The correct answer almost NEVER uses the same words as the text. And the wrong answer often DOES use the text's exact words.

We use Q25 as our primary laboratory, then extend the technique to all other questions.

Question 25 : la principale théorie de Ray Kurzweil affirme que
 A. les hommes et les outils technologiques ne feront plus qu'un.
 B. La technologie dépossédera l'homme de certaines de ses qualités.
 C. Les outils technologiques seront supérieurs en intelligence aux hommes.
 D. L'humanité utilisera la technologie pour améliorer son patrimoine génétique.

🧠 THE PROBLEM: THE PARADOX OF CORRECT ANSWERS

Here is the most counterintuitive truth about reading comprehension exams:

THE GREAT PARADOX:

  ╔════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
  ║                                                        ║
  ║  WRONG answers often contain EXACT WORDS from the text ║
  ║  RIGHT answers often contain ZERO words from the text  ║
  ║                                                        ║
  ╚════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

  WHY?
  
  Because exam designers KNOW that students scan for 
  word matches. So they:
  
  1. Put text words INTO wrong answers (to attract you)
  2. REPHRASE the correct answer (to test if you understand)
  
  RESULT:
  → Students who match WORDS choose wrong answers
  → Students who match MEANING choose right answers

Let us prove this with Q25:

WORD-MATCHING TEST — Q25 OPTIONS vs. TEXT:

  TEXT WORDS: "êtres humains", "machines non biologiques",
             "fusionné", "différences", "négligeables",
             "intelligence humaine", "améliorée",
             "patrimoine", "génétique"

  OPTION A: "les hommes et les outils technologiques 
             ne feront plus qu'un"
  → TEXT WORDS USED: ZERO ❗
  → "hommes" instead of "êtres humains"
  → "outils technologiques" instead of "machines non biologiques"
  → "ne feront plus qu'un" instead of "fusionné"
  → EVERY word is DIFFERENT from the text

  OPTION B: "La technologie dépossédera l'homme de 
             certaines de ses qualités"
  → TEXT WORDS USED: "technologie" (appears in text ✅)
  → Sounds sophisticated and plausible

  OPTION C: "Les outils technologiques seront supérieurs 
             en intelligence aux hommes"
  → TEXT WORDS USED: "intelligence" (appears in text ✅)
  → Uses a concept FROM the text

  OPTION D: "L'humanité utilisera la technologie pour 
             améliorer son patrimoine génétique"
  → TEXT WORDS USED: "humanité" ✅, "technologie" ✅,
    "améliorer" ≈ "améliorée" ✅, "patrimoine" ✅,
    "génétique" ✅
  → FIVE words borrowed from the text!

  WORD-MATCH SCORES:
  A: 0 words from text  ← CORRECT ANSWER ✅
  B: 1 word from text
  C: 1 word from text
  D: 5 words from text  ← MOST BORROWED WORDS
  
  THE ANSWER WITH THE MOST TEXT WORDS (D) IS WRONG.
  THE ANSWER WITH ZERO TEXT WORDS (A) IS RIGHT.

📌 This is not a coincidence. This is HOW exams are designed. Option D is a WORD BORROWING TRAP (Type 3 from Chapter 6). Option A is a PARAPHRASE — the correct answer disguised in completely new vocabulary.

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT: THE SCIENCE OF PARAPHRASE

A paraphrase is a restatement that changes the words but preserves the meaning. There are exactly five ways to paraphrase a sentence, and exam designers use all of them.

THE 5 PARAPHRASE MECHANISMS

╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║              THE 5 PARAPHRASE MECHANISMS                          ║
╠═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║                                                                   ║
║  MECHANISM 1: SYNONYM SUBSTITUTION                                ║
║  → Replace individual words with their synonyms                   ║
║  → "êtres humains" → "les hommes"                                ║
║  → "machines non biologiques" → "outils technologiques"           ║
║                                                                   ║
║  MECHANISM 2: STRUCTURAL REPHRASING                               ║
║  → Change the sentence structure while keeping meaning            ║
║  → "A et B fusionné" → "A et B ne feront plus qu'un"             ║
║  → Complex sentence → Simple sentence                             ║
║                                                                   ║
║  MECHANISM 3: ABSTRACTION SHIFT                                   ║
║  → Move from specific to general, or general to specific          ║
║  → "télécharger le cerveau" → "la fusion homme-machine"          ║
║  → (specific example → general concept)                           ║
║                                                                   ║
║  MECHANISM 4: PERSPECTIVE CHANGE                                  ║
║  → Same event described from a different angle                    ║
║  → "differences became negligible" →                              ║
║    "they will become one" (same reality, different lens)          ║
║                                                                   ║
║  MECHANISM 5: CONDENSATION/EXPANSION                              ║
║  → Long text passage compressed into short answer                 ║
║  → Or short text expanded in the answer                           ║
║  → An entire paragraph → one sentence answer                     ║
║                                                                   ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

Let us now see ALL FIVE mechanisms at work in Q25, Option A.

🔧 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT: DECONSTRUCTING THE PARAPHRASE IN Q25

THE TEXT (what Kurzweil predicts):

"êtres humains et machines non biologiques pourraient avoir 
 si parfaitement fusionné que les différences entre eux 
 seraient devenues négligeables"

THE CORRECT ANSWER (Option A):

"les hommes et les outils technologiques ne feront plus qu'un"

MECHANISM-BY-MECHANISM ANALYSIS:

MECHANISM 1: SYNONYM SUBSTITUTION

TEXT WORD/PHRASE          →  ANSWER SYNONYM
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"êtres humains"          →  "les hommes"
(human beings)              (men/humans)

"machines non biologiques"→  "les outils technologiques"
(non-biological machines)    (technological tools)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ANALYSIS:
                                                    
  "êtres humains" → "les hommes":
  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ "Êtres humains" = human beings (formal)      │
  │ "Les hommes" = humans/men (common)           │
  │                                              │
  │ Same referent: the human species             │
  │ Different register: formal → common          │
  │ VALID SYNONYM ✅                              │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  "machines non biologiques" → "outils technologiques":
  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ "Machines non biologiques"                   │
  │ = machines that are not biological           │
  │ = artificial/technological machines          │
  │                                              │
  │ "Outils technologiques"                      │
  │ = technological tools                        │
  │ = human-made technology devices              │
  │                                              │
  │ Same referent: technology/artificial systems  │
  │ Different angle: defined by what they're NOT  │
  │ (non-biological) vs. what they ARE (techno)  │
  │ VALID SYNONYM ✅                              │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────┘

MECHANISM 2: STRUCTURAL REPHRASING

TEXT STRUCTURE:
  "A et B pourraient avoir si parfaitement fusionné 
   que les différences entre eux seraient devenues 
   négligeables"
  
  STRUCTURE: A + B + conditional perfect + consequence clause
  (Complex: two conditional verbs + comparison + result)

ANSWER STRUCTURE:
  "A et B ne feront plus qu'un"
  
  STRUCTURE: A + B + future + simple expression
  (Simple: one verb + idiomatic expression)

COMPARISON:
  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │                                                    │
  │  TEXT:    Complex conditional sentence with         │
  │          two clauses and a comparison              │
  │          "so perfectly merged THAT differences      │
  │           would have become negligible"             │
  │                                                    │
  │  ANSWER:  Simple future sentence with               │
  │           one idiomatic expression                  │
  │           "will become one"                         │
  │                                                    │
  │  The STRUCTURE is completely different.             │
  │  The MEANING is identical.                         │
  │                                                    │
  │  VALID STRUCTURAL REPHRASE ✅                       │
  │                                                    │
  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

MECHANISM 3: ABSTRACTION SHIFT

TEXT: Provides SPECIFIC DETAILS about the fusion:
  → "si parfaitement" (so perfectly)
  → "différences... négligeables" (negligible differences)
  → Describes the DEGREE and RESULT of merging

ANSWER: Provides ABSTRACT SUMMARY:
  → "ne feront plus qu'un" (will become one)
  → No details about degree or result
  → Simply states the CONCLUSION

  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │                                                    │
  │  TEXT:    DETAILED description of HOW they merge    │
  │          (perfectly, with negligible differences)   │
  │                                                    │
  │  ANSWER:  ABSTRACT statement of WHAT happens        │
  │          (they become one)                          │
  │                                                    │
  │  SPECIFIC → GENERAL                                │
  │  Details  → Conclusion                             │
  │                                                    │
  │  VALID ABSTRACTION SHIFT ✅                         │
  │                                                    │
  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

MECHANISM 4: PERSPECTIVE CHANGE

TEXT PERSPECTIVE:
  Describes the process FROM THE OUTSIDE:
  "differences between them would become negligible"
  → OBSERVER perspective: looking at humans and machines
    and noting that you can't tell them apart

ANSWER PERSPECTIVE:
  Describes the result FROM THE INSIDE:
  "ne feront plus qu'un" = will be only one
  → IDENTITY perspective: they ARE one thing,
    not two things that look similar

  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │                                                    │
  │  TEXT:    "You won't be able to tell them apart"    │
  │          (external observation)                     │
  │                                                    │
  │  ANSWER:  "They will be the same thing"             │
  │          (internal identity)                        │
  │                                                    │
  │  Same reality, different viewpoint.                 │
  │                                                    │
  │  VALID PERSPECTIVE CHANGE ✅                         │
  │                                                    │
  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

MECHANISM 5: CONDENSATION

TEXT: An entire complex sentence (32 words in French):
  "êtres humains et machines non biologiques pourraient 
   avoir si parfaitement fusionné que les différences 
   entre eux seraient devenues négligeables"

ANSWER: A compact expression (10 words):
  "les hommes et les outils technologiques ne feront 
   plus qu'un"

  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │                                                    │
  │  32 words → 10 words                               │
  │  Complex conditional → Simple future               │
  │  Two clauses → One clause                          │
  │  Detailed process → Summary result                 │
  │                                                    │
  │  All the meaning preserved in 1/3 of the words.    │
  │                                                    │
  │  VALID CONDENSATION ✅                              │
  │                                                    │
  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

📊 PARAPHRASE vs. DISTORTION: THE CRITICAL DISTINCTION

Now that we understand what a VALID paraphrase looks like, let us contrast it with what the WRONG answers do. The wrong answers are NOT paraphrases — they are distortions.

THE DIFFERENCE:

  PARAPHRASE:  Changes WORDS, preserves MEANING ✅
  DISTORTION:  Changes WORDS AND changes MEANING ❌

OPTION B — DISTORTION ANALYSIS

TEXT:   "fusionné... différences négligeables... 
         intelligence améliorée"
        (merged... negligible differences... 
         improved intelligence)

ANSWER: "La technologie dépossédera l'homme de 
         certaines de ses qualités"
        (Technology will STRIP man of some of his qualities)

DISTORTION MAP:
  ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │                                                    │
  │  TEXT MEANING:    ENHANCE / MERGE / IMPROVE        │
  │  ANSWER MEANING:  STRIP / REMOVE / DIMINISH        │
  │                                                    │
  │  This is not a paraphrase.                         │
  │  This is a MEANING INVERSION.                      │
  │                                                    │
  │  The text says technology ADDS to humanity.        │
  │  The answer says technology TAKES FROM humanity.   │
  │                                                    │
  │  ADD ≠ TAKE                                        │
  │  ENHANCE ≠ DISPOSSESS                              │
  │  MERGE ≠ STRIP                                     │
  │                                                    │
  │  DISTORTION TYPE: INVERSION                        │
  │  (meaning flipped to its opposite)                 │
  │                                                    │
  └────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

✅ WHAT SHOULD YOU DO

RULE 1: THE MEANING-FIRST APPROACH

THE MEANING-FIRST PROTOCOL:

  STEP 1: Read the relevant text passage
  STEP 2: CLOSE YOUR EYES (mentally) and ask:
          "What does this MEAN in my own words?"
  STEP 3: Formulate the meaning WITHOUT looking at answers
  STEP 4: THEN open the answers and find which one 
          matches YOUR meaning, not the text's words
          
  EXAMPLE — Q25:
  
  STEP 1: Read "êtres humains et machines... fusionné...
           différences négligeables"
  STEP 2: Close eyes → "Humans and technology will 
           merge completely into one thing"
  STEP 3: My meaning = "humans and tech become one"
  STEP 4: Scan answers:
    A: "ne feront plus qu'un" = "become one" → MATCH! ✅
    B: "dépossédera" = "strip away" → NOT my meaning ❌
    C: "supérieurs" = "superior" → NOT my meaning ❌
    D: "patrimoine génétique" = "genetics" → NOT my meaning ❌

📌 The Meaning-First approach immunizes you against word-borrowing traps because you formulate the answer BEFORE seeing the options. You match MEANING, not WORDS.

  "augmenter"            →  "croître" / "s'accroître" / "progresser"
  "diminuer"             →  "baisser" / "réduire" / "décroître"
  "problème"             →  "difficulté" / "obstacle" / "défi"
  "danger"               →  "risque" / "menace" / "péril"
  "important"            →  "majeur" / "considérable" / "significatif"
  "commencer"            →  "débuter" / "entamer" / "amorcer"
  "pollution"            →  "contamination" / "dégradation"
  "consommation"         →  "utilisation" / "usage"
  
  STRUCTURAL SYNONYMS (expressions):
  "avoir pour but de"    →  "viser à" / "chercher à"
  "être capable de"      →  "pouvoir" / "être en mesure de"
  "mettre en péril"      →  "menacer" / "endangérer" / "compromettre"
  "dans le futur"        →  "à l'avenir" / "ultérieurement"
  "de plus en plus"      →  "croissant" / "en augmentation"

RULE 3: THE SEMANTIC EQUIVALENCE TEST

When you suspect an answer might be a paraphrase, apply this three-step test:

THE SEMANTIC EQUIVALENCE TEST:

  STEP 1: REVERSE PARAPHRASE
  → Can I rewrite the ANSWER in words that look like the TEXT?
  
  ANSWER: "les hommes et les outils technologiques 
           ne feront plus qu'un"
  
  REVERSE: "les êtres humains et les machines non biologiques
            fusionneront si parfaitement que les différences
            seront négligeables"
            
  Does this reverse paraphrase ≈ the text? YES ✅
  
  STEP 2: MEANING PRESERVATION CHECK
  → If I replace the answer in the question, 
    does it make a true statement about the text?
    
  "La principale théorie de Kurzweil affirme que 
   les hommes et les outils technologiques ne feront 
   plus qu'un"
   
  Is this what the text says? YES ✅
  
  STEP 3: NOTHING-ADDED CHECK
  → Does the answer add information NOT in the text?
  
  "les hommes" → in text as "êtres humains" ✅
  "outils technologiques" → in text as "machines" ✅
  "ne feront plus qu'un" → in text as "fusionné/négligeables" ✅
  
  Nothing added that isn't in the text ✅
  
  ALL THREE STEPS PASS → VALID PARAPHRASE ✅

Now apply the same test to a WRONG answer:

THE SEMANTIC EQUIVALENCE TEST — OPTION D:

  STEP 1: REVERSE PARAPHRASE
  → "L'humanité utilisera la technologie pour améliorer 
     son patrimoine génétique"
  → REVERSE: Where in the text does it say humanity 
    will use technology for genetic improvement?
  → Paragraph 2: brain upload, fusion, intelligence expansion
    → NONE of these are about genetics
  → Paragraph 3: "génie génétique" listed as DEBATE TOPIC
    → NOT as Kurzweil's prediction
  → REVERSE FAILS — cannot map back to text ❌
  
  STEP 2: MEANING PRESERVATION CHECK
  → "La principale théorie de Kurzweil affirme que 
     l'humanité améliorera son patrimoine génétique"
  → Is this what the text says? NO ❌
  → The text never attributes genetic improvement to Kurzweil
  
  STEP 3: NOTHING-ADDED CHECK
  → "patrimoine génétique" as Kurzweil's prediction → ADDED ❌
  → "utilisera... pour améliorer" genetics → ADDED ❌
  → The answer ADDS a connection that doesn't exist
  
  TEST FAILS AT ALL THREE STEPS → NOT A VALID PARAPHRASE ❌
  → This is a DISTORTION (context transplant)

RULE 4: RECOGNIZE THE FOUR DISTORTION TYPES

Now that you know what valid paraphrases look like, learn to instantly spot their evil twins:

THE 4 DISTORTION TYPES:

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │                                                         │
  │  DISTORTION 1: INVERSION                                │
  │  Text says X → Answer says OPPOSITE of X                │
  │  "enhance" → "strip away"                               │
  │  "merge" → "separate"                                   │
  │  "grow" → "die out"                                     │
  │  DETECTION: Look for OPPOSITE verbs/adjectives          │
  │                                                         │
  │  DISTORTION 2: RELATIONSHIP CHANGE                      │
  │  Text says A=B → Answer says A>B or A<B                 │
  │  "merge equally" → "machines superior"                  │
  │  "collaborate" → "dominate"                             │
  │  DETECTION: Check if the RELATIONSHIP between           │
  │  elements is preserved (equal vs. hierarchical)         │
  │                                                         │
  │  DISTORTION 3: CONTEXT TRANSPLANT                       │
  │  Words from Section X placed in Section Y's context     │
  │  "génétique" from debate topics → prediction context    │
  │  DETECTION: Verify that each concept comes from         │
  │  the SAME section of the text, not mixed sections       │
  │                                                         │
  │  DISTORTION 4: SCOPE INFLATION                          │
  │  Text says "some/possible" → Answer says "all/certain"  │
  │  "pourrait" → "will definitely"                         │
  │  "certain aspects" → "everything"                       │
  │  DETECTION: Check quantifiers and modality words        │
  │                                                         │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Develop this reflex:

THE WORD-MATCH WARNING:

  When you see an answer option that uses 
  EXACT WORDS from the text:
  
  ╔════════════════════════════════════════════╗
  ║                                            ║

  ║                                            ║

❌ WHAT YOU SHOULD NOT DO

MISTAKE 1: WORD MATCHING AS A STRATEGY

WRONG STRATEGY:
  "I'll find the answer that uses the most words 
   from the text. That must be the correct one."
   
WHY IT FAILS:
  Q25 proof:
  → Option D uses 5 words from the text → WRONG ❌
  → Option A uses 0 words from the text → RIGHT ✅
  
  Word matching is not just unreliable —
  it is ANTI-CORRELATED with correctness.
  The more text words an answer uses, 
  the MORE SUSPICIOUS it should be.

MISTAKE 2: REJECTING UNFAMILIAR VOCABULARY IN ANSWERS

WRONG:
  "Option C for Q27 says 'métaphysiques.' 
   I don't know this word and it doesn't appear in the text.
   So this can't be the right answer."
   
RIGHT:
  "I don't know 'métaphysiques' — let me use Chapter 3's tools:
   
   Cognate: 'metaphysical' in English = 
   beyond physical reality, philosophical, about existence
   
   Context: The text mentions 'Dieu' (God) as a theme.
   God is a metaphysical concept by definition.
   
   So 'métaphysiques' = a valid abstraction of 'Dieu' 
   and other existential themes.
   
   This COULD be a valid paraphrase. Let me check further."
   
  RULE: Unknown vocabulary in an answer is NOT a reason 
  to eliminate it. Use Chapter 3's tools FIRST.

MISTAKE 3: ACCEPTING PARTIAL PARAPHRASES

WRONG:
  "Option A for Q26 says 'la vie éternelle et le bonheur de tous.'
   'Vie éternelle' IS a paraphrase of 'vivre éternellement.'
   So A must be correct!"
   
RIGHT:
  "'Vie éternelle' IS a valid paraphrase of 
   'vivre éternellement' ✅
   
   BUT 'bonheur de tous' has NO text equivalent.
   The text NEVER mentions happiness. ❌
   
   An answer must be a COMPLETE paraphrase.
   If HALF is a valid paraphrase and HALF is invented,
   the answer is WRONG."
   
  RULE: Every element of the answer must be paraphrasable 
  back to the text. One invented element = eliminated.

MISTAKE 4: CONFUSING PARAPHRASE WITH INFERENCE

PARAPHRASE:
  Text says X in words A.
  Answer says X in words B.
  SAME MEANING, DIFFERENT WORDS.
  
  Text: "fusionné... différences négligeables"
  Answer: "ne feront plus qu'un"
  → Same meaning ✅

INFERENCE:
  Text says X.
  Answer says Y, which LOGICALLY FOLLOWS from X 
  but is NOT the same statement.
  
  Text: "fusionné... différences négligeables"
  Answer: "les machines contrôleront les humains"
  → This does NOT logically follow from merging
  → Even if it did, it's an INFERENCE, not a paraphrase ❌

THE DISTINCTION:
  PARAPHRASE = restating what IS said
  INFERENCE = concluding what FOLLOWS from what is said
  
  Different question types test different skills:
  → "Le texte dit que..." → needs PARAPHRASE recognition
  → "On peut déduire que..." → needs INFERENCE skills
  
  Chapter 8 teaches PARAPHRASE.
  Chapter 11 will teach INFERENCE.
  Don't mix them.

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL 1: THE "TOO SIMPLE" DISMISSAL

DANGER: The correct paraphrase often sounds 
        SIMPLER than the text.
        
  TEXT:    "êtres humains et machines non biologiques 
            pourraient avoir si parfaitement fusionné que 
            les différences entre eux seraient devenues 
            négligeables"
            (Complex, conditional, multi-clause)
            
  ANSWER:  "ne feront plus qu'un"
            (Simple, short, direct)
            
  Student thinks: "The text is so complex and the answer 
  is so simple — they can't be saying the same thing."
  
  REALITY: Condensation is a VALID paraphrase mechanism.
  A 30-word complex sentence CAN mean the same thing 
  as a 5-word simple expression.
  
  RULE: Don't judge paraphrase validity by LENGTH or 
  COMPLEXITY. Judge it by MEANING.

PITFALL 2: THE "SOUNDS SMARTER" BIAS

DANGER: Students prefer answers that sound 
        intellectually sophisticated.
        
  OPTION A: "ne feront plus qu'un"
            (straightforward, conversational)
            
  OPTION B: "dépossédera l'homme de certaines 
             de ses qualités"
            (formal, philosophical-sounding)
            
  OPTION D: "améliorer son patrimoine génétique"
            (scientific, technical-sounding)
            
  Students often prefer B or D because they 
  "sound smarter" and more "exam-worthy."
  
  But the correct answer (A) is the simplest one.
  
  RULE: Correct answers can be simple.
  Wrong answers often sound impressive 
  PRECISELY to attract you.

PITFALL 3: THE SYNONYM CHAIN ERROR

DANGER: Creating a chain of synonyms that drifts 
        too far from the original meaning.
        
  VALID CHAIN:
  "fusionner" → "s'unir" → "ne faire qu'un"
  (merge → unite → become one)
  Each step preserves the core meaning ✅
  
  INVALID CHAIN:
  "fusionner" → "se rapprocher" → "collaborer" → "coexister"
  (merge → get closer → collaborate → coexist)
  Each step SLIGHTLY changes the meaning
  By the end, "coexist" ≠ "merge" ❌
  
  RULE: A valid synonym preserves the CORE meaning.
  If you need more than ONE synonym step, 
  verify the FINAL meaning against the ORIGINAL.
  
  "Fusionner" = become ONE thing (complete merger)
  "Coexister" = remain TWO things side by side
  These are DIFFERENT concepts despite the chain.

PITFALL 4: IGNORING THE NEGATIVE CONSTRUCTION

DANGER: Missing "ne...plus" or "ne...que" in answers.

  OPTION A: "ne feront PLUS QU'un"
  
  This contains TWO negative constructions:
  
  "ne...plus" = no longer / no more
  "ne...que" = only
  
  Combined: "ne feront plus qu'un"
  = "will no longer be anything but one"
  = "will be ONLY one"
  = "will become one single thing"
  
  A student who misses the double negative might read:
  "will do more than one" (OPPOSITE meaning!)
  
  RULE: French negative constructions can reverse 
  the entire meaning of a sentence. 
  Always identify ne...pas, ne...plus, ne...que, 
  ne...jamais, ne...rien, ne...aucun.
