🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

THE FORMAT REVOLUTION

Everything changes now. For the first time in 9 chapters, you are NOT answering questions about a text. You are BUILDING the text itself.

Let me show you the fundamental difference:

STANDARD CE (Chapters 1-8):
  Complete text → Read → Questions → Find answers IN the text
  YOU EXTRACT information FROM a finished text.

TEXTE À TROUS (Chapter 9+):
  Incomplete text → Read → Gaps → Choose sentences to COMPLETE the text
  YOU CONSTRUCT coherence BY inserting the right sentences.

This is like the difference between READING a completed puzzle and PLACING missing pieces into an incomplete puzzle. Both require understanding the picture, but the skills are different.

Why Is This Format Harder?

In standard CE, the answer is always IN the text — you just need to find it and match it to a paraphrase. In texte à trous, the answer must be DEDUCED from the SURROUNDING context. The correct sentence is the one that:

  1. Connects logically to what comes BEFORE the gap
  2. Connects logically to what comes AFTER the gap
  3. Maintains grammatical consistency (pronouns, verb tenses, subject agreement)
  4. Maintains thematic consistency (stays on topic)
  5. Maintains tonal consistency (same register, same attitude)

You must satisfy ALL FIVE simultaneously. In standard CE, you typically only need to match content. Here, you need content + grammar + logic + tone.

The Core Skill: BEFORE-GAP-AFTER Analysis

For every gap, you must analyze THREE elements:

┌──────────────┐     ┌─────────┐     ┌──────────────┐
│  BEFORE gap  │ ──→ │   GAP   │ ──→ │  AFTER gap   │
│  (context)   │     │ (insert)│     │  (context)   │
└──────────────┘     └─────────┘     └──────────────┘

The inserted sentence must:
  ← Flow FROM what comes before
  → Flow INTO what comes after

I call this the B.G.A. Method (Before-Gap-After):

STEP 1: Read the sentence BEFORE the gap carefully
STEP 2: Read the sentence AFTER the gap carefully
STEP 3: Identify the LOGICAL CONNECTION needed
         (Does "after" continue? contrast? explain? exemplify?)
STEP 4: Check each option for:
         a) Logical flow (does it bridge before → after?)
         b) Grammatical fit (pronouns, subjects, tenses)
         c) Thematic fit (stays on topic)
STEP 5: Eliminate options that break ANY of the three fits

The New Skill: CONNECTOR WORD READING

The sentences AROUND the gaps often contain connector words (mots de liaison) that TELL YOU what type of sentence belongs in the gap. These are your most powerful clues:

CONNECTOR AFTER THE GAP:
"C'est pourquoi..." (That's why...) 
  → The gap must contain a REASON/CAUSE
  → "C'est pourquoi" introduces a CONSEQUENCE of what precedes it
  → So the gap sentence must state something that CAUSES what follows

"En effet..." (Indeed...)
  → The gap must contain a CLAIM that "en effet" CONFIRMS
  → OR: the gap comes after a claim, and "en effet" starts the confirmation

"Ainsi..." (Thus/Therefore...)
  → The gap must contain something that leads to a CONCLUSION

"Cependant..." / "Pourtant..." (However...)
  → The gap must contain something that CONTRASTS with what follows

"De plus..." / "En outre..." (Moreover...)
  → The gap must ADD information to what precedes

Connector words are like road signs — they tell you which DIRECTION the text is going. If you see "C'est pourquoi" AFTER a gap, you know the gap must contain a CAUSE.

The New Skill: PRONOUN TRACKING

In French, pronouns must agree with their antecedents. If the sentence AFTER the gap uses "elle" (she/it feminine), the gap sentence must either:

  • Introduce a feminine noun that "elle" refers to, OR
  • Continue discussing a feminine noun already introduced before the gap

This creates GRAMMATICAL CONSTRAINTS that eliminate wrong options:

If AFTER says "elle est valable toute l'année..."
  → "elle" must refer to something FEMININE SINGULAR
  → The gap must introduce or reference a feminine singular noun
  → "La carte" = feminine singular ✓
  → "Le programme" = masculine singular ✗ (would need "il")

The New Skill: COLON/PUNCTUATION READING

French punctuation gives you critical clues:

"... réductions immédiates : …(gap)… Elles portent sur..."

The COLON (:) signals that what follows SPECIFIES or DETAILS what precedes.
"Réductions immédiates :" → the gap must SPECIFY the reductions
"Elles portent sur..." → "Elles" refers to the reductions (feminine plural)

So the gap must give DETAILS about the reductions, and "elles" 
in the next sentence must refer to those same reductions.

🧩 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

GAP 1: Between "student life is hard" and "C'est pourquoi... the card was created"

Step 1: Apply B.G.A. Method.

BEFORE the gap:

"La vie d'étudiant n'est pas toujours facile. Entre un budget limité et le travail à fournir, il n'est pas toujours évident de trouver les moyens nécessaires au quotidien en général."

Content: Student life is hard. Limited budget + heavy workload → hard to find resources for daily life.

AFTER the gap:

"C'est pourquoi l'association Echo... a conçu la CarteEtudiante.com."

Content: THAT'S WHY Echo created the student card.

Step 2: Identify the logical connection needed.

The CRITICAL connector is "C'est pourquoi" (that's why). This means:

[BEFORE gap] + [GAP] → C'est pourquoi → [card was created]

"C'est pourquoi" introduces a CONSEQUENCE.
Everything before it = the CAUSE/REASON.

So: [student life is hard] + [GAP] = the FULL REASON why the card was created.

The text BEFORE the gap talks about budget and workload making daily life hard. But the card offers reductions on formations, restauration, vêtements, voyages, divertissements — these are mostly LEISURE/LIFESTYLE items, not just academic necessities.

For the card's creation to make sense, the REASON must include the difficulty of affording LEISURE, not just daily necessities. The sentence before the gap mentions "moyens nécessaires au quotidien en général" — but doesn't specifically mention leisure/fun.

The gap needs to BRIDGE from "daily life is hard" to "that's why a card with leisure discounts was created." The missing link: leisure is also hard to manage.

Step 3: Check each option.

Option A — "Alors, les parents aident leurs enfants."

"Parents help their children" → This would be a SOLUTION, not a problem. But "C'est pourquoi" after the gap introduces ANOTHER solution (the card). If parents already help, why create a card?

More importantly, if parents help, the text would need to explain why their help is INSUFFICIENT before moving to the card. This sentence doesn't create that bridge.

Also, the rest of the text never mentions parents again. Introducing parents here creates an ORPHAN TOPIC — a subject raised but never continued.

❌ ELIMINATED — Introduces a solution before the problem is fully stated; creates an orphan topic (parents never mentioned again)

Option B — "On organise alors son temps au mieux."

"One organizes their time as best they can" → This is about TIME MANAGEMENT. But the card offers FINANCIAL discounts, not time management tools. If the gap says "students manage their time," the connection to "that's why a DISCOUNT CARD was created" doesn't make sense.

"Students manage their time well" → C'est pourquoi → a discount card was created
                                         ???
Time management ≠ need for financial discounts

The logical chain breaks. Time management doesn't lead to needing a discount card.

❌ ELIMINATED — Time management doesn't logically lead to the creation of a financial discount card

Option C — "Pourtant, les jeunes ne se découragent pas."

"However, young people don't get discouraged" → "Pourtant" (however) introduces a CONTRAST. This would contrast with the difficulty described before. But then "C'est pourquoi" after the gap introduces a CONSEQUENCE.

[Life is hard] → "Pourtant, they don't give up" → "C'est pourquoi a card was created"
                                                        ???
"They don't give up" → C'est pourquoi → card created?
Why would NOT giving up be the REASON a card was created?

The logical chain: "they don't give up → that's why a card was created" doesn't work. Not giving up isn't a CAUSE for creating a discount card. It would make more sense as: "Life is hard → that's why we need help → card created."

❌ ELIMINATED — "Not giving up" doesn't logically cause the creation of a discount card

Option D — "Il est difficile aussi de s'occuper de ses loisirs."

"It is also difficult to take care of one's leisure activities."

Let's test the logical chain:

[Budget limited + workload heavy → daily life hard]
  + [ALSO difficult to manage leisure]
  → C'est pourquoi → a card with leisure/lifestyle discounts was created

This works perfectly:

  1. "Aussi" (also) — ADDS to the problems already listed (budget + workload). It's an ADDITIONAL difficulty, not a contrast or solution.

  2. "Loisirs" (leisure) — BRIDGES to the card's offerings: the card gives discounts on "formations, restauration, vêtements, voyages et divertissements." "Divertissements" = entertainment/leisure. The card was created BECAUSE leisure is also hard to afford.

  3. Logical chain:

    • Life is hard (budget + workload)
    • Leisure is ALSO hard to manage
    • THAT'S WHY (c'est pourquoi) → the card was created (offering leisure discounts)

✅ CORRECT — "aussi" adds to the problems; "loisirs" bridges to the card's leisure-oriented discounts; the logical chain to "c'est pourquoi" is complete

✅ ANSWER: D

GAP 5: Between "safety charter with shuttles and first aid" and "Certaines pensent même à l'utiliser comme badge..."

"Certaines" — feminine plural pronoun. Who are "certaines"?

"Même" (even) — indicates ESCALATION. What comes after "même" goes BEYOND what precedes it. So the gap sentence describes something, and the AFTER sentence adds an EVEN FURTHER step.

GAP:   [Some institutional actors do something with the card]
AFTER: "Some EVEN think about using it as an access badge" (goes further)

✅ WHAT COULD BE DONE

The Master Strategy: B.G.A. + P.C.T. Method

For every gap in a texte à trous, apply this two-part method:

PART 1: B.G.A. (Before-Gap-After)
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ B — Read BEFORE the gap                 │
│     What topic/idea is being discussed? │
│     What logical direction is it going? │
│                                         │
│ G — Identify what the GAP needs         │
│     What logical role must it play?     │
│     (cause? detail? bridge? escalation?)│
│                                         │
│ A — Read AFTER the gap                  │
│     What connector word starts it?      │
│     What topic continues?               │
│     What pronouns need antecedents?     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘

PART 2: P.C.T. (Pronoun-Connector-Tone)
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ P — PRONOUN tracking                    │
│     Does the AFTER use "elle/elles/il/  │
│     ils/certains/celles-ci"?            │
│     → The gap must provide a matching   │
│       antecedent                        │
│                                         │
│ C — CONNECTOR reading                   │
│     Does the AFTER start with "c'est    │
│     pourquoi/en effet/pourtant/ainsi"?  │
│     → The gap must match the logical    │
│       relationship the connector demands│
│                                         │
│ T — TONE consistency                    │
│     Is the surrounding text positive/   │
│     negative/neutral?                   │
│     → The gap must match the tone       │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘

The Connector Dictionary

Build this reference for all future texte à trous:

CONNECTORS THAT SIGNAL CAUSE/CONSEQUENCE:
  "C'est pourquoi" → what precedes is the CAUSE
  "Ainsi" → what precedes leads to this CONCLUSION
  "Par conséquent" → therefore (consequence follows)
  "D'où" → hence (consequence follows)

CONNECTORS THAT SIGNAL ADDITION:
  "De plus" / "En outre" → adds MORE information
  "Aussi" / "Également" → also/likewise
  "De surcroît" → furthermore

CONNECTORS THAT SIGNAL CONTRAST:
  "Cependant" / "Pourtant" / "Toutefois" → however
  "Mais" → but
  "En revanche" → on the other hand
  "Néanmoins" → nevertheless

CONNECTORS THAT SIGNAL SPECIFICATION:
  "En effet" → indeed (confirms/specifies what precedes)
  ":" (colon) → what follows DETAILS what precedes
  "À savoir" → namely

CONNECTORS THAT SIGNAL ESCALATION:
  "Même" → even (goes further)
  "Voire" → or even (escalates)

The Gap Function Typology

Each gap serves a specific FUNCTION in the text. Identifying the function narrows your options:

GAP FUNCTION 1: ADDITION — Adds more to what precedes
  Signal: "aussi," "de plus," "en outre" nearby
  Example: Gap 1 ("Il est difficile AUSSI de...")

GAP FUNCTION 2: TRANSITION — Bridges from general to specific
  Signal: Move from claim to details, or from one topic to another
  Example: Gap 2 (general ally → "offers advantages" → specific features)

GAP FUNCTION 3: SPECIFICATION — Details what precedes
  Signal: Colon (:) before the gap, or "en effet" after
  Example: Gap 3 (reductions : → "10 to 50%" → domains listed)

GAP FUNCTION 4: BRIDGE — Connects two different domains/topics
  Signal: Topic shift between BEFORE and AFTER
  Example: Gap 4 (virtual website → physical events)

GAP FUNCTION 5: PREPARATION — Introduces elements needed AFTER
  Signal: Demonstratives or pronouns in AFTER need antecedents
  Example: Gap 5 (introduces "écoles et associations" → "certaines" refers to them)

✅ WHAT SHOULD YOU DO

DO #1: Read the ENTIRE text with gaps BEFORE solving any gap

Don't solve Gap 1 immediately. Read the WHOLE text first to understand:

  • The overall topic (student card)
  • The text's structure (problem → solution → features → community → safety → adoption)
  • The overall tone (positive, promotional)

This global understanding helps you evaluate each gap in context.

DO #2: Always check the sentence AFTER the gap FIRST

The sentence after the gap is often MORE constraining than the sentence before, because it contains:

  • Connector words that dictate logic
  • Pronouns that need antecedents
  • Demonstratives that need referents
PRIORITY ORDER:
  1. Read AFTER the gap (most constraining)
  2. Read BEFORE the gap (sets the topic)
  3. Identify the gap's function
  4. Check options against all three

DO #3: Use PRONOUN TRACKING as your primary elimination tool

Pronouns are MECHANICAL — they either match or they don't. No interpretation needed:

AFTER says "Elles portent sur..." → needs feminine plural antecedent
  Option A: "celles-ci" → pronoun, not a noun → "Elles" reference unclear ❌
  Option B: "elle lui permet" → singular → "Elles" has no antecedent ❌
  Option C: "remises" → feminine plural → "Elles" = "remises" ✓
  Option D: "délai" → masculine singular → "Elles" has no antecedent ❌

Pronoun tracking alone can eliminate 2-3 options instantly.

DO #4: Verify there are no CONTRADICTIONS with the rest of the text

If the text says the card is "valable toute l'année scolaire" and a gap option says "trois mois," that's a contradiction. Always cross-check gap options against information stated elsewhere in the text.

DO #5: Check for ORPHAN TOPICS

If a gap option introduces a subject (parents, surveys, insurance) that is NEVER mentioned again in the text, it's likely wrong. Good texts don't introduce topics and abandon them.

ORPHAN TOPIC TEST:
  "Does this option introduce something that appears NOWHERE ELSE in the text?"
  If YES → likely wrong (unless it's a closing statement)

❌ WHAT SHOULD YOU NOT DO

DON'T #1: Solve gaps based on CONTENT ALONE — check GRAMMAR too

WRONG APPROACH: "This sentence is about students and the text is about 
                students → it must be correct!"
RIGHT APPROACH: "This sentence uses 'il' but the AFTER uses 'elles' → 
                pronoun mismatch → eliminate regardless of content"

DON'T #2: Ignore connector words

"C'est pourquoi" AFTER the gap = the gap must contain a CAUSE
If you ignore this, you might insert a CONSEQUENCE instead of a CAUSE.

Connectors are NON-NEGOTIABLE constraints.

DON'T #3: Choose a sentence just because it sounds good in isolation

Every option will sound reasonable BY ITSELF. The question is whether it fits THE SPECIFIC GAP — before AND after. Test the FLOW, not the sentence alone.

Option B for Gap 2: "Une enquête conforte cette idée"
  → Sounds intelligent and reasonable by itself
  → But it doesn't bridge to the technical features that follow
  → DOESN'T FIT THIS GAP even though it's a good sentence

DON'T #4: Insert negative/critical sentences into positive texts (or vice versa)

The student card text is ENTIRELY positive and promotional. If an option introduces worry, criticism, or problems, it breaks the tone:

TEXT TONE: Positive (ally, advantages, reductions, safety, promotion)
Option A Gap 5: "suscite l'inquiétude" (worries) → TONE BREAK ❌

DON'T #5: Forget to check the DEMONSTRATIVE antecedent

"Ces manifestations," "cette idée," "ce projet" — demonstratives point to something JUST MENTIONED. If the gap doesn't mention events, "ces manifestations" has no referent.

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL 1: The Pronoun Mismatch Trap (NEW — Core concept of Chapter 9)

MECHANISM:  The gap option introduces a noun whose gender/number doesn't 
            match the pronoun in the AFTER sentence
EXAMPLE:    AFTER: "Elles portent sur..." (feminine plural)
            Gap option: "elle lui permet..." (introduces singular concepts)
            → "Elles" has no clear feminine plural antecedent
DEFENSE:    ALWAYS check pronoun gender and number between gap and AFTER.
            Feminine plural → gap must introduce/reference feminine plural noun.

PITFALL 2: The Orphan Topic Trap (NEW)

MECHANISM:  The gap option introduces a subject never mentioned elsewhere
EXAMPLE:    "Une enquête sur leurs attentes..." → surveys never mentioned again
            "Une assurance est proposée..." → insurance never mentioned again
DEFENSE:    Ask: "Does this topic appear ANYWHERE else in the text?"
            If not → likely wrong (orphan topic)

PITFALL 3: The Connector Ignorance Trap (NEW)

MECHANISM:  Ignoring a connector word that dictates the logical relationship
EXAMPLE:    "C'est pourquoi" AFTER the gap requires the gap to contain a CAUSE
            Inserting a CONTRAST or SOLUTION instead of a CAUSE → breaks logic
DEFENSE:    Identify ALL connector words near each gap.
            Match the gap's content to the connector's logical demand.

PITFALL 4: The Tone Break Trap (NEW)

MECHANISM:  Inserting a negative/worried sentence into a positive text
EXAMPLE:    Entire text is promotional/positive about the student card
            Gap option: "suscite l'inquiétude des municipalités" (worry)
            → Breaks the consistently positive tone
DEFENSE:    Identify the text's overall tone before solving gaps.
            Each gap sentence must match this tone.

PITFALL 5: The Missing Antecedent Trap (NEW)

MECHANISM:  The AFTER sentence uses a demonstrative ("ces," "cette") 
            that needs a referent, but the gap option doesn't provide one
EXAMPLE:    AFTER: "ces manifestations" (these events)
            Gap option about website registration → no events introduced
            → "ces manifestations" has no referent
DEFENSE:    When AFTER uses demonstratives, the gap MUST introduce 
            the noun they point to.

PITFALL 6: The Contradiction Trap (NEW)

MECHANISM:  The gap option contradicts information stated elsewhere in the text
EXAMPLE:    Text says card is "valable toute l'année scolaire"
            Gap option: "un délai de trois mois" → contradicts yearly validity
DEFENSE:    Cross-check every gap option against ALL stated facts in the text.
