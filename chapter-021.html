🧠 HOW TO APPROACH IT

What makes this article DIFFERENT from Chapter 20

In Chapter 20 (French Mobile Phones), you learned the Skeleton Method for enumerated sociological arguments with visible markers (premièrement, ensuite, de plus, enfin). That text had a clean, labeled skeleton.

This text is deceptively different. It looks simple — just practical laundry advice — but it introduces THREE new challenges:

Challenge 1: The Tone Pivot
The text starts POSITIVE ("nobody would question its usefulness"), then PIVOTS to criticism, then PIVOTS AGAIN to practical tips. You must track where you are on this emotional rollercoaster.

Challenge 2: Connectors That Look Alike But Point in Opposite Directions
This is the central lesson of this chapter. Look at the connectors in the answer choices:

ConnectorDirectionMeaning
Cependant↩️ CONTRASTHowever
Néanmoins↩️ CONTRASTNevertheless
De même➡️ CONTINUATIONSimilarly / Likewise
En revanche↩️ CONTRASTOn the other hand
Pourtant↩️ CONTRASTYet / However
C'est pourquoi➡️ CONSEQUENCEThat's why
D'ailleurs➡️ ADDITIONBesides / Moreover
Quant à🔄 TOPIC SHIFTAs for / Regarding

Choosing the wrong connector = choosing the wrong answer, even if the content seems right.

Challenge 3: The "Celles" Pronoun Trap
Gap 5 connects to a sentence containing "privilégiez celles" (prefer those — feminine plural). Your gap answer MUST introduce a feminine plural noun for "celles" to refer to. This is the Pronoun Anchor Test from Chapter 18, but now applied to GENDER and NUMBER agreement.

✅ HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

QUESTION 1 — The Tone Pivot Gap

What comes BEFORE:

"Aujourd'hui, le lave-linge s'est installé dans toutes les familles et personne n'oserait contester son utilité."

Translation: The washing machine is in every home and nobody would question its usefulness.

Tone: 100% POSITIVE. Universal acceptance. No criticism.

What comes AFTER:

"Ainsi en est-il des machines à laver sèche-linge, grosses consommatrices d'électricité et très peu performantes..."

Translation: Such is the case with washer-dryers, which consume lots of electricity and are very inefficient...

Tone: NEGATIVE. Criticism of a specific product.

The critical expression: "Ainsi en est-il de..."

This expression means "Such is the case with..." or "This applies to..." — it provides a CONCRETE EXAMPLE of whatever was stated in the gap. Whatever the gap says, the washer-dryer is an EXAMPLE of it.

BEFORE: Washing machines are great [POSITIVE]
GAP 1 = ??? [must PIVOT from positive to negative]
AFTER: "Such is the case with" washer-dryers [NEGATIVE EXAMPLE]

The gap MUST:

  1. Contain a contrast connector (to pivot from positive to negative)
  2. Introduce a general negative statement (that washer-dryers exemplify)
ChoiceConnectorDirectionContentFits?
ANéanmoins (nevertheless)↩️ CONTRAST"some professionals warn us"Contrast works ✅ but "Ainsi en est-il" would mean washer-dryers are an example of "professionals warning us" — awkward ⚠️
BD'ailleurs (besides/moreover)➡️ CONTINUATION"the latest innovations prove it"Continues the POSITIVE tone. But the AFTER sentence is NEGATIVE. Direction clash ❌
CTout d'abord (first of all)➡️ ENUMERATION"no novelty on the market is criticized"Says NOTHING is criticized. But the AFTER sentence CRITICIZES washer-dryers! Direct contradiction ❌
DCependant (however)↩️ CONTRAST"some 'innovations' leave one perplexed"Contrast pivots from positive to negative ✅ AND "Ainsi en est-il" gives washer-dryers as an EXAMPLE of perplexing innovations ✅

Deep comparison: A vs. D

Both A and D have contrast connectors. The difference is in how they connect to "Ainsi en est-il":

With A: "Professionals warn us. SUCH IS THE CASE WITH washer-dryers..."
→ Washer-dryers are an example of professionals warning us?
→ Grammatically possible but semantically awkward.
→ The subject shifts from PEOPLE (professionals) to PRODUCTS (washer-dryers).

With D: "Some 'innovations' leave one perplexed. SUCH IS THE CASE WITH washer-dryers..."
→ Washer-dryers are an example of perplexing innovations.
→ PRODUCT → PRODUCT. Same semantic category. Clean reference.

Also notice the quotation marks around "innovations" in choice D. These quotation marks signal irony — the author is suggesting these aren't REAL innovations, they're questionable products. This ironic tone perfectly matches the criticism that follows.

Let's verify the complete flow with D inserted:

"Personne n'oserait contester son utilité. Cependant, quelques "innovations" laissent perplexe. Ainsi en est-il des machines à laver sèche-linge, grosses consommatrices d'électricité et très peu performantes..."

Translation: Nobody would question its usefulness. However, some "innovations" leave one perplexed. Such is the case with washer-dryers, heavy energy consumers and very inefficient...

✅ Positive → Pivot → Negative example. Flawless.

✅ Answer 1: D

🔧 NEW Technique: THE "AINSI EN EST-IL" TEST
When you see "Ainsi en est-il de X" after a gap, the gap must state a GENERAL claim that X is a SPECIFIC EXAMPLE of. The gap's subject category must match X's category (product→product, person→person, idea→idea).

QUESTION 2 — The Continuation-of-Criticism Gap

What comes BEFORE:

"...grosses consommatrices d'électricité et très peu performantes puisqu'il est souvent nécessaire de renouveler le cycle de séchage."

Washer-dryers: energy-hungry, inefficient, need repeated cycles.

What comes AFTER:

"Les lessives modernes et les nouvelles techniques de lavage suffisent à vous procurer un linge parfaitement propre et vous épargneront 15% d'énergie."

Modern detergents and new techniques suffice for perfectly clean laundry + save 15% energy.

The logical bridge:

BEFORE: Washer-dryers are wasteful [CRITICISM of product 1]
GAP 2 = ??? 
AFTER: Modern detergents suffice and save 15% energy [POSITIVE ALTERNATIVE]

The gap sits between TWO pieces of criticism and a positive solution. It must either:

  • CONTINUE the criticism (adding another wasteful feature), OR
  • TRANSITION to the positive alternative

The AFTER sentence starts with a flat statement ("Les lessives modernes...") without a contrast connector. This means the gap itself must complete the critical section, and the AFTER sentence simply offers the alternative.

The key question: What direction does the gap point?

ChoiceConnectorDirectionContentAnalysis
AEn revanche (on the other hand)↩️ CONTRAST"the pre-wash function is rather useful"Says pre-wash IS useful. This reverses the critical tone and introduces a POSITIVE statement. But the text is still in its criticism phase. Premature positivity.
BC'est pourquoi (that's why)➡️ CONSEQUENCE"most functions are necessary"Says most functions ARE necessary. But the whole paragraph argues that some functions are WASTEFUL. This contradicts the argument.
CDe même (similarly/likewise)➡️ CONTINUATION"the pre-wash function is almost always superfluous"Continues the criticism: washer-dryers are wasteful, SIMILARLY pre-wash is superfluous. Then: "Modern detergents suffice" = you don't NEED pre-wash.
DPourtant (yet/however)↩️ CONTRAST"this function turns out to be generally economical"Says the function IS economical. "Pourtant" would contrast with the criticism, but then the AFTER sentence about modern detergents being sufficient wouldn't follow logically.

Why C is the only answer that works:

Let's test the FLOW with each viable choice:

With A (En revanche, pre-wash is useful):
  Washer-dryers are wasteful. ON THE OTHER HAND, pre-wash is useful.
  Modern detergents suffice and save 15% energy.
  → If pre-wash is useful, why does the next sentence say detergents SUFFICE?
  → Contradiction: "useful" then "you don't need it" ❌

With C (De même, pre-wash is superfluous):
  Washer-dryers are wasteful. SIMILARLY, pre-wash is superfluous.
  Modern detergents suffice and save 15% energy.
  → Pre-wash is superfluous BECAUSE modern detergents suffice.
  → Perfect logical chain ✅

With D (Pourtant, this function is economical):
  Washer-dryers are wasteful. YET, this function is economical.
  Modern detergents suffice and save 15% energy.
  → If the function is economical, why switch to detergents?
  → Incoherent motivation ❌

✅ Answer 2: C

🔧 NEW Technique: THE DIRECTION CHAIN TEST

Read three consecutive elements — BEFORE, GAP, AFTER — and check if the DIRECTION is consistent:

BEFORE (−) → GAP (−) → AFTER (+) = Criticism block then solution ✅
BEFORE (−) → GAP (+) → AFTER (+) = Premature reversal, suspicious ⚠️
BEFORE (−) → GAP (+) → AFTER (−) = Zigzag, almost always wrong ❌

"De même" (similarly) continues the SAME direction. That's why it works here — it extends the criticism before the text pivots to the positive alternative.

QUESTION 3 — The Section Opener Gap

What comes BEFORE:

"...vous épargneront 15% d'énergie."

End of the criticism/alternative section. The text has finished explaining what's wasteful and what's sufficient.

What comes AFTER:

"Pensez par exemple à remplir toujours complètement vos machines sinon utilisez la touche demi-charge,"

Translation: Think for example about always filling your machines completely, otherwise use the half-load button,

The critical clue: "par exemple"

"Par exemple" (for example) means the AFTER sentence is giving a SPECIFIC EXAMPLE. Therefore, the gap must introduce the GENERAL CATEGORY that this example belongs to.

GAP 3 = [general category of tips/tricks]
AFTER: "Think FOR EXAMPLE about filling your machines..." = first specific tip

B"Little tricks can also reduce your laundry budget"✅ Introduces the CATEGORY of "little tricks""Think for example about filling your machines" = a first trick. Perfect flow!✅ CORRECT

"...vous épargneront 15% d'énergie. Des petits trucs peuvent aussi réduire votre budget linge. Pensez par exemple à remplir toujours complètement vos machines..."

Translation: ...will save you 15% energy. Little tricks can also reduce your laundry budget. Think for example about always filling your machines...

✅ General category → "for example" → specific tip. Classic advice-text structure.

✅ Answer 3: B

🔧 Technique reinforced: THE "PAR EXEMPLE" RULE
Whenever you see "par exemple" in the sentence AFTER a gap, the gap MUST introduce a GENERAL statement that the example illustrates. The example must be a SUBSET of the gap's claim.

GAP: [general category]
AFTER: "par exemple" + [specific instance of that category]

This rule was implicit in Chapter 20's Skeleton Method. Here it becomes EXPLICIT.

QUESTION 4 — The Benefit-of-a-Tip Gap

What comes BEFORE:

"Pensez par exemple à remplir toujours complètement vos machines sinon utilisez la touche demi-charge**,**"

Note the comma — the sentence is NOT finished. Gap 4 completes this sentence.

Translation: Think about always filling your machines completely, otherwise use the half-load button, ...(4)...

What comes AFTER:

"Il existe aussi des petites balles en caoutchouc..."

"Il existe aussi" = "There are also" — this starts a NEW tip. So Gap 4 must FINISH the current tip before the text moves on.

TIP 1: Fill machines completely OR use half-load, GAP 4 [= RESULT/BENEFIT]
TIP 2: "There are also rubber balls..."

The gap must give the BENEFIT or RESULT of following Tip 1.

ChoiceConnector/LogicContentFits as benefit?Verdict
Aainsi (thus)"you will save 50% of water"RESULT connector + concrete saving. Logical: half-load uses less water.✅ CORRECT
Bmais (but)"wait before starting the machine"Introduces a caveat. But why wait? No reason given. Feels disconnected.❌ Unmotivated
Cet (and)"you will use a little more water"Using MORE water contradicts the money-saving advice! The whole section is about REDUCING costs.❌ Contradicts purpose
Dafin de (in order to)"better wash the laundry"Purpose connector, but the point of filling completely is to SAVE resources, not to wash better.❌ Wrong purpose

Why C is a particularly clever trap:

Choice C says "et vous utiliserez un peu plus d'eau" — a student might think "half-load uses a bit more water than necessary compared to a full load" (which is technically true — a half-load doesn't use exactly half the water). But in the context of ADVICE about saving money, telling someone "you'll use more water" defeats the purpose. The text is giving POSITIVE results, not warning about drawbacks.

Let's verify:

"Pensez par exemple à remplir toujours complètement vos machines sinon utilisez la touche demi-charge, vous économiserez ainsi 50% d'eau. Il existe aussi des petites balles en caoutchouc..."

Translation: Think about always filling your machines completely, otherwise use the half-load button, thus saving 50% of water. There are also rubber balls...

✅ Tip → Benefit → Next tip. Clean advice structure.

✅ Answer 4: A

QUESTION 5 — The Topic Shift Gap (The Pronoun Gender Trap)

This is the most sophisticated question in the article. It tests three skills simultaneously.

What comes BEFORE:

"Il existe aussi des petites balles en caoutchouc qui reproduisent l'effet de l'ancien battoir à linge que nos grand-mères utilisaient."

About rubber balls that reproduce the effect of the old laundry beater.

What comes AFTER the gap:

"privilégiez celles ne contenant pas de phosphates, qui asphyxient les cours d'eau par la prolifération d'algues."

Translation: prefer those (feminine plural) not containing phosphates, which suffocate waterways through algae proliferation.

THE THREE TESTS FOR THIS GAP:

Test 1: What does "celles" refer to?

"Celles" is a demonstrative pronoun — feminine plural. It MUST refer to a feminine plural noun introduced in the gap.

ChoiceNoun introducedGender/NumberCan "celles" refer to it?
ANo specific noun (just "restez vigilants")—❌ No antecedent
B"les machines à laver"Feminine plural ✅Technically yes, but do machines contain phosphates? ⚠️
C"celles-ci" (refers back to rubber balls)Feminine plural ✅But "celles-ci sont recyclées" then "privilégiez celles" = confusing double pronoun
D"les lessives" (detergents)Feminine plural ✅Do detergents contain phosphates? YES! ✅

Test 2: Semantic coherence — what contains phosphates?

The AFTER sentence says "prefer those not containing phosphates." What products contain phosphates?

ProductContains phosphates?Makes sense?
Machines à laver (washing machines)No — machines don't contain phosphates❌
Balles en caoutchouc (rubber balls)No — rubber balls don't contain phosphates❌
Lessives (detergents)YES — detergents historically contain phosphates✅

Only lessives can logically be followed by "prefer those without phosphates."

Test 3: The connector "Quant à/aux"

"Quant aux lessives" = "As for detergents" — this is a TOPIC SHIFT connector. It signals: "We've been talking about machines and accessories. Now let's talk about a DIFFERENT but related topic: detergents."

This perfectly matches the text's progression:

Paragraph 2 progression:
  → Tip 1: Fill machines completely (about the MACHINE)
  → Tip 2: Use rubber balls (about an ACCESSORY)
  → Tip 3: "As for DETERGENTS..." (about the PRODUCT)
  → Final: prefer phosphate-free ones

The text moves from machine → accessory → product. "Quant aux" signals this final shift.

Let's eliminate the other choices definitively:

ChoiceWhy it fails
A "Mais restez vigilants,"No noun for "celles" to refer to. After "stay vigilant," the text would need to specify ABOUT WHAT.
B "Bref pour les machines à laver,""Bref" (in short) signals a CONCLUSION. But the text isn't concluding — it's adding a final tip. Also, machines don't contain phosphates.
C "En effet celles-ci sont recyclées,"Creates a confusing chain: "celles-ci" (these) then "celles" (those) — two demonstrative pronouns in a row referring to different things. Also, rubber balls being "recycled" has nothing to do with phosphates.
D "Quant aux lessives,"Topic shift to detergents ✅ "Celles" = lessives ✅ Detergents contain phosphates ✅

Let's verify the complete flow with D inserted:

"Il existe aussi des petites balles en caoutchouc qui reproduisent l'effet de l'ancien battoir à linge que nos grand-mères utilisaient. Quant aux lessives, privilégiez celles ne contenant pas de phosphates, qui asphyxient les cours d'eau par la prolifération d'algues."

Translation: There are also rubber balls that reproduce the effect of the old laundry beater that our grandmothers used. As for detergents, prefer those not containing phosphates, which suffocate waterways through algae proliferation.

✅ Topic shift → Specific advice → Environmental justification. Flawless.

✅ Answer 5: D

🔧 NEW Technique: THE TRIPLE LOCK TEST
For the hardest gaps, THREE conditions must be satisfied simultaneously:

LOCK 1: PRONOUN AGREEMENT — Does the gap provide the correct 
        gender/number antecedent for pronouns in the AFTER sentence?

LOCK 2: SEMANTIC COHERENCE — Does the gap's topic make sense 
        with the AFTER sentence's specific claims?

LOCK 3: CONNECTOR LOGIC — Does the gap's connector match 
        its structural position (continuation? contrast? topic shift?)

All three locks must open. If even ONE fails, the answer is wrong.

🎯 WHAT COULD BE DONE (Strategic Options for Advice Texts)

Advice texts follow a predictable progression. Recognize the pattern:

PATTERN 1: Problem → Solution → Tips
  (This text: wasteful features → modern alternatives → practical tricks)

PATTERN 2: General rule → Exceptions → Warnings
  (Common in health/diet articles)

PATTERN 3: Common belief → Correction → Better practice
  (Common in myth-busting articles)

This text follows Pattern 1. Once you identify the pattern, you know:

  • Early gaps are in the PROBLEM zone (need critical tone)
  • Middle gaps are in the SOLUTION zone (need transition to positive)
  • Late gaps are in the TIPS zone (need practical, concrete content)

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL #1: The "Both Contrast Connectors" Confusion 🔄

Gap 1 choices include:
  A: "Néanmoins" (nevertheless)
  D: "Cependant" (however)
Both are contrast connectors! Which one?

When two choices have the SAME type of connector, the content decides:

  • A's content (professionals warn us) → subject is PEOPLE
  • D's content (innovations leave one perplexed) → subject is PRODUCTS

The AFTER sentence ("Ainsi en est-il des machines...") gives a PRODUCT example. So the gap must talk about PRODUCTS, not people. → D wins.

Rule: When connectors match, the SEMANTIC OWNERSHIP test (Chapter 20) breaks the tie.

PITFALL #2: The "Technically True" Trap 🎭

Gap 4, Choice C: "you will use a little more water"

This is TECHNICALLY TRUE — a half-load cycle doesn't use exactly half the water of a full cycle. But in a text about SAVING money and resources, telling the reader they'll use MORE water is counterproductive. The text's PURPOSE overrides technical accuracy.

Rule: In advice texts, the gap must serve the text's PURPOSE (helping the reader save), not just state a neutral fact.

PITFALL #3: The Double-Pronoun Collision 💥

Gap 5, Choice C: "En effet celles-ci sont recyclées,"
AFTER: "privilégiez celles ne contenant pas de phosphates"

"Celles-ci" in the gap + "celles" in the AFTER = two demonstrative pronouns in consecutive sentences referring to DIFFERENT things. This creates confusion:

  • "Celles-ci" would refer to rubber balls
  • "Celles" would refer to... also rubber balls? But rubber balls don't contain phosphates!

Rule: Avoid answers that create pronoun ambiguity chains. Each pronoun must have ONE clear, unambiguous antecedent.

PITFALL #4: The "Wrong Phase" Error 📍

Gap 3, Choice D: "it's difficult to save more"
AFTER: "Think for example about filling your machines"

Choice D says saving is DIFFICULT. But the text is about to give EASY tips. This answer belongs in a CONCLUSION phase ("we've done all we can"), not in a TIPS-INTRODUCTION phase.

Rule: Every gap lives in a specific PHASE of the text's progression. An answer from the wrong phase — even if well-written — is wrong.

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY

What Chapter 21 taught you — FOUR NEW TECHNIQUES:

TECHNIQUE #1: THE DIRECTION CHAIN TEST
  → Read BEFORE, GAP, and AFTER as a three-part sequence
  → Check if the DIRECTION (positive/negative) flows logically
  → (−)(−)(+) is valid; (−)(+)(−) is almost always wrong
  → A connector's direction must match its position in the chain

TECHNIQUE #2: THE "AINSI EN EST-IL" TEST  
  → When "Ainsi en est-il de X" follows a gap, the gap must be a 
    GENERAL claim that X is a SPECIFIC EXAMPLE of
  → The gap's subject category must match X's category

TECHNIQUE #3: THE TRIPLE LOCK TEST
  → For the hardest gaps, THREE conditions must ALL be met:
     Lock 1: Pronoun gender/number agreement
     Lock 2: Semantic coherence (topic makes sense)
     Lock 3: Connector logic (right structural position)
  → If even ONE lock fails, eliminate the answer

TECHNIQUE #4: ADVICE-TEXT PROGRESSION MAP
  → Identify which PHASE the gap is in:
     Problem zone → critical tone
     Solution zone → transitional tone  
     Tips zone → practical, concrete tone
  → An answer from the wrong phase is always wrong
