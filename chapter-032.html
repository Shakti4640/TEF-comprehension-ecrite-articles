Mastering the Texte à Trous (I) — Filling Gaps Through Logical Coherence

📄 COMPLETE ARTICLE

Texte 52 : Bien recycler pour limiter les déchets (texte à trous)

On appelle ça la « responsabilité élargie des producteurs ». Le principe est simple : un industriel ou un distributeur qui vend un produit à son client doit le reprendre quand il est usé ou cassé afin de le recycler. ...(16)... C'est ainsi qu'ont été récoltés l'an dernier en Belgique plus de 800 millions d'euros.

La méthode fait florès avec aujourd'hui une dizaine de filières en activité. ...(17)... Les lampes et les textiles utilisent, eux, l'apport volontaire en magasin.

Des efforts restent à faire pour réduire drastiquement le volume de déchets. Chaque Belge produit 590 kilos d'ordures ménagères par an, soit deux fois plus qu'il y a quarante ans. ...(18)... Il s'agit de boire l'eau du robinet, de réparer ses appareils électriques, d'opter pour les piles rechargeables... Et enfin, donner les objets dont on n'a plus besoin pour qu'ils soient réutilisés.

Questions:

Q16:

  • A — Le principe sera mis en place d'ici la fin de l'année.
  • B — Le recyclage ne rapporte pas d'argent pour le moment.
  • C — Ainsi, elle ne finit pas dans une poubelle ou dans une déchèterie.
  • D — L'opération est financée par une taxe incluse dans le prix d'achat.

Q17:

  • A — Les tissus et les éclairages ne sont pas encore recyclés.
  • B — Tous les objets sans exception sont collectés à domicile.
  • C — Les appareils sont repris lors de la livraison d'un appareil neuf.
  • D — Les consommateurs peuvent déposer leurs appareils usés chez le vendeur.

Q18:

  • A — Aussi, les gestes les plus vertueux consistent à ne pas en produire.
  • B — Seules des décisions prises en haut lieu pourraient inverser la tendance.
  • C — En cause, les habitudes néfastes de consommation des pays industrialisés.
  • D — Et malheureusement aucun indicateur ne donne l'espoir d'un changement.

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH THIS TEXT

The Format Changes EVERYTHING

In Chapters 1–5, you read a complete text and answered questions about it. The text was finished; your job was to understand it. This chapter reverses the process entirely.

CHAPTERS 1-5 (Standard Questions):
  COMPLETE TEXT → Read → Questions ask ABOUT the text → Find answers IN the text

CHAPTER 6 (Texte à Trous):
  INCOMPLETE TEXT → Read → Questions ask you to COMPLETE the text → Build the text

You are no longer an analyst. You are a reconstructor. The text has holes, and you must fill them with the sentence that makes the text whole again.

Step 1: Recognize the Format

A texte à trous has these distinctive features:

FeatureWhat You See
Numbered gaps...(16)..., ...(17)..., ...(18)...
Options are full sentencesNot single words or short phrases — entire sentences
Each gap has its own set of options4 options per gap, independent of other gaps
The surrounding text provides all cluesWhat comes BEFORE and AFTER the gap tells you what's missing

Step 2: Understand the Three Tests Every Gap-Filler Must Pass

A correct sentence must pass ALL THREE of these tests simultaneously:

TEST 1: LOGICAL COHERENCE
  Does the sentence make logical sense between what comes before and after?
  Does it create a cause-effect, contrast, or continuation that flows?

TEST 2: THEMATIC COHERENCE  
  Does the sentence stay on the same topic as the surrounding text?
  Does it introduce information consistent with the paragraph's subject?

TEST 3: GRAMMATICAL/REFERENCE COHERENCE
  Do pronouns in the sentence match their antecedents?
  Do connectors ("ainsi," "eux," "c'est pourquoi") point in the right direction?
  Does the verb tense match the surrounding text?

If a sentence fails even ONE test, it's wrong — even if it passes the other two.

Step 3: Map the Text Architecture (With Gaps)

Apply the mapping skill (→ Project 1), but now mark each gap's position within the logical flow:

PARAGRAPH 1: THE PRINCIPLE + ITS FINANCIAL MECHANISM
    - "Responsabilité élargie des producteurs"
    - Producers must take back used/broken products to recycle
    - GAP 16 → [something about money/financing]
    - "C'est ainsi" → 800 million euros collected in Belgium

PARAGRAPH 2: THE METHOD IN PRACTICE
    - Method is flourishing, ~10 active sectors
    - GAP 17 → [something about how products are collected]
    - "Les lampes et textiles, EUX" → use voluntary drop-off in stores

PARAGRAPH 3: WHAT REMAINS TO BE DONE
    - Efforts still needed to reduce waste volume
    - 590 kg per Belgian per year, double from 40 years ago
    - GAP 18 → [something introducing solutions]
    - "Il s'agit de" → drink tap water, repair appliances, rechargeable batteries...

Notice: Each gap sits between two known sentences. The sentences BEFORE and AFTER the gap are your anchor points. The correct filler must connect both anchors seamlessly.

Step 4: Master the "Sandwich Method"

This is the core technique for texte à trous. Every gap is a sandwich:

BREAD (TOP):     Sentence BEFORE the gap
FILLING:         The missing sentence (what you must find)
BREAD (BOTTOM):  Sentence AFTER the gap

The filling must taste right with BOTH pieces of bread.

For each gap:

  1. Read the sentence BEFORE carefully
  2. Read the sentence AFTER carefully
  3. Ask: "What BRIDGE would connect these two?"
  4. Test each option as a potential bridge

🧩 HOW TO SOLVE EACH QUESTION

GAP 16: Between the recycling principle and the 800 million euros

Step 1: Identify the sandwich

TOP BREAD:    "...un industriel ou un distributeur qui vend un produit à son 
               client doit le reprendre quand il est usé ou cassé afin de le recycler."
               
FILLING:       GAP 16 = ???

BOTTOM BREAD: "C'est ainsi qu'ont été récoltés l'an dernier en Belgique 
               plus de 800 millions d'euros."

Step 2: Analyze the bottom bread for clues

The sentence AFTER the gap is crucial. It starts with "C'est ainsi" (This is how / In this way). This connector points BACKWARD — it refers to something just stated. Whatever fills the gap must describe a mechanism that explains HOW 800 million euros were collected.

Ask yourself: what kind of sentence would make "C'est ainsi qu'ont été récoltés... 800 millions" a logical continuation?

The sentence needs to describe a financial mechanism — something that generates money in the context of recycling.

Step 3: Test each option using the three tests

Option A: "Le principe sera mis en place d'ici la fin de l'année."
(The principle will be implemented by the end of the year.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical❌ FAILIf the principle hasn't been implemented yet (future tense: "sera"), how were 800 million euros ALREADY collected ("ont été récoltés" — past tense)? The future contradicts the past.
Thematic≈ PartialIt's about the same principle, so thematically close.
Grammatical❌ FAILTense mismatch: "sera" (future) → "C'est ainsi qu'ont été récoltés" (past). The system can't collect money in the past from a principle not yet implemented.

Verdict: ❌ ELIMINATED — temporal contradiction

Option B: "Le recyclage ne rapporte pas d'argent pour le moment."
(Recycling doesn't bring in money for now.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical❌ FAIL"Doesn't bring in money" directly followed by "800 million euros were collected" — this is a flat contradiction. You can't say "no money" and then say "800 million."
Thematic✅ PASSIt's about recycling and money — same theme.
Grammatical✅ PASSPresent tense is compatible.

Verdict: ❌ ELIMINATED — direct logical contradiction with bottom bread

Option C: "Ainsi, elle ne finit pas dans une poubelle ou dans une déchèterie."
(Thus, it doesn't end up in a trash can or a dump.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical❌ FAILThis sentence talks about waste disposal — where things DON'T end up. The bottom bread talks about money collection. There's no logical bridge from "not ending in a dump" to "800 million euros collected."
Thematic≈ PartialRelated to recycling, but shifts to physical waste, not finance.
Grammatical❌ FAILCritical: "elle" (she/it, feminine) — what does it refer to? The last noun before the gap is "produit" (product), which is MASCULINE. "Elle" has no valid feminine antecedent. This is a pronoun mismatch.

Verdict: ❌ ELIMINATED — pronoun mismatch + no logical bridge to money

Option D: "L'opération est financée par une taxe incluse dans le prix d'achat."
(The operation is financed by a tax included in the purchase price.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical✅ PASSA tax in the purchase price → money is collected → "C'est ainsi qu'ont été récoltés 800 millions." The tax IS the mechanism that generates the 800 million. Perfect causal bridge.
Thematic✅ PASSStays on topic: recycling system → how it's funded → how much it generates.
Grammatical✅ PASSPresent tense ("est financée") describes an ongoing mechanism. "C'est ainsi" naturally follows a description of a method.

Verdict: ✅ PASSES ALL THREE TESTS

✅ ANSWER: D — L'opération est financée par une taxe incluse dans le prix d'achat.

The logical flow with D inserted:

Producers must take back products to recycle them.
→ This operation is financed by a tax in the purchase price.
→ This is how 800 million euros were collected last year in Belgium.

CAUSE:  Tax in purchase price
BRIDGE: "C'est ainsi" (this is how)
EFFECT: 800 million collected

GAP 17: Between the flourishing method and lamps/textiles

Step 1: Identify the sandwich

TOP BREAD:    "La méthode fait florès avec aujourd'hui une dizaine de 
               filières en activité."

FILLING:       GAP 17 = ???

BOTTOM BREAD: "Les lampes et les textiles utilisent, EUX, l'apport 
               volontaire en magasin."

Step 2: Analyze the bottom bread for clues

This sentence contains a critical grammatical signal: the word "eux" (them, for their part). In French, "eux" placed between commas like this creates an emphatic contrast:

"Les lampes et les textiles utilisent, EUX, l'apport volontaire en magasin."
= "Lamps and textiles, FOR THEIR PART, use voluntary drop-off in stores."

"Eux" signals that lamps and textiles do something DIFFERENT from what was just described. Whatever fills the gap must describe a different collection method for different products. Then the bottom bread contrasts by saying "lamps and textiles, THEY use a different approach."

This is a contrast structure:

GAP 17:       [Product type A] uses [Collection method 1]
BOTTOM BREAD: [Product type B], for their part, uses [Collection method 2]

Step 3: Test each option using the three tests

Option A: "Les tissus et les éclairages ne sont pas encore recyclés."
(Fabrics and lighting are not yet recycled.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical❌ FAIL"Tissus" = textiles/fabrics. "Éclairages" = lighting ≈ lampes. This says fabrics and lighting are NOT yet recycled. But the bottom bread says lamps and textiles USE voluntary drop-off — meaning they ARE being recycled. Direct contradiction.
Thematic✅ PASSAbout recycling sectors.
Grammatical❌ FAILIf A says "tissus et éclairages" aren't recycled, the bottom bread can't say they use drop-off (which IS a recycling method).

Verdict: ❌ ELIMINATED — contradicts the bottom bread

Option B: "Tous les objets sans exception sont collectés à domicile."
(All objects without exception are collected at home.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical❌ FAIL"Sans exception" (without exception) means EVERYTHING uses home collection. But the bottom bread says lamps and textiles use a DIFFERENT method (voluntary drop-off in stores). If ALL objects without exception were collected at home, there would be no need for "eux" to contrast. "Sans exception" kills the contrast.
Thematic✅ PASSAbout collection methods.
Grammatical❌ FAIL"Eux" requires a contrast, but "sans exception" eliminates any possibility of contrast.

Verdict: ❌ ELIMINATED — "sans exception" destroys the contrast required by "eux"

Option C: "Les appareils sont repris lors de la livraison d'un appareil neuf."
(Appliances are taken back during delivery of a new appliance.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical✅ PASSAppliances use take-back at delivery (Method 1). Then lamps and textiles, FOR THEIR PART, use voluntary drop-off (Method 2). Two different product types, two different methods. The contrast works perfectly.
Thematic✅ PASSAbout recycling collection methods — same theme as the paragraph.
Grammatical✅ PASS"Eux" creates a valid contrast: appliances → delivery take-back; lamps/textiles → store drop-off. The emphatic pronoun has a clear justification.

Verdict: ✅ PASSES ALL THREE TESTS

Option D: "Les consommateurs peuvent déposer leurs appareils usés chez le vendeur."
(Consumers can drop off their used appliances at the seller's.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical❌ FAIL"Déposer chez le vendeur" (drop off at the seller's) is essentially the same as "apport volontaire en magasin" (voluntary drop-off in store). If the gap describes dropping off at the seller, and the bottom bread describes dropping off in store, there's NO CONTRAST — they're the same method. "Eux" becomes meaningless.
Thematic✅ PASSAbout recycling.
Grammatical❌ FAIL"Eux" requires contrast, but the two methods described would be identical.

Verdict: ❌ ELIMINATED — no contrast possible (same method described twice)

✅ ANSWER: C — Les appareils sont repris lors de la livraison d'un appareil neuf.

The logical flow with C inserted:

The method is flourishing with about ten active sectors.
→ Appliances are taken back during delivery of a new one. [METHOD 1: take-back]
→ Lamps and textiles, FOR THEIR PART, use voluntary drop-off. [METHOD 2: drop-off]

CONTRAST:
  Appliances → passive (taken back during delivery — you don't go anywhere)
  Lamps/textiles → active (you bring them to the store yourself)

The "eux" signal was the key. This single word told you that the gap MUST describe a contrasting method. Only Option C provides that contrast.

GAP 18: Between the waste statistics and the practical solutions

Step 1: Identify the sandwich

TOP BREAD:    "Chaque Belge produit 590 kilos d'ordures ménagères par an, 
               soit deux fois plus qu'il y a quarante ans."

FILLING:       GAP 18 = ???

BOTTOM BREAD: "Il s'agit de boire l'eau du robinet, de réparer ses appareils 
               électriques, d'opter pour les piles rechargeables..."

Step 2: Analyze both breads

Top bread: States the problem — Belgians produce 590 kg of waste per year, double what it was 40 years ago. This is a problem statement with alarming data.

Bottom bread: Lists concrete individual actions — drink tap water, repair appliances, use rechargeable batteries. These are practical solutions to reduce waste.

The gap must BRIDGE from "here's the problem" to "here are the solutions." What kind of sentence creates this bridge?

PROBLEM:  Too much waste (590 kg, doubled in 40 years)
GAP 18:   [BRIDGE: introduces the solution philosophy]
SOLUTIONS: Specific actions (tap water, repairs, rechargeables)

The bridge should say something like: "The best approach is to not produce waste in the first place" → which then leads naturally into specific ways to avoid producing waste.

Step 3: Test each option using the three tests

Option A: "Aussi, les gestes les plus vertueux consistent à ne pas en produire."
(Also, the most virtuous actions consist of not producing [waste].)

TestResultReasoning
Logical✅ PASS"The most virtuous actions = not producing waste" → then "Il s'agit de..." lists specific actions that PREVENT waste (tap water instead of bottles, repair instead of replace, rechargeables instead of disposable). Perfect bridge from philosophy to practice.
Thematic✅ PASSStays on waste reduction. "En" refers to "ordures/déchets" — waste.
Grammatical✅ PASS"Aussi" (also/therefore) connects to the previous problem statement. "En" (of it = waste) has a clear antecedent in "ordures ménagères." "Il s'agit de" naturally follows as concrete examples.

Verdict: ✅ PASSES ALL THREE TESTS

Option B: "Seules des décisions prises en haut lieu pourraient inverser la tendance."
(Only decisions taken at the highest levels could reverse the trend.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical❌ FAIL"Only high-level decisions" → but the bottom bread lists INDIVIDUAL actions (drink tap water, repair your appliances). If only top-down decisions can help, why list personal actions? The gap would say "only governments can fix this" and then immediately list things YOU can do. Contradiction in agency.
Thematic≈ PartialAbout waste reduction, but shifts to institutional level.
Grammatical✅ PASSTense and structure are compatible.

Verdict: ❌ ELIMINATED — contradicts the individual-action focus of the bottom bread

Option C: "En cause, les habitudes néfastes de consommation des pays industrialisés."
(To blame: the harmful consumption habits of industrialized countries.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical❌ FAILThis sentence EXPLAINS the cause of the problem. But the bottom bread offers SOLUTIONS. The transition from "here's who's to blame" to "here's what to do" is abrupt and lacks a bridge. There's no logical connector between blame and action.
Thematic✅ PASSAbout consumption and waste.
Grammatical≈ Partial"Il s'agit de" after a blame statement feels disconnected — "s'agit de" introduces what needs to be DONE, not what caused the problem.

Verdict: ❌ ELIMINATED — blame doesn't bridge to solutions

Option D: "Et malheureusement aucun indicateur ne donne l'espoir d'un changement."
(And unfortunately no indicator gives hope for change.)

TestResultReasoning
Logical❌ FAIL"No hope for change" → immediately followed by a list of concrete changes people can make. If there's no hope, why list solutions? The hopelessness is contradicted by the practical optimism of the bottom bread.
Thematic≈ PartialAbout waste trends, but pessimistic.
Grammatical✅ PASSStructure is compatible.

Verdict: ❌ ELIMINATED — pessimism contradicts the solution-oriented bottom bread

✅ ANSWER: A — Aussi, les gestes les plus vertueux consistent à ne pas en produire.

The logical flow with A inserted:

Each Belgian produces 590 kg of waste per year, double from 40 years ago.
→ Also, the most virtuous actions consist of not producing waste.    [PHILOSOPHY]
→ This means drinking tap water, repairing appliances, using         [PRACTICE]
   rechargeable batteries...

BRIDGE STRUCTURE:
  PROBLEM (too much waste) 
    → PRINCIPLE (best action = don't produce it) 
      → EXAMPLES (specific ways to not produce it)

✅ WHAT SHOULD BE DONE

1. Always Apply the Sandwich Method

This is the non-negotiable technique for every texte à trous:

STEP 1: Read the sentence BEFORE the gap (top bread)
STEP 2: Read the sentence AFTER the gap (bottom bread)
STEP 3: Determine what BRIDGE is needed between them
STEP 4: Test each option as a potential bridge
STEP 5: The correct option must connect to BOTH breads seamlessly

Never read only the top bread or only the bottom bread. Both are essential. The gap-filler must work in both directions.

2. Decode Grammatical Signals in the Bottom Bread

The sentence AFTER the gap often contains signals that tell you what the gap must contain:

Signal in Bottom BreadWhat It Demands From the Gap
"C'est ainsi que..." (This is how)Gap must describe a METHOD or MECHANISM
"..., eux, ..." (they, for their part)Gap must describe a CONTRASTING element
"Il s'agit de..." (It's about / One should)Gap must introduce a PRINCIPLE that examples illustrate
"C'est pourquoi..." (That's why)Gap must state a CAUSE
"En revanche..." (On the other hand)Gap must state the OPPOSITE of what follows
"Par exemple..." (For example)Gap must state a GENERAL RULE that examples support

In this text:

  • Gap 16 → "C'est ainsi" demanded a mechanism → answer was the tax
  • Gap 17 → "eux" demanded a contrast → answer was a different method
  • Gap 18 → "Il s'agit de" demanded a principle → answer was the waste prevention philosophy

These signals are your most powerful tools. Learn to spot them instantly.

3. Check Pronoun References

Every pronoun in a candidate sentence must have a valid antecedent (the noun it refers to). If it doesn't, the sentence is wrong.

The pronoun check from this chapter:

OptionPronounSupposed AntecedentGender Match?Verdict
Q16-C"elle" (feminine)"produit" (masculine)❌ NOWrong
Q18-A"en" (replaces "de + noun")"ordures ménagères" / "déchets"✅ YESValid

Rule: If a candidate sentence contains a pronoun, immediately look for its antecedent in the surrounding text. If no valid antecedent exists, or if the gender/number doesn't match, eliminate that option.

Common pronouns to check:

PronounReplacesCheck
il / elleA specific masculine/feminine nounGender match
ils / ellesPlural masculine/feminine nounsGender + number match
en"de + something"Must find the "something" nearby
y"à + something"Must find the "something" nearby
le / la / lesA specific noun (direct object)Gender + number match
celui-ci / celle-ciLast mentioned masculine/feminine nounGender match

4. Test for Tense Consistency

The gap-filler's verb tense must be compatible with the surrounding text's tense:

Surrounding Text TenseGap-Filler Should BeWhy
Past ("ont été récoltés")Present or PastCan't describe future if results already exist
Present ("fait florès")PresentDescribes current situation
Present problem + solutionsPresentBoth describe current reality

In this text:

  • Gap 16: Top bread = present, bottom bread = past → Gap must NOT be future → Option A eliminated (future tense)
  • Gap 17: All present tense → Gap should be present → All options use present ✓
  • Gap 18: Present problem + present solutions → Gap should be present → All options use present ✓

❌ WHAT SHOULD NOT BE DONE

1. Do NOT Read Only the Top Bread

Many candidates read only the sentence BEFORE the gap and choose the option that continues it nicely. But the gap-filler must also connect to the sentence AFTER.

Example of this error in Gap 17:

If you only read the top bread ("La méthode fait florès avec une dizaine de filières"), Option D ("Les consommateurs peuvent déposer leurs appareils usés chez le vendeur") seems fine — it describes one of those active sectors.

But when you check the bottom bread ("Les lampes et les textiles utilisent, eux..."), you realize "eux" demands a contrast. Option D describes the same method as the bottom bread, creating no contrast. The bottom bread eliminated what the top bread accepted.

2. Do NOT Ignore the Word "Eux" / "Elle" / "Lui" Between Commas

Emphatic pronouns between commas in French are contrast markers. They're as important as "mais" (but) or "en revanche" (on the other hand):

"Les lampes et les textiles utilisent, EUX, l'apport volontaire..."
= Lamps and textiles, THEY (as opposed to what was just mentioned), use...

This tiny word — just three letters — eliminates two out of four options in Gap 17. If you miss it, you're solving the question blind.

3. Do NOT Choose an Option That Contradicts the Bottom Bread's Tone

Gap 18 demonstrates this perfectly. The bottom bread lists optimistic, actionable solutions. Two options (B and D) are pessimistic:

Option B: "Only high-level decisions could help" → pessimistic about individual action
Option D: "No indicator gives hope for change"   → completely pessimistic

Bottom bread: "Drink tap water, repair appliances..." → practical, optimistic, individual

Pessimism followed by optimistic solutions is a tonal contradiction. The gap must match the bottom bread's tone, not contradict it.

4. Do NOT Assume "Same Topic = Correct Answer"

All four options for each gap are about recycling and waste. They're ALL on topic. Thematic relevance alone NEVER selects the right answer in a texte à trous. You must also pass the logical and grammatical tests.

WRONG STRATEGY:  "This option is about recycling → it must fit"
RIGHT STRATEGY:  "This option is about recycling AND bridges the top 
                  and bottom breads AND has valid pronouns AND 
                  matches the tense → it fits"

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

Pitfall 1: The "Plausible Continuation" Trap

Some options continue the top bread beautifully but crash into the bottom bread. Option C in Gap 16 is a perfect example:

TOP BREAD:   "...must take back products to recycle them"
OPTION C:    "Thus, it doesn't end up in a dump"        ← Nice continuation!
BOTTOM BREAD: "This is how 800 million euros were collected" ← CRASH. 
              "Not ending in a dump" doesn't explain money collection.

Defense: ALWAYS check the bottom bread. A sentence that flows well from the top but crashes into the bottom is WRONG.

Pitfall 2: The "Absolute Word" Eliminator

Options containing absolute words like "sans exception" (without exception), "aucun" (none), "seules" (only), "tous" (all) are often wrong because they're too extreme for the nuanced context:

Absolute WordOptionWhy It Fails
"sans exception" (Q17-B)All objects collected at homeContradicts the existence of different methods
"aucun... espoir" (Q18-D)No hope for changeContradicts the solutions that follow
"seules" (Q18-B)Only high-level decisionsContradicts the individual actions listed

Rule of thumb: When you see an absolute word in a gap-filler option, check whether the surrounding text allows for that absoluteness. If the text shows nuance, variety, or exceptions, the absolute option is probably wrong.

Pitfall 3: The "Same Method Described Twice" Blind Spot

Option D in Gap 17 describes consumers dropping off appliances at the seller. The bottom bread describes voluntary drop-off in stores. These are essentially the same method:

OPTION D:     "déposer leurs appareils usés chez le vendeur"
               (drop off used appliances at the seller's)

BOTTOM BREAD: "l'apport volontaire en magasin"
               (voluntary drop-off in store)

vendeur ≈ magasin → seller ≈ store → SAME METHOD

If the gap describes the same method as the bottom bread, the word "eux" loses its purpose. Candidates who don't recognize the synonym relationship between "chez le vendeur" and "en magasin" will miss this.

Defense: Before accepting an option, paraphrase it AND the bottom bread in simple terms. If they describe the same thing, the contrast signal is violated.

Pitfall 4: The "Blame vs. Solution" Disconnect

Option C in Gap 18 explains the CAUSE of the problem (harmful consumption habits). The bottom bread offers SOLUTIONS (tap water, repairs, etc.). While cause-and-solution are related, the transition is too abrupt:

"Harmful consumption habits are to blame."
"This means drinking tap water, repairing appliances..."

→ "Il s'agit de" introduces WHAT TO DO, not WHAT CAUSED IT.
→ The bottom bread needs a SOLUTION PRINCIPLE, not a BLAME STATEMENT.

Option A provides the bridge: "The most virtuous actions consist of not producing waste" → "This means..." The principle introduces the actions. Blame doesn't.

🌉 BRIDGE TO PROJECT 7

You've just conquered your first texte à trous with an informational/factual text about recycling. The gaps were relatively straightforward — each one required a single logical bridge (mechanism, contrast, or principle).

In Chapter 7 (Baudelaire — Mastering the Texte à Trous II: Biography), the texte à trous format returns, but with a crucial difference: the text is a biography. This changes the game because:

  • Chronological logic becomes paramount — events must follow in the right order
  • Emotional/relational coherence matters — family relationships drive the narrative
  • Biographical facts must not contradict each other (ages, dates, life events)
  • Some gaps require you to connect cause and consequence across a person's life

You'll use every tool from this chapter (sandwich method, three-test system, signal reading) but add a new layer: biographical timeline reconstruction. The gap-fillers must not only fit logically and grammatically — they must fit into a life story that makes chronological and emotional sense.

The chronological tracking skills from Chapters 2 and 3 (→ Projects 2, 3) will be essential, now applied to a person's life rather than a system's development.
