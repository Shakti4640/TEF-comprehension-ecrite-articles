🔍 STEP 2: HOW TO APPROACH IT

Why this text is your FIFTEENTH challenge

In Project 13, you learned the Gap-Fill Method with a POSITIVE descriptive text.
In Project 14, you mastered ARGUMENTATIVE gap-fills with direction reversals.

This text is different again. It's a BUSINESS NARRATIVE — it tells the STORY of a company, following a chronological and causal chain:

Chapter 13: Positive descriptive gap-fill (consistent tone)
Chapter 14: Argumentative gap-fill (thesis → turn → counter-thesis)
                    ↓
Chapter 15: Business NARRATIVE gap-fill (concept → process → result)
            → No argumentative turn
            → Consistent POSITIVE tone throughout
            → But strict LOGICAL SEQUENCE matters
            → Each gap fits ONE step in the business story

The anatomy of THIS specific text

TYPE:        Texte à trous narratif / business story
LENGTH:      ~140 words (with gaps)
STRUCTURE:   
  SECTION 1: THE CONCEPT (what Mamy Factory does + results)
  SECTION 2: THE TRENDS (why this works in today's market)
  SECTION 3: THE PROCESS (how production works)
  SECTION 4: THE PACE (how grandmothers work)
  SECTION 5: THE POPULARITY (how successful it is)

TONE:        Positive throughout (no turn)
DIFFICULTY:  ★★★★☆ (4/5)
TRAP LEVEL:  ★★★★☆ (4/5) — subtle contradictions with established facts

💡 KEY INSIGHT: The "Au contraire" Contrast Match

THE "AU CONTRAIRE" CONTRAST MATCH:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

"Au contraire" creates a MIRROR contrast:
→ GAP says X → "Au contraire" → AFTER says the OPPOSITE of X

THE DIMENSIONS MUST MATCH:
→ If the after-sentence is about TIME → gap must be about TIME
→ If the after-sentence is about COST → gap must be about COST
→ If the after-sentence is about QUALITY → gap must be about QUALITY

THIS TEXT:
→ AFTER: "mois de production" = TIME dimension (slow)
→ GAP must be about TIME (fast)
→ NOT about money, ethics, or environment

WRONG: "Carbon footprint is good" ↔ "months of production"
       (environment ↔ time = dimension MISMATCH)

RIGHT: "Cycle is reduced" ↔ "months of production"
       (time ↔ time = dimension MATCH ✓)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

🆕 KEY CONCEPT: Dimension Matching in Contrasts

When a text uses a contrast connector ("au contraire," "en revanche," "tandis que"), the gap and the after-sentence must discuss the SAME DIMENSION (time, cost, quality, etc.) but from OPPOSITE sides. A time contrast requires both sides to discuss time. A cost contrast requires both sides to discuss cost. Mismatched dimensions = wrong answer.

✅ STEP 5: WHAT SHOULD YOU DO

Rules for Business Narrative Gap-Fills

1. Inventory ALL established facts before solving

Note who the workers are, what they make, where production happens, how design works. Any gap that contradicts these facts = wrong.

2. Match the DIMENSION in contrast pairs

If "au contraire" leads to "months of production" (time), the gap must be about time (short cycle), not about cost, ethics, or environment.

3. Use the "en effet" test to verify claims and evidence

"En effet" confirms. If the evidence is 3,500 contacts (= popularity), the claim must be about popularity (not modesty, not wage demands).

4. Track the STORY ARC position of each gap

Each gap belongs to a specific ACT in the business story. Don't insert a "result" sentence in a "process" gap, or a "condition" sentence in a "concept" gap.

5. Check that "pour autant" sentences bridge EFFICIENCY to HUMANITY

"Pour autant" often signals: "Yes, the system works well, BUT we also care about the people." The after-sentence should illustrate the human side.

6. Verify that POSITIVE texts stay positive

This text is consistently positive about Mamy Factory. Any gap introducing conflict (demands, pressure, overwhelm) breaks the tone.

❌ STEP 6: WHAT SHOULD YOU NOT DO

❌ DON'T confuse RETIREES with other social categories

"Retraitées" ≠ "cadres" (executives). "Retraitées" ≠ "chômeuses" (unemployed). "Retraitées" ≠ "couturières limogées" (fired seamstresses). These are DIFFERENT groups. Read precisely.

❌ DON'T confuse the TARGET MARKET with the WORKERS

They make CHILDREN'S clothes (target market = children). The workers are GRANDMOTHERS. Don't confuse who MAKES the product with who BUYS it. "Tenues pour séniors" (senior outfits) confuses this.

❌ DON'T insert evidence that CONTRADICTS the claim it's supposed to confirm

"Beginnings are modest" + "indeed, 3,500 contacted" = contradiction. 3,500 is NOT modest. The evidence must SUPPORT the claim.

❌ DON'T mix dimensions in contrast pairs

"Carbon footprint is positive" ↔ "months of production" = dimension mismatch. Environment ≠ time. Both sides of a contrast must discuss the same topic.

❌ DON'T introduce CONFLICT in a harmonious narrative

"Workers demand a raise" introduces labor conflict. The text presents a harmonious model where everyone benefits. Don't break this with adversarial elements.
