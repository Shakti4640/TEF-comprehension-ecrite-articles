📄 THE COMPLETE ARTICLE (with gaps)

Les Français et les téléphones portables

Dix ans seulement ont suffi au téléphone portable pour devenir un objet de consommation courante, alors que le téléphone sans fil a mis cent ans à se démocratiser. ...(36)...

Cet objet n'est même plus un signe de distinction sociale. Les historiens, les sociologues et les psychiatres s'intéressent à ce problème. ...(37)... des tendances apparaissent, qui permettent le succès du portable.

Premièrement, le Français, qui vit dans l'urgence, a le sentiment de gagner du temps ; ...(38)... Ensuite, le portable assure l'indépendance de son utilisateur : ...(39)...

De plus, le portable est un instrument de communication intrafamilial. ...(40)... Il offre enfin à l'homme l'occasion d'organiser de nouvelles relations sociales.

🔍 THE FIVE QUESTIONS

Question 36 :

  • A — La télévision s'est, elle, démocratisée en moins de temps.
  • B — Amateurs de changement, ils ont spontanément adopté le portable.
  • C — Aujourd'hui, quarante millions de Français possèdent un téléphone portable.
  • D — Cette accélération du temps est une caractéristique de la société de consommation.

Question 37 :

  • A — Seuls les démographes ne s'y intéressent pas.
  • B — Celui-ci insiste sur l'aspect pratique de cet outil.
  • C — L'utilisation du téléphone mobile révèle, selon eux, l'accélération des changements de la société française.
  • D — Ils constatent que l'utilisation des cabines téléphoniques est en baisse constante depuis cinq ans en France.

Question 38 :

  • A — Deuxièmement, il n'a plus besoin de chercher de cabine.
  • B — Pourtant, il doit respecter les horaires de ses interlocuteurs.
  • C — Par conséquent, il peut téléphoner de l'autobus, de sa voiture ou du supermarché.
  • D — Notamment parce qu'il peut gérer simultanément vie privée, vie professionnelle et vie sociale.

Question 39 : (answer given)

  • A — On peut appeler et être joint à tout moment.

Question 40 : (answer given)

  • B — Il permet d'être dehors tout en étant rassuré.

🧠 HOW TO APPROACH IT

What makes this article DIFFERENT from Chapters 18–19

In Chapters 18 and 19 you learned the fundamentals of Texte à Trous:

  • Chapter 18 (French Holiday Habits): Pronoun anchoring and statistical coherence
  • Chapter 19 (Work More, Earn More?): First-person tone matching and physical context clues

This article introduces a new structural pattern — the enumerated sociological argument. The text presents a LIST of reasons why the mobile phone succeeded, using transitional adverbs that create a visible skeleton:

SKELETON OF THE TEXT:

Paragraph 1: CONTEXT (speed of adoption) → Gap 36 → SCALE (how many)
Paragraph 2: EXPERT ANALYSIS → Gap 37 → FINDINGS
Paragraph 3: Premièrement... → Gap 38 → Ensuite... → Gap 39
Paragraph 4: De plus... → Gap 40 → Enfin...

These transitional markers — premièrement, ensuite, de plus, enfin — are your greatest allies. They tell you EXACTLY where you are in the argument and what must come next.

THE SKELETON METHOD (New technique for this chapter)

Before looking at ANY answer choices, map the skeleton of the entire text:

Step 1: Read through the text IGNORING the gaps
Step 2: Identify every transitional marker
Step 3: Label each section's FUNCTION
Step 4: Determine what ROLE each gap must play
Step 5: NOW look at answer choices

✅ HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

QUESTION 36 — The Bridge Gap

What comes BEFORE the gap:

"Dix ans seulement ont suffi au téléphone portable pour devenir un objet de consommation courante, alors que le téléphone sans fil a mis cent ans à se démocratiser."

Translation: The portable took only 10 years to become common, while the landline took 100 years.

What comes AFTER the gap:

"Cet objet n'est même plus un signe de distinction sociale."

Translation: This object is no longer even a status symbol.

The logical bridge needed: Something that PROVES how widespread the portable is. Why? Because the sentence after says it's "no longer a status symbol" — you only say that about something EVERYONE has.

BEFORE: Speed of adoption (10 years vs 100 years)
GAP 36 = ?
AFTER: No longer a status symbol → experts study it

The gap must provide EVIDENCE of mass adoption. A number. A fact.

ChoiceContentFits the bridge?Verdict
A"TV spread even faster"Introduces TV — new topic that "Cet objet" wouldn't refer to. Derails the argument.❌ Topic drift
B"Lovers of change, they spontaneously adopted it"Vague. Doesn't explain WHY "cet objet" is no longer a status symbol. No concrete evidence.❌ Too vague
C"Today, 40 million French people own a mobile phone"Concrete number → proves mass adoption → logically leads to "no longer a status symbol"✅ PERFECT BRIDGE
D"This acceleration of time is a characteristic of consumer society"Philosophical commentary on speed. But the next sentence starts "Cet objet" — referring to the portable, not to "acceleration." Grammatical mismatch.❌ Reference mismatch

The critical test: What does "Cet objet" in the AFTER sentence refer to?

  • If you choose C: "40 million French own a portable. Cet objet n'est même plus..." → "This object" = the portable. ✅ Clear reference.
  • If you choose D: "This acceleration of time is a characteristic... Cet objet n'est même plus..." → "This object" would awkwardly refer back to "acceleration"? No. ❌ Broken reference.

✅ Answer 36: C

🔧 Technique used: PRONOUN ANCHOR TEST (introduced in Chapter 18)
When the sentence AFTER a gap contains a pronoun or demonstrative ("cet objet," "celui-ci," "ils"), your gap answer MUST provide a clear antecedent for it.

QUESTION 37 — The Expert Conclusion Gap

What comes BEFORE the gap:

"Les historiens, les sociologues et les psychiatres s'intéressent à ce problème."

Three types of experts are studying this phenomenon.

What comes AFTER the gap:

"des tendances apparaissent, qui permettent le succès du portable."

Tendencies appear that explain the portable's success.

The bridge needed: What did these experts FIND? Their conclusion must lead into the list of tendencies.

BEFORE: Experts (historians, sociologists, psychiatrists) study this
GAP 37 = their finding/conclusion
AFTER: Tendencies appear that explain the portable's success
ChoiceContentAnalysisVerdict
A"Only demographers aren't interested"Tells us who ISN'T studying it. But the AFTER sentence launches into findings ("des tendances apparaissent..."). This adds nothing to the argument.❌ Dead-end information
B"Celui-ci insiste sur l'aspect pratique""Celui-ci" = singular masculine. But the BEFORE sentence mentions THREE groups of experts (plural). Grammatical mismatch.❌ Pronoun error
C"Mobile phone use reveals, according to them, the acceleration of changes in French society""Selon eux" = according to them = the experts. This is their FINDING. Then "des tendances apparaissent" elaborates on these changes. Perfect flow.✅ CORRECT
D"They note that phone booth usage has been declining for 5 years"About phone booths, not about societal changes. Doesn't lead to "tendances qui permettent le succès du portable."❌ Wrong topic focus

Let's verify the complete flow with C inserted:

"Les historiens, les sociologues et les psychiatres s'intéressent à ce problème. L'utilisation du téléphone mobile révèle, selon eux, l'accélération des changements de la société française. Des tendances apparaissent, qui permettent le succès du portable."

Translation: "Historians, sociologists, and psychiatrists are studying this problem. Mobile phone use reveals, according to them, the acceleration of changes in French society. Tendencies appear that explain the portable's success."

✅ Perfect logical chain: Experts study → They find acceleration of change → Here are the tendencies.

✅ Answer 37: C

🔧 Technique used: PRONOUN MATCH + SEMANTIC FLOW

  • "Selon eux" must match a PLURAL antecedent → the three expert groups ✅
  • "Celui-ci" (choice B) requires a SINGULAR antecedent → fails ❌
  • The content must LEAD INTO the enumerated list that follows

QUESTION 38 — The Explanation Gap (Inside Point 1)

This is the trickiest question in the article. Here's why:

What comes BEFORE the gap:

"Premièrement, le Français, qui vit dans l'urgence, a le sentiment de gagner du temps ;"

FIRST reason: The French person, who lives in urgency, feels they GAIN TIME.

What comes AFTER the gap:

"Ensuite, le portable assure l'indépendance de son utilisateur :"

SECOND reason: The portable ensures the user's INDEPENDENCE.

The semicolon (;) is critical. A semicolon means the sentence CONTINUES. Gap 38 is still INSIDE Point 1 — it elaborates on WHY the portable saves time. It is NOT Point 2 (that comes with "Ensuite").

STRUCTURE:
  Premièrement → [time-saving claim] ; GAP 38 [explanation of time-saving]
  Ensuite → [independence claim] : GAP 39 [illustration of independence]

The gap must EXPLAIN the time-saving feeling, NOT introduce a new point.

ChoiceContentAnalysisVerdict
A"Deuxièmement, il n'a plus besoin de chercher de cabine"FATAL FLAW: "Deuxièmement" = secondly. But the SECOND point already starts with "Ensuite" in the AFTER sentence. You can't have TWO second points! Structural collision.❌ Numbering conflict
B"Pourtant, il doit respecter les horaires de ses interlocuteurs""Pourtant" = however/yet. This introduces a CONTRADICTION. But the text is building a POSITIVE argument (why the portable succeeds). A contradiction here would break the flow.❌ Wrong logical direction
C"Par conséquent, il peut téléphoner de l'autobus, de sa voiture ou du supermarché""Par conséquent" = therefore. This shows the RESULT of time-saving: you can call from anywhere. But calling from anywhere is more about MOBILITY/INDEPENDENCE — which is Point 2, not Point 1.❌ Content belongs to Point 2
D"**Notamment parce qu'**il peut gérer simultanément vie privée, vie professionnelle et vie sociale""Notamment parce que" = notably because. This EXPLAINS WHY the French person feels they gain time: they manage all three spheres of life simultaneously.✅ CORRECT

Why D and not C? The deep analysis:

The question here tests whether you understand the DIFFERENCE between:

ConceptConnectorWhich Point?
Gaining time by managing multiple life domains simultaneously"Notamment parce que" (explains cause)Point 1 ✅
Being free to call from anywhere (bus, car, supermarket)"Par conséquent" (shows consequence of mobility)Point 2 (independence)

Managing private + professional + social life AT THE SAME TIME = time-saving.
Calling from the bus, car, supermarket = mobility = independence.

Choice C's content SEMANTICALLY belongs under "Ensuite, le portable assure l'indépendance de son utilisateur" — not under "le sentiment de gagner du temps."

Let's verify the complete flow with D inserted:

"Premièrement, le Français, qui vit dans l'urgence, a le sentiment de gagner du temps ; notamment parce qu'il peut gérer simultanément vie privée, vie professionnelle et vie sociale. Ensuite, le portable assure l'indépendance de son utilisateur :"

Translation: "Firstly, the French person, who lives in urgency, feels they gain time; notably because they can simultaneously manage their private, professional, and social life. Next, the portable ensures the user's independence:"

✅ The semicolon connects to an explanation. "Notamment parce que" provides the reason. The enumeration continues cleanly with "Ensuite."

✅ Answer 38: D

🔧 NEW Technique: SEMANTIC OWNERSHIP TEST
When two choices seem plausible, ask: "Which POINT in the argument does this content BELONG TO?" If the content belongs to a different point than the gap's position, it's wrong — even if it's a true statement about mobile phones.

QUESTION 39 — The Independence Illustration (Answer Given)

What comes BEFORE:

"Ensuite, le portable assure l'indépendance de son utilisateur :"

The colon (:) signals that what follows is an ILLUSTRATION or EXPLANATION.

The answer:

A — On peut appeler et être joint à tout moment.

Translation: "You can call and be reached at any time."

Why this is correct:
Being reachable at ANY moment = not depending on being at a specific location or time = independence.

The colon after "indépendance de son utilisateur" demands a concrete example. "Calling and being reached at any moment" is exactly that example.

✅ Answer 39: A (given)

QUESTION 40 — The Family Communication Illustration (Answer Given)

What comes BEFORE:

"De plus, le portable est un instrument de communication intrafamilial."

The portable is a tool for communication WITHIN THE FAMILY.

What comes AFTER:

"Il offre enfin à l'homme l'occasion d'organiser de nouvelles relations sociales."

"Enfin" = finally. This is the LAST point (new social relations). So Gap 40 must COMPLETE the family communication point before the text moves on.

The answer:

B — Il permet d'être dehors tout en étant rassuré.

Translation: "It allows you to be outside while still feeling reassured."

Why this is correct:
"Being outside while feeling reassured" = the family connection. A parent lets their teenager go out but stays reassured because they can call. A spouse is traveling but the family feels connected. This is the essence of intrafamilial communication — staying connected even when physically apart.

✅ Answer 40: B (given)

🎯 WHAT COULD BE DONE (Strategic Options for Enumerated Texts)

When you encounter a gap-fill text with enumerated arguments, you have two strategies:

Strategy A: Skeleton-First (RECOMMENDED for enumerated texts)

1. Scan for ALL transitional markers (premièrement, ensuite, de plus, enfin)
2. Label each section's TOPIC in the margin
3. Determine each gap's ROLE (bridge? explanation? illustration?)
4. THEN read answer choices
Time: 5-6 minutes | Accuracy: Very High

Strategy B: Gap-by-Gap Sequential

1. Read up to Gap 36 → solve it → continue to Gap 37 → solve it → etc.
Time: 5-6 minutes | Accuracy: Medium (you may miss the overall structure)

Strategy A is superior here because understanding the FULL SKELETON prevents the "semantic ownership" error (putting the right content in the wrong gap).

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL #1: The Numbering Collision 🔢

Text structure:    Premièrement... ; GAP 38 ... Ensuite...
Choice A says:     "Deuxièmement, il n'a plus besoin..."

The trap: "Deuxièmement" seems natural after "Premièrement." But "Ensuite" ALREADY introduces the second point in the text. You'd have:

Premièrement... Deuxièmement... Ensuite (= also secondly?)...

Two "second points" in a row. The enumeration collapses.

How to detect it: Before choosing an answer with a numbering word, CHECK what the sentence AFTER the gap starts with. If it already has a number/sequence marker, your gap answer CANNOT have the next one.

PITFALL #2: The Semantic Displacement 🔄

Gap 38 is inside Point 1 (TIME-SAVING)
Choice C talks about calling from buses, cars, supermarkets
This is actually about MOBILITY = INDEPENDENCE = Point 2

The trap: The content is 100% true about mobile phones. It even seems to relate to saving time (you call while doing other things). But its PRIMARY semantic domain is INDEPENDENCE/MOBILITY, which belongs to the next point.

How to detect it: Ask: "If I had to put this sentence under a HEADING, which heading would it go under?"

  • "Manage all life spheres simultaneously" → heading: TIME MANAGEMENT ✅
  • "Call from the bus, car, supermarket" → heading: FREEDOM/MOBILITY ❌ (for this gap)

PITFALL #3: The Singular/Plural Pronoun Mismatch 👤👥

Text: "Les historiens, les sociologues et les psychiatres..." (PLURAL)
Choice B: "Celui-ci insiste..." (SINGULAR)

The trap: "Celui-ci" refers to ONE person or thing. But the preceding sentence mentions THREE groups. You can't use a singular pronoun to refer to a plural subject.

How to detect it: Circle every pronoun in the answer choice. Draw an arrow to what it refers to in the text. If singular points to plural (or vice versa), ELIMINATE.

PITFALL #4: The Logical Direction Reversal ↩️

Text is building POSITIVE arguments for mobile phone success
Choice B: "Pourtant, il doit respecter les horaires..." (introduces a LIMITATION)

The trap: "Pourtant" means "however/yet" — it introduces a contrast or problem. But the entire paragraph is listing ADVANTAGES. A sudden limitation breaks the positive momentum.

How to detect it: Identify the paragraph's DIRECTION (positive? negative? neutral?). If a choice's connector reverses that direction without the text doing so, eliminate it.
