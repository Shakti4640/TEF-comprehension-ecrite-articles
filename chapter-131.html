📖 CHAPTER 1: LE TOAST AU CAVIAR — YOUR FIRST FACTUAL EXTRACTION

Phase 1 | Very Easy | Foundation Thinking

📰 COMPLETE ARTICLE

Un toast au caviar de 130m de long

Un toast au caviar 130 mètres de long a été exposé jeudi à Saint-Pétersbourg, et ses cuisiniers espèrent entrer dans le livre de records. Une immense tranche pain blanc, surmontée d'une rivière de 130 mètres de caviar rouge. Moins cher que le caviar noir, a été préparé par les cuisiniers d'un hôtel de luxe de Saint-Pétersbourg. La deuxième ville de Russie à l'occasion du 135° anniversaire de la fondation de l'établissement.

Le toast a ensuite été exposé dans la rue, le long de la façade de l'hôtel avant d'être englouti par les invités de la cérémonie d'anniversaire.

THE QUESTIONS:

Question 14 : Le toast au caviar

  • A) est entré dans le livre des records
  • B) a été fabriqué au bord d'une rivière
  • C) a été réalisé par les employés d'un hôtel
  • D) a été préparé avec du caviar rouge et noir

Question 15 : À la fin de la cérémonie, la tartine a été

  • A) Exposée devant l'hôtel
  • B) Mangée par les participants
  • C) Gardée par les cuisiniers de l'hôtel
  • D) Conservée au sein de l'établissement

Question 16 : Cet évènement a eu lieu pour

  • A) La commémoration de la création d'un hôtel en Russie
  • B) L'ouverture d'un hôtel de luxe à Saint-Pétersbourg
  • C) L'anniversaire du cuisinier d'un grand hôtel russe
  • D) La fête nationale dans la deuxième ville de Russie

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

This is your very first Compréhension Écrite exercise. Before you touch a single question, you need to understand what this test actually measures.

The Fundamental Truth of CE:

Every correct answer is ALREADY in the text. You are not being asked for your opinion. You are not being asked to guess. You are being asked to MATCH information.

This text is a short factual news report — the simplest type you will encounter. It reads like a newspaper brief. There is:

  • No hidden opinion
  • No complex argumentation
  • No irony or sarcasm
  • No ambiguity

It's pure WHO did WHAT, WHERE, WHEN, WHY, and HOW.

The Framework You Need: W.W.W.W.W.H.

Before reading any question, read the entire text ONCE and mentally answer:

QuestionAnswer from Text
WHO?Les cuisiniers d'un hôtel de luxe
WHAT?Un toast au caviar de 130 mètres de long
WHERE?Saint-Pétersbourg, Russie
WHEN?Jeudi (+ à l'occasion du 135° anniversaire)
WHY?Pour le 135° anniversaire de la fondation de l'hôtel
HOW?Caviar rouge sur pain blanc, exposé dans la rue, puis mangé

If you can fill this table before looking at the questions, you've already solved 90% of the problem.

Why Start Here?

Because this tiny 80-word text teaches you the single most important skill in all of Compréhension Écrite:

The answer is a PARAPHRASE of the text — never a COPY, never an INVENTION.

The exam never quotes the text word-for-word in the correct answer. It rephrases. Your job is to recognize the rephrase and reject the traps.

🧩 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

Let's solve each question with a forensic, step-by-step method that you will use for every CE question from now on.

QUESTION 14: Le toast au caviar...

Step 1: Read all four options BEFORE deciding.

OptionWhat it claims
Aest entré dans le livre des records
Ba été fabriqué au bord d'une rivière
Ca été réalisé par les employés d'un hôtel
Da été préparé avec du caviar rouge et noir

Step 2: Go back to the text and CHECK each one.

Option A — "est entré dans le livre des records"

Text says: "ses cuisiniers espèrent entrer dans le livre de records"

The word "espèrent" (hope to) is critical. They HOPE to enter. They have NOT entered. This is the classic "almost right" trap. The text mentions the book of records, which makes your brain think "yes!" — but the verb tense and meaning say "not yet."

❌ ELIMINATED — Aspiration ≠ Achievement

Option B — "a été fabriqué au bord d'une rivière"

Text says: "surmontée d'une rivière de 130 mètres de caviar rouge"

The word "rivière" appears in the text — but it's used metaphorically! A "rivière de caviar" means a long stream/line of caviar. It does NOT mean the toast was made next to a river.

❌ ELIMINATED — Metaphorical word ≠ Literal meaning

This is your first encounter with one of the most dangerous traps in CE: the word-match trap. You see a word from the text in an answer option and your brain says "match!" — but the MEANING is completely different.

Option C — "a été réalisé par les employés d'un hôtel"

Text says: "a été préparé par les cuisiniers d'un hôtel de luxe"

Now notice: the text says "cuisiniers" (cooks), the answer says "employés" (employees). Are cooks employees of the hotel? YES. "Employés" is a broader, more general term that includes "cuisiniers."

This is a PARAPHRASE — the hallmark of a correct answer.

✅ CORRECT — "cuisiniers d'un hôtel" → "employés d'un hôtel"

Option D — "a été préparé avec du caviar rouge et noir"

Text says: "caviar rouge. Moins cher que le caviar noir"

The text mentions caviar noir only as a comparison ("less expensive than black caviar"). The toast was made with RED caviar only. The mention of black caviar is there to explain why they chose red.

❌ ELIMINATED — Comparison reference ≠ Ingredient used

✅ ANSWER: C

QUESTION 15: À la fin de la cérémonie, la tartine a été...

Step 1: Notice the question's time marker — "À la fin de la cérémonie"

This tells you to look at what happened LAST. Go to the end of the text.

Step 2: Locate the relevant sentence.

"Le toast a ensuite été exposé dans la rue, le long de la façade de l'hôtel avant d'être englouti par les invités de la cérémonie d'anniversaire."

Key word: "englouti" — this means "devoured / gobbled up / eaten"

The sequence is:

  1. FIRST → exposed in the street (exposé dans la rue)
  2. THEN → eaten by the guests (englouti par les invités)

Step 3: Check each option.

OptionClaimVerdict
AExposée devant l'hôtelThis happened BEFORE the end — it was exposed first, then eaten. The question asks about "à la fin." ❌
BMangée par les participants"Mangée" = eaten. "Participants" = attendees. Text says "englouti par les invités." PARAPHRASE! ✅
CGardée par les cuisiniersText says nothing about cooks keeping it. Pure invention. ❌
DConservée au sein de l'établissementText says nothing about preserving it. Invented. ❌

The critical lesson here: Option A describes something TRUE that the text says — the toast WAS exposed in front of the hotel. But the question asks about what happened "à la fin" (at the end). Reading the question precisely is just as important as reading the text.

✅ ANSWER: B

QUESTION 16: Cet évènement a eu lieu pour...

Step 1: What does "a eu lieu pour" mean?
It means "took place for / on the occasion of." We need the REASON / OCCASION.

Step 2: Locate it.

"à l'occasion du 135° anniversaire de la fondation de l'établissement"

  • "135° anniversaire" = 135th anniversary
  • "de la fondation" = of the founding/creation
  • "de l'établissement" = of the establishment (= the hotel)

Step 3: Check each option.

OptionClaimAnalysis
ALa commémoration de la création d'un hôtel en Russie"Commémoration" ≈ "anniversaire." "Création" = "fondation." "Hôtel en Russie" = yes, Saint-Pétersbourg is in Russia. This is a PARAPHRASE. ✅
BL'ouverture d'un hôtel de luxe"Ouverture" means opening — implies a NEW hotel. But this is the 135th ANNIVERSARY. The hotel already exists. ❌
CL'anniversaire du cuisinierAnniversary of the COOK? No — anniversary of the HOTEL'S FOUNDING. ❌
DLa fête nationale dans la deuxième ville de Russie"Fête nationale" = national holiday. Nothing about this in the text. The mention of "deuxième ville de Russie" is a geographical detail, not a reason. ❌

Watch Option B carefully: "ouverture" and "fondation" seem similar, but "ouverture" implies it's happening NOW (a new hotel opening), while "anniversaire de la fondation" means remembering a PAST creation. The 135 years make this obvious — but in harder texts, this distinction will be subtler.

✅ ANSWER: A

✅ WHAT COULD BE DONE

Here are strategies that WORK for factual texts like this one:

Strategy 1: The Two-Pass Reading Method

PASS 1 (30 seconds): Read the ENTIRE text without looking at questions.
         → Goal: Understand the general situation (WHO, WHAT, WHERE, WHEN, WHY)
         → Do NOT memorize details. Just get the "movie" in your head.

PASS 2 (per question): Go back to the text to VERIFY each answer.
         → Goal: Find the EXACT sentence that supports your choice.
         → If you can't point to a specific sentence, your answer is probably wrong.

Strategy 2: The Elimination Tournament

Never try to find the right answer directly. Instead:

Round 1: Eliminate options that are CLEARLY INVENTED (not in text at all)
Round 2: Eliminate options that CONTRADICT the text
Round 3: Between remaining options, find the one that PARAPHRASES the text

In Question 14:

  • Round 1: No option is completely invented (all use words from the text)
  • Round 2: A contradicts ("espèrent" ≠ "est entré"), D contradicts (only red, not red AND black)
  • Round 3: Between B and C → B uses "rivière" literally (contradiction), C paraphrases "cuisiniers" as "employés" ✅

Strategy 3: Synonym Awareness

Start building your paraphrase reflex. The correct answer almost NEVER uses the exact same words as the text:

Text saysAnswer says
cuisiniers d'un hôtelemployés d'un hôtel
englouti par les invitésmangée par les participants
anniversaire de la fondationcommémoration de la création
espèrent entrer≠ est entré

✅ WHAT SHOULD YOU DO

DO #1: Read the QUESTION before the options

The question itself contains crucial information. In Q15, "à la fin de la cérémonie" tells you WHERE to look in the text. If you skip this, you'll pick A (which is true but doesn't answer the question).

DO #2: Underline time markers and sequence words

In this text:

  • "a été exposé jeudi" → when
  • "a ensuite été exposé dans la rue" → sequence (then)
  • "avant d'être englouti" → sequence (before being)
  • "à l'occasion du 135° anniversaire" → reason/occasion

These words are your navigation system through the text.

DO #3: Verify EVERY word in your chosen answer

If even ONE word in the answer doesn't match the text, it's probably wrong. In Option D of Q14, every word matches except "et noir" — that's enough to kill it.

DO #4: Accept that correct answers use DIFFERENT vocabulary

Train your brain: same meaning, different words = likely correct. Same words, different meaning = likely a trap.

DO #5: Treat each option as a True/False statement

Don't compare options to each other. Compare each one INDIVIDUALLY to the text:

  • "Does the text say this? Yes or No?"
  • Only ONE will be "Yes."

❌ WHAT SHOULD YOU NOT DO

DON'T #1: Pick an answer because you see the same word in the text

This is the #1 killer in CE. Option B has "rivière" — it's IN the text. But it means something completely different. This trap will appear in EVERY exam, at EVERY level.

DON'T #2: Use your general knowledge

You might know that Saint-Pétersbourg is Russia's second city. But the question asks WHY the event took place, not WHERE. Your knowledge of Russian geography doesn't help — only the text matters.

DON'T #3: Read too fast and skip small words

The difference between "espèrent entrer" and "est entré" is the difference between wrong and right. Small words like espèrent, avant, ensuite, seulement, moins change EVERYTHING.

DON'T #4: Change your answer without a TEXT-BASED reason

If you want to change an answer, you must be able to point to a SENTENCE in the text that justifies the change. "I have a feeling" is not a reason.

DON'T #5: Spend more than 2 minutes on a factual question like this

Short factual texts should take 3-4 minutes TOTAL (all questions). If you're stuck on one question, mark your best guess, move on, and come back later.

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL 1: The "Keyword Match" Illusion

YOUR BRAIN: "I see 'rivière' in the text AND in option B → must be correct!"
REALITY:    "rivière de caviar" = metaphor for a long line of caviar
            "au bord d'une rivière" = next to an actual river
LESSON:     Same word ≠ Same meaning

PITFALL 2: The "Almost Right" Trap

YOUR BRAIN: "The text mentions the book of records → Option A talks about records → correct!"
REALITY:    Text says "HOPE to enter" / Option says "HAS entered"
LESSON:     Verify the VERB and its TENSE, not just the noun

PITFALL 3: The "True But Wrong" Trap

YOUR BRAIN: "The toast WAS exposed in front of the hotel → Option A is true!"
REALITY:    The QUESTION asks "à la fin de la cérémonie" — exposure happened BEFORE the end
LESSON:     A true statement can be the WRONG answer if it doesn't answer the QUESTION

PITFALL 4: The "Partial Information" Trap

YOUR BRAIN: "The text mentions red caviar AND black caviar → Option D says both → correct!"
REALITY:    Black caviar is mentioned as a COMPARISON, not as an ingredient
LESSON:     Just because something is MENTIONED doesn't mean it APPLIES

PITFALL 5: The "Confusion of Similar Concepts" Trap

YOUR BRAIN: "Ouverture and fondation are basically the same thing"
REALITY:    "Ouverture" = opening (implies new) / "Anniversaire de la fondation" = anniversary 
             of creation (implies old, celebrating the past)
LESSON:     Near-synonyms with different implications are favorite exam traps

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY + BRIDGE TO NEXT CHAPTER

What Chapter 1 Taught You:

The Three Laws of Factual Comprehension:

LAW 1: The correct answer is a PARAPHRASE of the text — never a copy, never an invention.

LAW 2: Every word matters — one wrong verb tense, one added adjective, one literal interpretation of a metaphor can make a true-sounding answer FALSE.

LAW 3: Answer the QUESTION being asked, not the question you THINK is being asked. Time markers, conditions, and scope words in the question are as important as the text itself.

The W.W.W.W.W.H. Framework:

WHO → WHAT → WHERE → WHEN → WHY → HOW
  ↓
Fill this BEFORE looking at questions
  ↓
Each question will target ONE of these
  ↓
Go back to text, find the EXACT sentence, VERIFY

Your New Reflex Checklist:

  • Read full text first, questions second
  • Fill the W.W.W.W.W.H. mentally
  • Read the question CAREFULLY (especially time/condition markers)
  • Eliminate by contradiction, not by guessing
  • Correct answer = paraphrase (different words, same meaning)
  • Trap answer = same words, different meaning

🌉 BRIDGE TO CHAPTER 2

In Chapter 2 (Album d'Alicia Cruz), you will face your first review/critique — a text where someone expresses a judgment about an artist. This introduces:
