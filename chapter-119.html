Avec une population mondiale déjà à 50% urbaine et prévue à 70% en 2050, le développement durable est un enjeu fondamental de l'évolution des villes. ...(36)... Son empreinte écologique doit être la plus réduite possible pour atténuer le réchauffement climatique. Il faut contrôler le degré de pollution, le recyclage des déchets, adopter des modes de consommation et de production responsables. ...(37)... Et même après. D'où l'intérêt de construire avec des matériaux recyclables. Les bâtiments à haute qualité environnementale ont aussi l'intérêt d'améliorer le bien-être de ceux qui les habitent.

...(38)... : faire en sorte que les habitants s'épanouissent dans leur ville. ...(39)... La réflexion sur les transports est donc un des axes majeurs d'une politique de développement durable.

Entre le bruit, la pollution, les risques d'accident et la consommation énergétique, ...(40)... Mais doit-on pour autant la rejeter complètement ?

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

What's New in This Chapter

Chapter 14 introduced texte à trous with CONNECTOR-BASED gaps — short phrases where the logical connector (même si, mais, dès lors que) was the decisive element. The content was secondary to the logical relationship.

Chapter 15 escalates dramatically: the gaps require FULL SENTENCES. Each option is a complete statement that must:

FULL-SENTENCE GAP REQUIREMENTS:

1. THEMATIC CONTINUITY
   → The sentence must fit the TOPIC of the surrounding paragraph
   → If the paragraph discusses pollution, a sentence about politics doesn't fit

2. LOGICAL FLOW
   → The sentence must connect to what comes BEFORE and AFTER
   → It must serve as a BRIDGE between the two sides

3. GRAMMATICAL REFERENCE
   → Pronouns and references in surrounding text must make sense
   → If the text says "Son empreinte" after the gap,
     the gap must introduce something with a FEMININE possessive

4. ARGUMENT ADVANCEMENT
   → The sentence must MOVE the argument forward
   → It shouldn't repeat what was already said
   → It shouldn't jump to a topic not yet introduced

The New Core Concept: Thematic Paragraph Mapping

Before filling any gap, you must understand what THEME each paragraph addresses:

THEMATIC MAP OF THIS TEXT:

PARAGRAPH 1: ECOLOGICAL CRITERIA of sustainable cities
  → Pollution, recycling, consumption, construction materials
  → Theme: ENVIRONMENTAL impact

PARAGRAPH 2: HUMAN WELL-BEING in sustainable cities
  → Inhabitants flourishing, streets, transport
  → Theme: SOCIAL/HUMAN dimension

PARAGRAPH 3: THE CAR QUESTION
  → Noise, pollution, accidents, energy
  → Theme: TRANSPORT (specifically cars)

Each gap must fit its paragraph's THEME.
A sentence about politics doesn't fit an ecology paragraph.
A sentence about construction doesn't fit a transport paragraph.

The New Core Concept: The Pronoun/Reference Anchor

In full-sentence gaps, the text AFTER the gap often contains pronouns or references that POINT BACK to the gap. These are ANCHORS that help you identify what the gap must contain:

PRONOUN ANCHORS:

GAP 36: After the gap → "Son empreinte écologique"
  "Son" = its (feminine possessive) → refers to something introduced in the gap
  WHAT has an "ecological footprint"? → A city / "une cité durable"

GAP 37: After the gap → "Et même après"
  "Après" = after → after WHAT? → after the thing described in the gap
  "Et même après" suggests something that continues BEYOND a period

GAP 38: After the gap → "faire en sorte que les habitants s'épanouissent"
  The colon (:) after the gap means the gap INTRODUCES what follows
  The gap must be a statement that is EXPLAINED by "faire en sorte que..."

GAP 39: After the gap → "La réflexion sur les transports est DONC..."
  "DONC" = therefore → the gap provides the REASON for transport being important
  The gap must establish WHY transport matters for well-being

This is a CRITICAL new technique:

THE ANCHOR METHOD:

STEP 1: Read the text AFTER the gap
STEP 2: Identify any PRONOUNS, REFERENCES, or CONNECTORS
STEP 3: Determine what these must POINT TO
STEP 4: The gap must contain what these anchors reference

If "Son" follows the gap → the gap introduces a feminine noun
If "donc" follows the gap → the gap provides a reason/cause
If "Et même après" follows → the gap describes a time period
If a colon (:) follows → the gap introduces what the colon explains

✅ SOLVING GAP 37

Context sandwich:

BEFORE: "Il faut contrôler le degré de pollution, le recyclage des déchets, 
         adopter des modes de consommation et de production responsables."
  = We must control pollution, recycling, adopt responsible modes

AFTER:  "ET MÊME APRÈS. D'où l'intérêt de construire avec des matériaux 
         recyclables."
  = And even AFTER. Hence the interest of building with recyclable materials.

ANCHORS:
  1. "Et même APRÈS" → after WHAT? The gap must describe a TIME PERIOD
     that has an "after" — something with a DURATION
  2. "D'où l'intérêt de construire avec des matériaux recyclables"
     → Therefore, recyclable materials are important
     → The gap must explain WHY recyclable construction matters
     → Something about construction's LONG-TERM impact

LOGICAL FLOW:
  Pollution control, recycling, responsible consumption
  → [GAP describes something with duration]
  → "And even after [that duration]"
  → Therefore, build with recyclable materials
  
  What has a "life" and an "after"? → A BUILDING
  A building impacts the environment during its ENTIRE LIFE
  And even AFTER (demolition/end of life)
  → Hence recyclable materials (for the "after" phase)
OptionAnalysisVerdict
A) Le recyclage quotidien est en train de devenir une habitude."Daily recycling is becoming a habit." This talks about PEOPLE's recycling habits. "Et même après" (and even after) doesn't connect — after what? After recycling becomes a habit? That doesn't lead to "build with recyclable materials." No clear TIME PERIOD with an "after."❌ ELIMINATED (no anchor match)
B) Il y a toujours eu et il y aura toujours différentes formes de pollution."There have always been and will always be different forms of pollution." This is a GENERAL statement about pollution's permanence. "Et même après" doesn't connect — after always? The eternal nature of pollution contradicts a specific "after."❌ ELIMINATED (no anchor match)
C) Une construction a un impact sur l'environnement durant toute sa vie."A building has an impact on the environment throughout its entire life." "Durant toute sa VIE" (throughout its life) establishes a TIME PERIOD. "Et même après" = and even after [its life] = even after the building is demolished. This leads to: "D'où l'intérêt de construire avec des matériaux recyclables" = hence recyclable materials (so materials can be reused even after the building's life ends). PERFECT logical chain.✅ CORRECT
D) Avant de commencer à construire, il faut lister les matériaux nécessaires."Before starting to build, you must list necessary materials." This talks about PLANNING before construction. "Et même après" would mean "and even after [listing materials]," which doesn't lead logically to recyclable materials. The connection is forced.❌ ELIMINATED (weak logic)

Answer: C

The critical reading skills here:

Skill 1 — The "Et même après" anchor resolution:

"ET MÊME APRÈS" ANCHOR:

This phrase means: "And even AFTER [something]"
It requires the gap to describe something with a DEFINED DURATION
that has a meaningful "after."

Option C: "durant toute sa VIE" (throughout its life)
  → Life of a building = defined duration
  → "Et même après" = and even after its life (after demolition)
  → This makes sense: impact during life AND after

Why others fail:
  A: "becoming a habit" → no defined "after" point
  B: "always" → eternal = no "after" possible
  D: "before building" → "after" would mean "after listing" → trivial

ONLY Option C provides a TIME PERIOD with a meaningful "after."

This introduces a powerful technique:

THE ANCHOR RESOLUTION METHOD:

When the text AFTER a gap contains a REFERENCE WORD:
  "après" → the gap must describe a TIME PERIOD
  "son/sa" → the gap must introduce a NOUN with the right gender
  "donc" → the gap must provide a CAUSE/REASON
  "ceci/cela" → the gap must describe a THING
  "y/en" → the gap must introduce a PLACE or QUANTITY

The reference word in the AFTER text is your ANCHOR.
Trace it back to find what the gap MUST contain.

Skill 2 — Following the causal chain: duration → after → recyclable:

CAUSAL CHAIN:

GAP: "A building impacts the environment throughout its life"
  → Buildings have environmental impact for their ENTIRE duration

"Et même après" → impact continues even AFTER the building's life

"D'où l'intérêt de construire avec des matériaux recyclables"
  → THEREFORE, recyclable materials are important
  → Because materials can be REUSED after the building is gone
  → Reducing impact even in the "after" phase

CHAIN:
  Impact during life → impact even after life → 
  THEREFORE → use recyclable materials (to minimize "after" impact)

Each link in the chain REQUIRES the previous link.
Without the "building's life" concept, "et même après" has no referent.
Without "et même après," the push for recyclable materials has no motivation.

Answer: B

The critical reading skills here:

Skill 1 — The feminine pronoun "la" as a decisive anchor:

PRONOUN ANCHOR: "la rejeter"

"la" = feminine singular direct object pronoun
"rejeter" = to reject

The gap must introduce a FEMININE NOUN that:
  1. Causes noise, pollution, accidents, energy consumption
  2. Can logically be "rejected"
  3. Makes sense with "mais doit-on la rejeter complètement?"

CANDIDATES:
  A: "la ville" (the city) — feminine, but rejecting cities contradicts the text
  B: "la voiture" (the car) — feminine, causes all listed problems, can be rejected ✅
  C: "la mobilité écologique" — feminine, but rejecting eco-mobility contradicts text
  D: "la mobilisation" — feminine, but rejecting green mobilization contradicts text

Only "la voiture" fits ALL three criteria:
  1. Cars cause noise, pollution, accidents, energy consumption ✅
  2. Cars can logically be rejected (stop using them) ✅
  3. "Should we reject THE CAR completely?" is a valid nuanced question ✅

Skill 2 — The "MAIS" opposition structure for nuanced positions:

NUANCED POSITION STRUCTURE:

[NEGATIVE statement about X] + MAIS + [Should we reject X entirely?]

GAP: "La voiture ne peut plus être le nerf de la ville"
  = The car can no longer be the backbone of the city (NEGATIVE)

MAIS: "Mais doit-on pour autant la rejeter complètement ?"
  = But should we therefore reject it completely? (DOUBT about total rejection)

This creates a NUANCED POSITION:
  YES, the car is problematic (GAP acknowledges problems)
  BUT total rejection may be too extreme (MAIS introduces doubt)
  = The car needs to be REDUCED, not ELIMINATED

This is similar to Ch.9's dual position:
  Ch.9: Approve severity for serious cases BUT reject for minor ones
  Ch.15: Acknowledge car problems BUT question total rejection

Skill 3 — Eliminating options that contradict the text's premise:

THE PREMISE CHECK:

The text's title: "La ville est-elle l'avenir de l'homme?"
  = Is the city humanity's future?
  → The text explores how to make cities BETTER, not whether to abandon them

The text's theme: SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT of cities
  → The text is PRO-CITY, PRO-SUSTAINABILITY

Options that contradict this premise:
  A: "reject the city" → contradicts pro-city stance ❌
  C: "reject ecological mobility" → contradicts pro-sustainability stance ❌
  D: "green mobilization doomed to failure" → contradicts pro-sustainability ❌

Only B maintains the premise:
  "The car can no longer be central" → criticizes ONE element (cars)
  Without rejecting cities or sustainability as a whole

Strategy: The Three-Anchor System for Full-Sentence Gaps

For every gap in a full-sentence texte à trous, check THREE anchors:

THE THREE-ANCHOR SYSTEM:

ANCHOR 1: PRONOUN REFERENCE
  → What pronouns appear AFTER the gap?
  → "Son" → feminine possessor needed
  → "la" → feminine noun needed
  → "y" → place needed
  → "en" → quantity/topic needed

ANCHOR 2: LOGICAL CONNECTOR
  → What connector appears AFTER the gap?
  → "donc" → gap provides REASON
  → "mais" → gap makes a statement that "mais" COUNTERS
  → "et même" → gap describes something being ESCALATED
  → "d'où" → gap provides the CAUSE for what follows

ANCHOR 3: THEMATIC CONTINUITY
  → What THEME does the paragraph address?
  → Does the option fit this theme?
  → Does it advance the argument or regress?

Before accepting any option, verify it doesn't contradict the text's OVERALL premise:

3. Is the contradiction INTENTIONAL (nuanced position) or ACCIDENTAL?

   → "Green mobilization doomed" = accidental contradiction (rejects the premise)

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

Pitfall #1: The "Broken Reference Chain" Trap (Most Important Pitfall of This Chapter)

What happens: The text after a gap contains a pronoun or reference word that REQUIRES a specific antecedent in the gap. An option doesn't provide that antecedent, breaking the reference chain.

THE MECHANISM:

AFTER gap: "Son empreinte écologique" (Its ecological footprint)
  "Son" needs a FEMININE possessor
  
Option C: "Pourquoi est-IL si peu considéré" (Why is IT so little considered)
  "il" = masculine → refers to "développement durable" (masculine)
  → "Son empreinte" would then mean "the footprint of sustainable development"
  → But sustainable development doesn't HAVE a footprint; CITIES do
  → The reference chain BREAKS

Option A: "les critères d'une CITÉ DURABLE" (criteria of a sustainable city)
  "cité" = feminine → "son empreinte" = the city's footprint
  → The reference chain WORKS ✅

Defense: After selecting an option, read the COMPLETE sentence (before + gap + after) and verify all pronouns have clear, logical antecedents.

Pitfall #2: The "Thematic Intruder" Trap

What happens: An option introduces a topic from a DIFFERENT paragraph or a completely new theme. It might be a perfectly valid sentence on its own, but it doesn't belong in THIS paragraph.

Gap 36 is in the ECOLOGY paragraph.
Option B introduces POLITICS ("rôle des politiciens").
Option D introduces DEMOGRAPHY ("contrôle démographique").

Both are valid topics for urban sustainability.
But THIS paragraph discusses ECOLOGICAL criteria.
Politics and demography belong in OTHER paragraphs.

The sentence might be "correct" in isolation
but "wrong" in THIS specific location.

Defense: Before accepting an option, ask: "Does this sentence belong in THIS paragraph, given its theme?"

Pitfall #3: The "Argument Regression" Trap

What happens: The text's argument PROGRESSES from one theme to another. An option pulls the reader BACK to a previous theme instead of advancing.

ARGUMENT PROGRESSION:
  Ecology → Human well-being → Transport → Cars

Gap 38 is at the TRANSITION from ecology to well-being.
Option D goes BACK to buildings (ecology theme).
But the text has already moved PAST buildings.

The argument should ADVANCE:
  Buildings (done) → Well-being (starting) → Transport (coming)

Going back to buildings = REGRESSION
Moving to well-being = PROGRESSION

Defense: Ask: "Does this option move the argument FORWARD or BACKWARD?" Arguments in French journalistic texts typically progress from broad to specific, from established to new.

Pitfall #4: The "Premise Contradiction" Trap

What happens: An option contradicts the text's fundamental position. A text about making cities better shouldn't conclude by saying the effort is doomed.

TEXT PREMISE: Cities can be improved through sustainable development
TEXT TONE: Constructive, solution-oriented

Option D (Gap 40): "La mobilisation pour une ville verte semble vouée à l'échec"
  = The mobilization for a green city seems doomed to failure
  = CONTRADICTS the constructive, solution-oriented tone
  = The text wouldn't spend three paragraphs describing solutions
    only to conclude that everything is doomed

PREMISE CONTRADICTION: the option says the opposite of what the text argues.

Defense: Before accepting a concluding option, ask: "Does this align with the text's overall MESSAGE? Would the author say this?"
