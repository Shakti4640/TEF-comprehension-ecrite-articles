CHAPTER 9: THE ORMANO ASSOCIATION IN MADAGASCAR

📰 Texte 93 — Environmental Education | Phase 2: Core Patterns

📄 THE COMPLETE ARTICLE

L'association Ormano

L'association Ormano a vu le jour en 1999, au nord-est de Madagascar, dans une région riche en forêts et en mangroves (zone marécageuse en bord de mer) mais menacée de défrichement.

Conçues non seulement pour préserver ces espaces remarquables, mais aussi pour faire découvrir aux enfants leur environnement, cet organisme porte un superbe projet d'éducation à la nature. Les actions sont directement menées par les professeurs qui accompagnent leurs élèves au cœur de la forêt et leur apprennent à voir et à comprendre l'étroite interdépendance entre toutes les formes de vie qui la peuplent. Les observations sont reprises dans des fiches, servent à faire des posters ou à confectionner des dictionnaires de nature. Cette démarche conduit aussi les parents à participer et être sensibilisés.

Ces actions, localisées au départ, sont reprises progressivement par d'autres provinces de Madagascar et, par un effet de répétition et de multiplication, deviennent d'autant plus efficaces.

🧠 HOW TO APPROACH IT

What's NEW in this chapter

Chapter 8 tracked cause-effect chains in a science article. This chapter introduces organizational analysis — understanding an institution's structure, objectives, methods, and growth.

NEW CONCEPT 1: Primary vs. Secondary Objectives

Organizations often have multiple goals. The text might mention several, but one is the MAIN purpose and others are secondary benefits. The question "but principal" (main purpose) tests whether you can identify the HIERARCHY.

Look at this sentence from the text:

"Conçues non seulement pour préserver ces espaces remarquables, mais aussi pour faire découvrir aux enfants leur environnement"

This contains the classic "non seulement... mais aussi..." structure:

non seulement POUR [OBJECTIVE A]     → preserve natural spaces
mais aussi POUR [OBJECTIVE B]        → help children discover their environment

Both are objectives. But which is PRIMARY? The answer lies in what the text emphasizes MOST — not just what it lists first.

NEW CONCEPT 2: Actor Differentiation

This text involves FOUR types of actors:

THE ASSOCIATION (Ormano)    → creates and carries the project
THE TEACHERS (professeurs)  → execute the actions directly
THE STUDENTS (élèves)       → the primary beneficiaries
THE PARENTS                 → secondary beneficiaries (also get sensitized)

Each question targets a DIFFERENT actor. Mixing up who does what leads to wrong answers.

NEW CONCEPT 3: Scope Expansion

The text describes how the initiative GROWS:

PHASE 1: Local ("localisées au départ")
          → Started in one region (north-east Madagascar)

PHASE 2: Regional ("reprises progressivement par d'autres provinces")
          → Spread to other provinces

PHASE 3: Multiplied ("par un effet de répétition et de multiplication")
          → Became more effective through scaling

Questions about the initiative's SUCCESS test whether you can track this expansion.

✅ HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

QUESTION 17: L'association Ormano a pour but principal...

The critical word in the question: "principal" — MAIN purpose. Not just ANY purpose, but the PRIMARY one.

The key phrases in the text:

"non seulement pour préserver ces espaces remarquables, mais aussi pour faire découvrir aux enfants leur environnement"
"cet organisme porte un superbe projet d'éducation à la nature"

Now, how do we determine which objective is PRIMARY?

Method: The "Label Test"

When an organization is DESCRIBED or LABELED, that label reveals its primary identity:

"un superbe projet d'ÉDUCATION À LA NATURE"
                    ↑
                    This is the LABEL

The text calls it an "education project." Not a "preservation project" or an "ecological combat project." The label = the primary purpose.

Method: The "Volume Test"

Count how many sentences are devoted to each objective:

PRESERVATION: mentioned in ONE phrase
   "préserver ces espaces remarquables" (half a sentence)

EDUCATION: developed across MULTIPLE sentences
   "faire découvrir aux enfants leur environnement"
   "projet d'éducation à la nature"
   "Les professeurs accompagnent leurs élèves"
   "leur apprennent à voir et à comprendre"
   "observations reprises dans des fiches, posters, dictionnaires"
   "les parents à participer et être sensibilisés"

Preservation gets half a sentence. Education gets an entire paragraph. The article DWELLS on education. That's the main purpose.

Method: The "non seulement... mais aussi..." Analysis"

This structure has a subtle hierarchy:

"non seulement pour [A], mais aussi pour [B]"
= "not only for [A], but also for [B]"

In French rhetoric, "non seulement... mais aussi..." typically INTRODUCES A to SET UP B. The element after "mais aussi" is often the one the author wants to EMPHASIZE — it's the addition, the surprise, the main point. However, this isn't always the case. The SAFEST way to determine the primary objective is to use the Label Test and Volume Test together.

A — de créer des vocations écologiques"Créer des vocations" = create vocations/callings. The text talks about EDUCATING children about nature, not creating professional ecologists. There's no mention of children pursuing ecology as a career or vocation.❌ Too specific — education ≠ career creation
B — d'éduquer les écoliers à leur milieu naturel"Éduquer les écoliers" = educate schoolchildren. "À leur milieu naturel" = about their natural environment. The text says: "projet d'éducation à la nature" + "faire découvrir aux enfants leur environnement" + teachers who "apprennent à voir et à comprendre." PERFECT PARAPHRASE of the main purpose.✅ CORRECT
C — d'encourager la fabrication de produits naturels"Fabrication de produits naturels" = manufacturing natural products. The text mentions making "fiches, posters, dictionnaires de nature" — these are EDUCATIONAL MATERIALS, not commercial natural products. ✅ Answer: B

🔬 THE "PRIMARY PURPOSE" IDENTIFICATION METHOD

Here's a systematic way to identify the PRIMARY purpose in any organizational text:

TEST 1: THE LABEL
   Does the text explicitly NAME what type of project it is?
   "projet d'ÉDUCATION à la nature" → EDUCATION is primary
   
TEST 2: THE VOLUME
   Which objective gets the most sentences/detail?
   Education → entire paragraph
   Preservation → half a sentence
   → EDUCATION wins by volume

TEST 3: THE ACTORS
   Who are the main ACTORS described?
   Teachers + students → EDUCATION actors
   → EDUCATION confirmed

TEST 4: THE TOOLS
   What tools/outputs are described?
   Fiches, posters, dictionaries → LEARNING tools
   → EDUCATION confirmed

ALL 4 TESTS → EDUCATION = PRIMARY PURPOSE ✅

🚨 GOLDEN RULE #15: When a question asks for the "but principal" (main purpose), use the LABEL TEST first — look for how the text explicitly names or describes the organization's project. Then confirm with the VOLUME TEST — which objective gets the most development in the text.

QUESTION 18: Les professeurs engagés dans cet organisme...

The question targets: THE TEACHERS specifically. Not the association, not the students, not the parents.

The key phrases about teachers:

"Les actions sont directement menées par les professeurs"
"qui accompagnent leurs élèves au cœur de la forêt"
"et leur apprennent à voir et à comprendre l'étroite interdépendance entre toutes les formes de vie"

Let's summarize what teachers DO:

TEACHERS' ACTIONS:
  1. Lead the actions directly ("directement menées par les professeurs")
  2. Accompany students into the forest ("accompagnent leurs élèves")
  3. Teach them to observe and understand ("apprennent à voir et à comprendre")
  
TEACHERS' FOCUS = STUDENTS' LEARNING

Now there's a tricky element — the text ALSO mentions:

"Cette démarche conduit aussi les parents à participer et être sensibilisés"

But notice: the parents participate as a SECONDARY EFFECT ("conduit aussi" = also leads to). The teachers don't directly target parents — parents get involved as a CONSEQUENCE of the children's activities.

TEACHERS → directly work with → STUDENTS (primary focus)
STUDENTS → bring home knowledge → PARENTS get sensitized (secondary effect)

Now let's test each answer:

ChoiceAnalysisVerdict
A — luttent contre la déforestation régionale"Luttent contre la déforestation" = fight against deforestation. While the region is "menacée de défrichement" (threatened by clearing), the teachers' role is EDUCATION, not activism. They don't fight deforestation directly — they TEACH children about nature. The text never describes teachers as environmental activists.❌ Wrong role — teachers educate, they don't fight deforestation
B — organisent des séminaires pour les parents"Séminaires pour les parents" = seminars for parents. The text says parents "participent et sont sensibilisés," but this happens INDIRECTLY ("cette démarche conduit aussi les parents"). Teachers don't organize parent seminars — parents get involved through their children's activities.❌ Wrong mechanism — parents are secondary, not direct targets

"Confectionner" (to make/craft) ≠ "publier" (to publish).❌ Distorts classroom crafts into published works
D — se concentrent sur l'apprentissage des enfants"Se concentrent sur" = focus on. "L'apprentissage des enfants" = children's learning. The text says teachers "accompagnent leurs élèves" and "apprennent à voir et à comprendre." Their PRIMARY focus is teaching children. PARAPHRASE.✅ CORRECT

✅ Answer: D

When the question says "Les professeurs..." → look ONLY at the TEACHERS row.

Wrong answers try to assign actions from OTHER rows to the teachers:

  • Answer C inflates the STUDENTS' classroom materials into published works

🚨 NEW CONCEPT: Actor-Action Matching. When a question names a specific actor, only consider actions the text EXPLICITLY attributes to that actor. Don't transfer actions from one actor to another.

ANSWER:  "suivie par d'autres régions"

Both mean: other geographical areas adopted the same initiative. Type 2 paraphrase.

🎯 WHAT COULD BE DONE

The "Organizational Text" Reading Framework

When you encounter a text about an organization, association, or initiative, organize your reading into five layers:

LAYER 1: CONTEXT — Where and when was it created? What problem does it address?
         → 1999, northeast Madagascar, deforestation threat

LAYER 2: PURPOSE — What is its main objective? Any secondary objectives?
         → Primary: educate children about nature
         → Secondary: preserve natural spaces

LAYER 3: ACTORS — Who does what?
         → Association: creates/carries the project
         → Teachers: lead field activities, teach students
         → Students: observe, create materials
         → Parents: participate indirectly

LAYER 4: METHODS — How does it work?
         → Forest excursions, observation, creating fiches/posters/dictionaries

LAYER 5: RESULTS — What has been achieved? How has it grown?
         → Spread to other provinces, became more effective

Each question typically targets ONE specific layer:

Q17 ("but principal")         → LAYER 2 (PURPOSE)
Q18 ("les professeurs...")    → LAYER 3 (ACTORS)
Q19 ("l'initiative...")       → LAYER 5 (RESULTS)

The "Primary vs. Secondary" Hierarchy Detection

When a text mentions multiple objectives, use this hierarchy test:

PRIMARY OBJECTIVE:
  ✓ Gets the most text space (Volume Test)
  ✓ Is explicitly labeled ("projet d'éducation") (Label Test)
  ✓ Has the most actors and methods described
  ✓ Is the focus of the questions

SECONDARY OBJECTIVE:
  ✓ Mentioned briefly, often in one phrase
  ✓ Introduced with "non seulement" or as context
  ✓ Not developed with specific actors/methods
  ✓ Serves as background for the primary objective

✅ WHAT YOU SHOULD DO

For organizational texts:

  1. Identify the LABEL — how does the text NAME the project? ("projet d'éducation" → education is primary)
  2. Map actors to actions — create a mental table of WHO does WHAT
  3. Distinguish direct from indirect effects — "conduit AUSSI les parents" = parents are a secondary effect, not a primary target
  4. Track scope expansion — look for words like "au départ," "progressivement," "d'autres provinces" that show growth

For "but principal" questions:

  1. Apply the Label Test first (how is the project named?)
  2. Confirm with the Volume Test (which objective gets the most text?)
  3. If "non seulement... mais aussi..." is present, don't automatically assume the first element is primary — test both with the Label and Volume methods

For actor-specific questions:

  1. Read the question's subject carefully — "Les professeurs" means ONLY the teachers
  2. Go to the text and extract only what is said about THAT specific actor
  3. Reject any answer that attributes another actor's actions to the one the question asks about

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL #29: The "Noble But Wrong Purpose" Trap 🌍

Answer A (Q17): "créer des vocations écologiques" (create ecological vocations)
Answer D (Q17): "inciter les autorités à la conservation" (urge authorities to conserve)

Both sound NOBLE and ENVIRONMENTALLY POSITIVE. Both seem like worthy goals for an ecological association. But neither is what the text says.

The text says the association's main purpose is EDUCATION — teaching children to understand their environment. Not creating careers. Not lobbying authorities.

The mechanism: The exam offers answers that are thematically ADJACENT to the text's topic (ecology, environment, conservation) but describe a DIFFERENT specific action than what the text states.

How to avoid it: Don't choose an answer just because it sounds like something an environmental organization WOULD do. Choose the answer that matches what THIS specific organization DOES according to THIS specific text.

This is Rule #3 in its most nuanced form: "True for environmental organizations in general" ≠ "True for this specific organization in this text."

PITFALL #30: The "Direct vs. Indirect" Confusion 🔗

TEXT: "Cette démarche conduit AUSSI les parents à participer"
      → Parents participate as an INDIRECT consequence
      → The teachers' direct action is with STUDENTS

TRAP B (Q18): "organisent des séminaires pour les parents"
              → Implies teachers DIRECTLY target parents

The word "aussi" (also) is the key signal:

"conduit AUSSI les parents" = ALSO leads parents to participate
                   ↑
                 "ALSO" = this is an ADDITIONAL effect, not the main one

How to detect it: Look for these "secondary effect" markers:

French markerMeaningSignal
aussialsoAdditional, not primary
en outrefurthermoreExtra benefit
de plusmoreoverAdded bonus
conduit àleads toIndirect consequence
par conséquentconsequentlyResult, not purpose

When an action is introduced with these markers, it's a SIDE EFFECT — not the main purpose.

PITFALL #31: The "Confectionner ≠ Publier" Vocabulary Trap 📚

TEXT:    "confectionner des dictionnaires de nature"
         = CRAFT/MAKE nature dictionaries (classroom activity)

TRAP C:  "publient des ouvrages sur l'environnement"
         = PUBLISH works about the environment (professional activity)
VerbMeaningContext
confectionnerto make / craft / put togetherHands-on, amateur, educational
publierto publishProfessional, commercial, public distribution

Making a nature dictionary in class with students ≠ publishing a book about the environment. The SCALE and PURPOSE are completely different.

How to avoid it: Pay attention to the LEVEL of the action described. Is it a classroom activity or a professional endeavor? Is it local or public? The answer must match the text's level.

PITFALL #32: The "Invented Official Status" Trap 🏛️

Answer C (Q19): "reconnue d'utilité publique" (recognized as serving public interest)
Answer D (Q19): "subventionnée par le conseil régional" (funded by regional council)

Neither official recognition nor government funding is mentioned ANYWHERE in the text. These are completely fabricated details.

Why students fall for them: Because they sound PLAUSIBLE. An association doing good work MIGHT be officially recognized. It MIGHT receive funding. But the text doesn't say so.

How to avoid it: Apply Rule #3 strictly: if you can't point to a specific line, the answer is wrong. Ask yourself: "Where in the text does it mention official recognition?" If the answer is "nowhere," eliminate it.
