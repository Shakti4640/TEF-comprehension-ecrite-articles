🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

What's different from Projects 1, 2, and 3?

All previous texts presented FACTS — information about a service, medical conditions, or a transport concept. The reader's job was to understand what the text describes.

This text is fundamentally different. It is a FORMAL COMMUNICATION that:

  • ASKS you to do something (fill out a questionnaire)
  • PROMISES you something in return (discounts, free products)
  • REQUIRES certain things (your identity)
  • EXEMPTS you from other things (answering every question)
  • GUARANTEES something (confidentiality)

This means the questions won't just test "What does the text say?" — they'll test "What does the text REQUIRE? What does it OFFER? What is OPTIONAL? What is MANDATORY?"

Step 1: Identify the text type

Quick scan reveals:

  • Direct address to the reader ("vous")
  • A request ("en remplissant le questionnaire ci-joint")
  • Promises and benefits ("vous pourrez bénéficier")
  • Legal-sounding guarantees ("ne pourront en aucun cas")
  • Formal closing ("Nous vous remercions par avance")

Verdict: FORMAL PERSUASIVE LETTER (lettre formelle / communication officielle)

| Formal Letter Feature | What It Means for Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Has a PURPOSE (why the letter exists) | A question will ask WHO benefits or WHY |
| Contains OBLIGATIONS ("il est impératif") | A question will test what is mandatory |
| Contains OPTIONS ("vous n'êtes pas obligé") | A trap will present an option as an obligation |
| Offers BENEFITS ("vous pourrez bénéficier") | A question will ask what you get in return |
| Makes GUARANTEES ("en aucun cas") | A trap may contradict the guarantee |

Step 2: Read questions first — identify the QUESTION TYPES

This is where formal letter questions differ from informational text questions:

QuestionWhat It Really AsksCategory
Q21: "va servir"WHO is the real beneficiary?PURPOSE
Q22: "pour qu'elle soit valable, il faut"What is MANDATORY?OBLIGATION
Q23: "en échange"What do you GET?BENEFIT
Q24: "est effectué pour"What is the GOAL?PURPOSE (deeper)

Notice: Q21 and Q24 BOTH ask about purpose — but from different angles. Q21 asks WHO benefits, Q24 asks WHAT the goal is. The exam tests the same concept twice to see if you're consistent.

Step 3: The "Obligation Spectrum" technique (NEW)

Formal letters contain statements on a spectrum from MANDATORY to FORBIDDEN. Before answering, classify each statement:

THE OBLIGATION SPECTRUM:

🔴 MANDATORY     "il est impératif que..."     → MUST do
🟡 CONDITIONAL   "si vous... vous pourrez..."  → IF you do X, THEN you get Y
🟢 OPTIONAL      "vous n'êtes pas obligé(e)"   → CAN skip
🔵 GUARANTEED    "en aucun cas"                → WILL NOT happen
⚫ FORBIDDEN     (not present here)            → MUST NOT do

Now let me map EVERY statement in the text to this spectrum:

StatementSpectrum LevelExact Quote

This map is your master key for this text. Every question can be answered by checking where the relevant information falls on this spectrum.

🏆 GOLDEN RULE #12: In formal letters and official communications, always map statements on the Obligation Spectrum (mandatory → conditional → optional → guaranteed → forbidden). The exam will test whether you confuse one level with another.

QUESTION 21: Cette enquête va servir...

What the question really asks: Who is the PRIMARY BENEFICIARY of this survey?

The key sentence:

"vous pourrez aider les entreprises à mieux cibler les besoins et les attentes des consommateurs"

Option B: "aux publicitaires"

The text DOES mention advertising: "ne pourront en aucun cas être utilisées ou divulguées à de tierces personnes à des fins publicitaires"

But read carefully — this sentence says the OPPOSITE. It GUARANTEES that responses will NOT be used for advertising. The word "publicitaires" appears in a NEGATION context.

The text mentions cosmetics once: "Tous les nouveaux produits sur le marché des cosmétiques vous seront automatiquement offerts"

But this is a BENEFIT offered to respondents — a perk for participating. The survey itself is not ABOUT cosmetics specifically, nor does it serve cosmetologists. "Cosmétiques" appears in the rewards section, not the purpose section.

The question asks "pour qu'elle soit valable" (for it to be valid) — this targets the MANDATORY level.

Only ONE thing is mandatory: providing identity and address.

Now let's test:

Option A: "répondre à toutes les questions"

The text says: "Vous n'êtes pas obligé(e) de répondre à toutes les questions"

This is EXPLICITLY marked as 🟢 OPTIONAL. The answer presents it as mandatory. This is a spectrum inversion trap — flipping optional to mandatory.

The text even elaborates: "Si vous n'avez pas d'avis particulier sur telle ou telle question, passez à la suivante" — you can SKIP questions.

❌ ELIMINATED — The text explicitly says this is NOT required

Option B: "garder votre anonymat"

The text says: "il est impératif que vous remplissiez la case « fiche signalétique » en mentionnant votre identité et votre domiciliation"

"Identité et domiciliation" = your NAME and ADDRESS. This means you must REVEAL your identity — the exact OPPOSITE of anonymity.

"Garder votre anonymat" (keep your anonymity) contradicts the mandatory requirement to identify yourself.

This is a direct opposition trap — the answer states the exact opposite of what the text requires.

❌ ELIMINATED — Direct opposite of the mandatory requirement

Option C: "donner votre nom et adresse"

The text says: "mentionnant votre identité et votre domiciliation"

Text saysOption C saysRelationship
"votre identité""votre nom"PARAPHRASE ✅
"votre domiciliation""votre adresse"PARAPHRASE ✅

"Identité" → "nom" (your identity = your name)
"Domiciliation" → "adresse" (your domicile = your address)

Both are perfect paraphrases. Golden Rule #2 confirmed — correct answers rephrase.

✅ CORRECT — Double paraphrase of the mandatory requirement

Option D: "la renvoyer rapidement"

The text says: "si vous nous le renvoyez dans les délais indiqués"

Two problems:

Problem 1: "Dans les délais indiqués" = within the indicated deadlines. This is NOT "rapidement" (quickly). A deadline could be 6 months from now — that's not "quickly." The answer exaggerates the time constraint.

Problem 2: Returning the survey within the deadline is a 🟡 CONDITIONAL requirement (it gets you benefits), not a 🔴 MANDATORY one (it doesn't make the survey valid). The question asks about VALIDITY, not about getting rewards.

This is the Obligation Spectrum trap — confusing CONDITIONAL with MANDATORY.

❌ ELIMINATED — Wrong spectrum level (conditional, not mandatory) + exaggeration of time constraint

🎯 Answer: C — donner votre nom et adresse

This question is a masterclass in spectrum awareness. All four options correspond to REAL elements in the text:

✅ WHAT YOU SHOULD DO

For Formal Letters and Official Communications Specifically:

1. Identify the REAL purpose behind the stated purpose.

Formal letters often have a surface purpose (what they say) and a deep purpose (what they actually want):

"Help us understand why products fail""Give us your data so companies can sell more effectively"

The questions may target either level. Q21 targets the deep purpose (who benefits). Q24 targets the surface purpose (what the stated goal is).

2. Map the Obligation Spectrum BEFORE answering.

This was introduced in the approach section. Don't just read the text — actively classify each statement as mandatory/conditional/optional/guaranteed.

3. Pay attention to SIGNAL WORDS that reveal obligation levels.

Signal Word/ExpressionObligation LevelExample from Text

4. Watch for "pourrez" vs. "pourriez" vs. "pouvez".

These three forms express different levels of certainty:

FormMeaningCertainty Level
"vous pouvez" (present)you canHIGH — current ability
"vous pourrez" (future)you will be able toMEDIUM — future possibility
"vous pourriez" (conditional)you could/mightLOW — hypothetical

The text uses "pourrez" — a future possibility, not a guarantee. The correct answer (B) preserves this with "pourrez bénéficier."

5. Separate the BENEFITS section from the PURPOSE section.

In this text:

  • Purpose = Paragraph 1 (why the survey exists)
  • Benefits = Middle section (what you get for participating)
  • Conditions = Scattered throughout (what you must/can do)

A question about purpose must be answered from the purpose section. A question about benefits must be answered from the benefits section. Don't cross-contaminate. (Rule #3)

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL 11: The Obligation Spectrum Inversion (NEW)

First introduced here. Will reappear in Projects 9, 16, 24.

What it is: An answer presents an OPTIONAL element as MANDATORY, or a CONDITIONAL element as ABSOLUTE.

How to detect: Before choosing, find the statement in the text and identify its signal word. "Il est impératif" = mandatory. "Vous n'êtes pas obligé" = optional. "Si vous..." = conditional. Then check: does the answer respect this level?

In this text: Q22 Option A claims answering all questions is required. The text says "vous n'êtes pas obligé(e)."

Defense: Build the Obligation Spectrum map before answering.

PITFALL 12: The Negation Extraction (NEW)

First introduced here. Will reappear in Projects 14, 21, 23.

What it is: A word that appears inside a NEGATIVE construction in the text is extracted and used POSITIVELY in an answer.

How to detect: When you see a familiar text word in an answer, go BACK and check: "Was this word inside a ne...pas / ne...jamais / en aucun cas construction?"

In this text: "Publicitaires" appears in "ne pourront en aucun cas...à des fins publicitaires." Option B (Q21) and Option C (Q24) exploit this.

Defense: Treat every negation word as a RED FLAG. If the text says "NOT X," any answer that claims "X" is almost certainly wrong.

PITFALL 13: The Role Confusion (NEW — named here, trap type from Q21)

First introduced here. Will reappear in Projects 10, 17, 23.

What it is: The text mentions multiple actors (source, beneficiary, subject). An answer swaps their roles.

How to detect: For each actor mentioned, ask: "What is this person's ROLE in the action?"

In this text:

  • YOU = data provider (source)
  • ENTERPRISES = beneficiaries (use the data)
  • CONSUMERS = subject (being studied)

Defense: Draw the actor map before answering purpose questions.

🌉 BRIDGE TO Chapter 5

Projects 1-4 all dealt with texts that were either factual (describing something) or functional (asking you to do something). The author's PERSONAL OPINION was absent — the texts were neutral or promotional.

Project 5 (Dans la Chaleur du Mélodrame) introduces the most subjective text type you'll encounter: a FILM REVIEW where the author expresses personal opinions.

You will learn:

  • How to detect positive, negative, and nuanced opinions
  • How to separate the plot summary from the critic's judgment
  • How to read tone vocabulary (irony, enthusiasm, reservation)
  • Why the question "What does the author THINK?" is fundamentally different from "What does the text SAY?"

The difficulty shifts from "understanding information" to "understanding opinion."
