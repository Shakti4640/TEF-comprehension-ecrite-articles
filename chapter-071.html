QUESTION 2: Une seule de ces affirmations est vraie. Laquelle ?

The skill: DETAIL VERIFICATION

This is the most dangerous question type in the exam. You must check FOUR statements against the text and find the ONE that is true. Three are false — but they are designed to SEEM true.

Method: Test each statement as if it were on trial. The text is the EVIDENCE. If the text doesn't EXPLICITLY support it, it's FALSE.

OPTION A: "L'alpinisme a vu le jour avec les débuts du tourisme."
(Alpinism was born with the beginnings of tourism.)

The evidence:

"Ce n'est qu'à la fin du XIXe siècle que se développera une véritable économie touristique poussant les agriculteurs des vallées à se transformer, l'été, en guides conduisant pour de l'argent les sportifs de l'époque... Alors commença la longue histoire de l'alpinisme."

Let's decode this sentence surgically:

ElementMeaning
"Ce n'est qu'à la fin du XIXe siècle"Only at the end of the 19th century
"que se développera une véritable économie touristique"a real tourism economy developed
"poussant les agriculteurs... à se transformer en guides"pushing farmers to become guides
"Alors commença la longue histoire de l'alpinisme"THEN began the long history of alpinism

The word "Alors" is the key. It means "THEN" — establishing a direct chronological link: tourism developed → THEN alpinism began.

Verdict: TRUE ✅ — Alpinism was indeed born with the development of tourism.

OPTION B: "De tout temps, des hommes illustres ont péri dans les Alpes."
(Throughout history, illustrious men have perished in the Alps.)

The evidence check:

  • Hannibal → crossed the Alps. The text says he was FASCINATED, not that he died there.
  • Bonaparte → same: fascination, not death.
  • The English reporter in 1865 → reached the summit. Four men died on descent, but they are NOT described as "illustrious."
  • 500 victims of Mont Cervin → described as "audacieux" (bold), not "illustres" (illustrious/famous).

Two problems:

  1. "De tout temps" (throughout history) → The text shows deaths only from the alpinism era (post-1865), not "throughout history"
  2. "Hommes illustres" → The victims are not described as famous/illustrious

Verdict: FALSE ❌ — The text does NOT say famous people died in the Alps throughout all of history.

OPTION C: "À la fascination pour la montagne a succédé une période de terreur."
(Fascination for the mountain was followed by a period of terror.)

The evidence check:
The text shows:

  • Fascination (Hannibal → painters → fashionable people) → YES
  • Then: tourism → alpinism → exploits AND tragedies → YES
  • "Épouvantail" (scarecrow) → suggests fear → BUT...

The text NEVER describes a "period of terror." It describes a coexistence of fascination and danger:

"il fait à la fois figure de mythe et d'épouvantail"

"À la fois" = AT THE SAME TIME. Myth AND scarecrow exist SIMULTANEOUSLY, not sequentially. The text does NOT say fascination was REPLACED by terror.

Verdict: FALSE ❌ — Fascination and danger coexist; terror did not SUCCEED fascination.

CRITICAL DISTINCTION:

What Option C claimsWhat the text actually says
Fascination → THEN → terror (sequential)Fascination AND danger AT THE SAME TIME (simultaneous)

This is a temporal logic trap: turning a simultaneous relationship into a sequential one.

OPTION D: "Des mulets furent utilisés pour les premières randonnées en montagne."
(Mules were used for the first mountain hikes.)

The evidence:

"les gens à la mode, qui se feront porter, quelquefois à dos d'homme sur les pentes du Mont Blanc"

What Option D saysWhat the text says
Mulets (mules)À dos d'homme (on men's backs)
Used for randonnées (hikes)Fashionable people were CARRIED (not hiking)

Two substitutions:

  1. "Mulets" replaces "homme" → animal substituted for human
  2. "Randonnées" replaces "se feront porter" → active hiking substituted for passive carrying

Verdict: FALSE ❌ — It was MEN carrying people, not MULES. And they weren't hiking, they were being carried.

This is a classic Subtle Substitution Trap (Golden Rule #1 from Project 1), but at Phase 4 difficulty because the substitution involves an entire scenario change.

🎯 Answer: A

THE LESSON — The "One True Statement" Protocol

🏆 GOLDEN RULE #13: When the question says "one statement is true," treat ALL four options as POTENTIALLY FALSE. Apply the same level of skepticism to every option. The true one will survive scrutiny; the others will crumble under one specific word.

The Crumble Points:

Notice: each false option is destroyed by ONE or TWO specific words. The rest of the sentence sounds plausible. The exam buries the lie inside reasonable-sounding language.

QUESTION 3: La particularité du Mont Cervin est...

The skill: SYNTHESIS — combining multiple details into one conclusion

The Mont Cervin section contains a CASCADE of information:

MONT CERVIN DATA:
├── Called "la plus belle montagne du monde"
├── 500 victims
├── "Mythe et épouvantail" (myth and scarecrow)
├── 1865: English reporter reaches summit → 4 die on descent
├── Louis Carrel: local, conquers South and East faces
├── 1932: Schmid brothers → North face
└── 1965: Bonatti → North face, ALONE, in WINTER

The question asks for its "particularité" — what makes it SPECIAL. Let's test each option against this data:

OPTION A: "que tous les hommes qui s'y sont attaqués sont partis en cordée"
(that all men who attempted it went in rope teams)

The text says:

Walter Bonatti parvint... à la même prouesse, mais cette fois-ci seul et en hiver.

"SEUL" = ALONE. Bonatti climbed WITHOUT a rope team. This directly contradicts "tous... en cordée."

Verdict: FALSE ❌ — Bonatti went ALONE. "Tous" (all) is destroyed by one counterexample.

OPTION B: "que ce mont a particulièrement résisté à l'assaut des alpinistes"
(that this mountain particularly resisted the assault of alpinists)

The evidence — a SYNTHESIS of multiple details:

DetailWhat it shows
500 victimsThe mountain KILLS — it fights back
"Épouvantail" (scarecrow)It FRIGHTENS — it resists psychologically
1865: first summit, but 4 die on descentEven SUCCESS comes with death
North face not conquered until 1932 (67 years later)The mountain HELD for decades
Solo winter ascent seemed "irréaliste" until 1965Even the idea of full conquest seemed impossible

All these details converge on ONE conclusion: the Mont Cervin RESISTED human attempts more than most mountains.

This is a synthesis answer — no single sentence in the text says "the mountain resisted." But the ACCUMULATION of evidence (500 deaths, decades between conquests, the word "épouvantail") creates this meaning.

Verdict: TRUE ✅

OPTION C: "qu'il a fallu attendre 1932 pour qu'un alpiniste en revienne vivant"
(that one had to wait until 1932 for an alpinist to return alive)

The text says:

"dès 1865, son sommet fut atteint par un reporter anglais"

The reporter REACHED the summit in 1865 and survived (four others died on descent, but HE survived — his "exploitation connut une extraordinaire publicité," meaning he was alive to enjoy the publicity).

Also Louis Carrel conquered faces "quelque temps plus tard" (shortly after 1865) and survived.

Verdict: FALSE ❌ — People survived the mountain well before 1932.

OPTION D: "que seuls les montagnards de naissance furent capables d'en gravir les flancs"
(that only native mountain people were capable of climbing its flanks)

The text says:

  • The FIRST person to reach the summit was "un reporter anglais" — an ENGLISH reporter, not a native mountaineer
  • The Schmid brothers and Bonatti are not described as native mountain people
  • Only Louis Carrel is described as "homme du pays" (local man)

Verdict: FALSE ❌ — The very FIRST summit was by a foreigner.

🎯 Answer: B

THE LESSON — Synthesis Answers

PHASE 4 SKILL: Sometimes the correct answer is NOT a paraphrase of a single sentence. It is the LOGICAL CONCLUSION that emerges from COMBINING multiple pieces of evidence.

Answer typeHow it worksExample
Phase 1-3: Direct paraphraseOne sentence → one answer"succès grandissant" → "constamment augmenté"
Phase 4: SynthesisMultiple details → one conclusion500 victims + épouvantail + decades of attempts → "particularly resisted"

How to recognize a synthesis question:

  • No single sentence in the text matches the answer
  • The answer uses a GENERAL word ("particulièrement résisté") that summarizes SPECIFIC details
  • You feel like the answer is "sort of everywhere" in the text but "nowhere specifically"

When this happens: TRUST THE ACCUMULATION. If multiple details all point to the same conclusion, that conclusion is correct even if no single sentence states it explicitly.

THE LESSON — Decoding Literary Quotations

This is a Phase 4 mastery skill that will recur in Projects 22-25. Here is the method:

THE QUOTE DECODING PROTOCOL:

Step 1: ISOLATE the quote from its context
         → Read ONLY the words between « »
         
Step 2: TRANSLATE each key word
         → rapport = link
         → beauté = beauty  
         → inaccessibilité = what cannot be reached
         → indissoluble = inseparable
         
Step 3: RECONSTRUCT the meaning in plain language
         → "Beauty and unreachability are permanently linked"
         
Step 4: IDENTIFY what the quote is ABOUT
         → Is it about BEAUTY? EFFORT? MOTIVATION? PSYCHOLOGY?
         → This quote is about THE NATURE OF BEAUTY
         
Step 5: ELIMINATE options that SHIFT the subject
         → Any answer about effort, motivation, or psychology = WRONG
         → Only an answer about beauty's relationship to unreachability = RIGHT

🏆 GOLDEN RULE #14: When interpreting a quote, the correct answer preserves the SUBJECT of the quote. If the quote is about beauty, the answer must be about beauty. Options that shift to human effort, motivation, or psychology are traps — they sound inspiring but miss the point.

The trap mechanism in quote questions:

The exam offers answers that are philosophically inspiring but thematically off-target. All four options sound like something a wise person might say about mountains. But only ONE actually matches what Rebuffat SAID.

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL 10: The Sequential-Simultaneous Confusion (NEW — Phase 4)

What it is: The text describes two things happening AT THE SAME TIME ("à la fois"), but a false answer presents them as happening IN SEQUENCE ("a succédé").

In this text (Q2, Option C):

  • Text: "il fait à la fois figure de mythe et d'épouvantail" (simultaneously myth AND scarecrow)
  • Option C: "À la fascination... a succédé une période de terreur" (fascination was FOLLOWED BY terror)

The temporal logic shift:

Text's structureOption C's structure
A AND B simultaneouslyA THEN B sequentially
"à la fois""a succédé"

How to detect: Watch for "à la fois," "en même temps," "simultanément" in the text. If an answer converts these to "après," "ensuite," "a succédé," it's a temporal logic trap.

PITFALL 11: The Synthesis Trap — "I can't find the exact sentence" (NEW — Phase 4)

What it is: You search for a sentence that says "the mountain resisted" and can't find it. So you doubt the answer.

In this text (Q3, Option B):
No single sentence says "le Mont Cervin a résisté." But:

  • 500 victims → resistance
  • "Épouvantail" → resistance
  • Decades between conquests → resistance
  • "Irréaliste" → resistance

The accumulated evidence is overwhelming, even without a single matching sentence.

How to handle it:

When no single sentence matches but MULTIPLE details converge on one meaning → TRUST THE CONVERGENCE.

This is the difference between Phase 1-3 reading (find the sentence) and Phase 4 reading (build the conclusion from evidence).

PITFALL 12: The Subject-Shift in Quote Interpretation (NEW — Phase 4)

What it is: All four options sound philosophical and mountain-related, but only one preserves the SUBJECT of the actual quote.

In this text (Q4):

Options A, C, D shift from "beauty's nature" to "human behavior." Only B stays on "beauty's nature."

The detection method: After reading the quote, ask: "What is this sentence ABOUT — is it defining something, describing an action, or explaining motivation?"
