La ville est-elle l'avenir de l'homme ?

Avec une population mondiale déjà à 50% urbaine et prévue à 70% en 2050, le développement durable est un enjeu fondamental de l'évolution des villes. ... (36) ... Son empreinte écologique doit être la plus réduite possible pour atténuer le réchauffement climatique. Il faut contrôler le degré de pollution, le recyclage des déchets, adopter des modes de consommation et de production responsables. ... (37) ... Et même après. D'où l'intérêt de construire avec des matériaux recyclables. Les bâtiments à haute qualité environnementale ont aussi l'intérêt d'améliorer le bien-être de ceux qui les habitent.

... (38) ... : faire en sorte que les habitants s'épanouissent dans leur ville. ... (39) ... La réflexion sur les transports est donc un des axes majeurs d'une politique de développement durable.

Entre le bruit, la pollution, les risques d'accident et la consommation énergétique, ... (40) ... Mais doit-on pour autant la rejeter complètement ?

Before diving into individual gaps, understand the global argument:

CONTEXT:     World is becoming urban → sustainable development matters
PARAGRAPH 1: Environmental criteria of a sustainable city

🧩 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

QUESTION 36 — The Rhetorical Question Gap

The landscape:

BEFORE the gapTHE GAPAFTER the gap
"le développement durable est un enjeu fondamental de l'évolution des villes."??? (36)"Son empreinte écologique doit être la plus réduite possible..."

Step 1: Identify what AFTER needs.

The word "Son" (Its) is a possessive pronoun. It must refer to something introduced in the gap. What has an "ecological footprint"?

  • A city? Yes → "Son empreinte écologique" = the city's ecological footprint
  • A politician? No → politicians don't have ecological footprints in this context
  • Demographics? No → demographic control doesn't have a footprint

So the gap must introduce a city or the concept of a sustainable city.

Step 2: Identify what BEFORE sets up.

The sentence before says sustainable development is a fundamental challenge for cities. What logically follows? A question: "What makes a city sustainable?" or a statement defining the criteria.

Step 3: Test each option.

OptionDoes it introduce a "city" for "Son"?Does it logically follow the setup?Does it lead to the criteria list?
A) Quels sont les critères d'une cité durable ?✅ "cité durable" → "Son" = its✅ "Sustainable development is fundamental" → "What are the criteria?" is natural✅ A question introduces the list of criteria that follows
B) Le rôle des politiciens est au cœur de cette question.❌ "Son" would refer to politicians' footprint? Nonsensical❌ Introduces politicians — not mentioned before or after❌ Doesn't lead to ecological criteria
C) Pourquoi est-il si peu considéré dans les métropoles ?❌ "il" = unclear referent, and "Son" after the gap creates double pronoun confusion❌ "Why is it so little considered?" implies neglect — but the text is EXPLAINING, not complaining❌ Doesn't set up criteria
D) Un contrôle démographique s'impose dans les grandes villes.❌ "Son" would refer to demographic control's footprint? Illogical❌ Demographic control is never mentioned in the rest of the text❌ Derails the argument

✅ Answer: A

🔑 New technique learned: RHETORICAL QUESTION AS STRUCTURAL DEVICE

In French argumentative texts, a rhetorical question placed after a general statement serves as a bridge between the statement and the detailed answer that follows:

GENERAL STATEMENT → RHETORICAL QUESTION → DETAILED ANSWER

"Sustainable development matters" → "What are the criteria?" → "Ecological footprint, pollution, recycling..."

This is a pattern you will encounter frequently. When the text after a gap contains a list of specifics, check if one of the options is a question that would INTRODUCE that list.

QUESTION 37 — The Temporal Chain Gap

The landscape:

BEFORE the gapTHE GAPAFTER the gap
"...adopter des modes de consommation et de production responsables."??? (37)"Et même après. D'où l'intérêt de construire avec des matériaux recyclables."

Step 1: Decode the anchor phrase — "Et même après."

This is the KEY. "Et même après" means "And even after."

After WHAT? The gap must establish a time period that "et même après" extends beyond.

Think of it this way:

GAP:           [Something happens during PERIOD X]
AFTER THE GAP: "And even after [PERIOD X]."

So the gap must contain a phrase that defines a duration, a lifespan, a period.

Step 2: Test each option for temporal logic.

OptionContains a time period?Does "Et même après" extend it?Does it lead to "matériaux recyclables"?
A) Le recyclage quotidien est en train de devenir une habitude.❌ No duration — describes a current trend"Even after a habit"? NonsensicalWeak — recycling is mentioned but not as a cause for recyclable materials
B) Il y a toujours eu et il y aura toujours différentes formes de pollution.❌ Eternal time — "always" — cannot be extended by "even after""Even after always"? ImpossibleWeak — pollution is a problem, not a cause for recyclable materials
C) Une construction a un impact sur l'environnement durant toute sa vie.✅ "durant toute sa vie" = a building's entire lifespan"And even after [its life]" = even after demolition ✅✅ If impact continues after demolition → recyclable materials make sense
D) Avant de commencer à construire, il faut lister les matériaux nécessaires.❌ "Avant" (before) — refers to a moment, not a duration"Even after listing materials"? Doesn't connectWeak — administrative step, not environmental impact

Step 3: Read the full reconstructed passage.

"Une construction a un impact sur l'environnement durant toute sa vie. Et même après. D'où l'intérêt de construire avec des matériaux recyclables."

Translation: "A building impacts the environment throughout its entire life. And even after. Hence the importance of building with recyclable materials."

The logic is perfect:

  • A building pollutes during its life → AND EVEN AFTER it's demolished → THEREFORE use recyclable materials so the impact after demolition is minimized.

✅ Answer: C

🔑 New technique learned: TEMPORAL CHAIN COMPLETION

When you see time-extension phrases like "et même après," "et bien au-delà," "depuis lors," the gap must establish the initial time frame that these phrases extend, modify, or continue.

GAP establishes: TIME PERIOD X
Text after gap:  "And even after/beyond X"

Always ask: "After WHAT?" — the answer is in the gap.

QUESTION 38 — The Colon Definition Gap

The landscape:

BEFORE the gapTHE GAPAFTER the gap
"...améliorer le bien-être de ceux qui les habitent."??? (38)*": faire en sorte que les habitants s'épanouissent dans leur ville."

Step 1: Decode the COLON.

This is the single most important clue for this gap. The colon ( : ) in French serves a very specific function:

CONCEPT : DEFINITION/EXPLANATION

"C'est une composante essentielle : faire en sorte que les habitants s'épanouissent"
= "It's an essential component: [namely,] ensuring inhabitants thrive"

The gap states the abstract concept. The text after the colon provides the concrete explanation.

So the gap must state something ABSTRACT that is then DEFINED as "ensuring inhabitants thrive in their city."

Step 2: Test each option.

OptionStates an abstract concept?Can ": faire en sorte que les habitants s'épanouissent" define it?Grammatically smooth?
A) Et c'est bien là le dilemme de toutes les grandes métropoles"Dilemme" = a choice between two options. But what follows isn't a dilemma — it's a single goal❌ Thriving inhabitants isn't a dilemma, it's an objectiveGrammatically OK but semantically wrong
B) Le confort des habitants, dont la meilleure recette est la suivante"Recette" (recipe) is informal and "la suivante" (the following) is redundant with the colon❌ Clunky — "the best recipe is the following: ensure inhabitants thrive" is awkward and doesn't connect to "développement durable"Stylistically poor
C) C'est même une composante essentielle du développement durable"Composante essentielle" = abstract concept that can be defined✅ "It's an essential component of sustainable development: ensuring inhabitants thrive in their city" → PERFECT✅ Smooth, formal, connects back to the main theme
D) Et même les bâtiments ne respectant pas les normes doivent garantir ceciIntroduces non-compliant buildings — a completely new and unrelated topic❌ "Buildings that don't meet standards must guarantee this: that inhabitants thrive" — contradictory logicGrammatically forced

Step 3: Verify the transition from the previous paragraph.

The previous paragraph ended with: "Les bâtiments à haute qualité environnementale ont aussi l'intérêt d'améliorer le bien-être de ceux qui les habitent."

This sentence is the bridge. It transitions from environmental criteria (ecological footprint, pollution) to human well-being. Gap 38 then ELEVATES this idea:

"Buildings improve well-being... It's even an essential component of sustainable development: ensuring inhabitants thrive in their city."

The word "même" (even) in option C signals escalation: well-being isn't just a nice bonus — it's an essential component. This is exactly the rhetorical move the text needs.

✅ Answer: C

🔑 New technique learned: THE COLON AS A GAP-SOLVING WEAPON

In French academic and journalistic writing, the colon performs three roles:

Colon functionPatternExample
DefinitionABSTRACT CONCEPT : concrete explanation"Un enjeu majeur : réduire les émissions"
EnumerationCATEGORY : list of items"Trois critères : pollution, recyclage, consommation"
ElaborationSTATEMENT : detailed expansion"Le résultat est clair : les ventes ont doublé"

When a gap appears BEFORE a colon, the gap must contain the general/abstract element. When a gap appears AFTER a colon, the gap must contain the specific/concrete element.

This text uses the definition function. The gap states the concept ("essential component of sustainable development") and the colon introduces its concrete meaning ("ensuring inhabitants thrive").

QUESTION 39 — The Backward Reasoning Gap

The landscape:

BEFORE the gapTHE GAPAFTER the gap
"...faire en sorte que les habitants s'épanouissent dans leur ville."??? (39)"La réflexion sur les transports est donc un des axes majeurs d'une politique de développement durable."

Step 1: Decode "donc" — the backward reasoning trigger.

The word "donc" (therefore) is a conclusion marker. It means:

CAUSE (in the gap) → DONC → CONCLUSION (transports are a major axis)

So the gap must contain a REASON that makes transport policy a logical conclusion.

Ask yourself: "Why would transport be a major axis of sustainable development?" Because of something stated in the gap.

Step 2: What logically connects inhabitant well-being to transport?

The text before the gap says inhabitants should "thrive in their city." The text after says transport is a major axis. What's the logical bridge?

If inhabitants are to thrive → they need to reclaim their streets/public spaces → THEREFORE transport policy matters.

Step 3: Test each option.

OptionCreates a logical CAUSE for "donc... transports"?Connects to "s'épanouissent dans leur ville"?
A) Un sentiment qui passe par une reconquête de la citoyenneté."Reconquering citizenship" → leads to transport? Very indirect and abstract. Citizenship is about rights and participation, not streets.Weak connection
B) Cela implique une solidarité à toute épreuve."Unwavering solidarity" → leads to transport? No logical link whatsoever.No connection
C) Un épanouissement que l'essor des loisirs favorise largement."Leisure development" → leads to transport? Possible but forced. And it would make transport a consequence of leisure, not of urban well-being.Tangential
D) Cela passe en général par une réappropriation des rues."Reappropriation of streets" → THEREFORE transport is a major axis ✅ If people reclaim streets, you MUST rethink how transport works in the city.✅ Perfect: thriving in the city → reclaiming streets → rethinking transport

Step 4: Read the full reconstructed passage.

"C'est même une composante essentielle du développement durable : faire en sorte que les habitants s'épanouissent dans leur ville. Cela passe en général par une réappropriation des rues. La réflexion sur les transports est donc un des axes majeurs d'une politique de développement durable."

Translation: "It's even an essential component of sustainable development: ensuring inhabitants thrive in their city. This generally involves reclaiming the streets. Transport policy is therefore a major axis of sustainable development."

The chain is flawless:

Thriving → Reclaiming streets → THEREFORE → Transport matters

✅ Answer: D

🔑 New technique learned: BACKWARD REASONING FROM "DONC"

In Chapter 16, you learned to read FORWARD: gap → what follows. Here you learn to read BACKWARD: what follows → what the gap must contain.

When you see "donc," "c'est pourquoi," "d'où," "par conséquent," "voilà pourquoi" AFTER a gap, you must work in reverse:

Step 1: Read the CONCLUSION after "donc"
Step 2: Ask "What would CAUSE this conclusion?"
Step 3: The gap must contain that cause

This is the opposite of the Chapter 16 method where you primarily analyzed what came BEFORE the gap. In Phase 4, you must be able to reason in BOTH directions simultaneously.

QUESTION 40 — The Pronoun Tracking Gap

The landscape:

BEFORE the gapTHE GAPAFTER the gap
"Entre le bruit, la pollution, les risques d'accident et la consommation énergétique,"??? (40)"Mais doit-on pour autant la rejeter complètement ?"

Step 1: Decode "la rejeter" — the pronoun clue.

"La" is a feminine singular pronoun. "Rejeter" means "to reject." So the sentence asks: "But should we completely reject IT (feminine)?"

The gap must introduce a feminine singular noun that "la" refers to.

Step 2: What connects to "bruit, pollution, accidents, consommation énergétique"?

These are all negative consequences of cars in cities: noise, pollution, accident risks, energy consumption. The gap must name the source of these problems.

Step 3: Test each option for pronoun agreement.

OptionContains a feminine singular noun?That noun is the source of noise/pollution/accidents?"Mais doit-on la rejeter?" makes sense?
A) La ville est devenue le théâtre de la démesure."La ville" = feminine ✅But the city isn't the SOURCE of these problems — it's the SETTING. You don't "reject the city" in an article about making cities better.❌ Rejecting the city contradicts the entire article's premise
B) La voiture ne peut plus être le nerf de la ville."La voiture" = feminine ✅YES — cars cause noise, pollution, accidents, energy consumption ✅✅ "The car can no longer be the backbone of the city. But should we reject it entirely?" → Balanced argument
C) La mobilité écologique peine à se faire une place."La mobilité" = feminine ✅But ecological mobility is the SOLUTION, not the problem. Why would you list car-related problems then mention ecological mobility struggling?❌ "Should we reject ecological mobility?" → Makes no sense — the article promotes it
D) La mobilisation pour une ville verte semble vouée à l'échec."La mobilisation" = feminine ✅Mobilization for a green city is a positive effort — rejecting it contradicts the article❌ "Should we reject green mobilization?" → Absurd in context

Step 4: Verify with "Mais" — the concession marker.

"Mais" (But) signals a counter-argument. The structure is:

PROBLEM STATED → Mais → NUANCED POSITION
"The car has all these problems" → "But should we reject it completely?"

This is a classic balanced argument structure: acknowledge the problem, then suggest moderation rather than elimination. Option B creates this structure perfectly.

Step 5: Read the full reconstructed passage.

"Entre le bruit, la pollution, les risques d'accident et la consommation énergétique, la voiture ne peut plus être le nerf de la ville. Mais doit-on pour autant la rejeter complètement ?"

Translation: "Between the noise, pollution, accident risks, and energy consumption, the car can no longer be the backbone of the city. But should we therefore reject it entirely?"

✅ Answer: B

🔑 New technique learned: PRONOUN TRACKING ACROSS GAPS

This is one of the most powerful techniques for texte à trous. When the text AFTER a gap contains a pronoun (la, le, les, lui, en, y, celui-ci), you must:

Step 1: Identify the pronoun (la = feminine singular)
Step 2: The gap MUST contain the noun this pronoun replaces
Step 3: Verify gender AND number AND semantic logic

In this case, ALL four options contained feminine nouns — so gender alone couldn't solve it. You needed semantic logic (which feminine noun is the source of the listed problems?) to choose correctly.

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

Pitfall 1: Confusing a Rhetorical Question with a Real Question

What happens: You see a question mark in the options and think it doesn't belong in the middle of a text — questions are for beginnings or endings.

Why it fails: French argumentative writing frequently uses rhetorical questions as structural hinges that pivot from a general statement to specific evidence:

"Sustainable development matters. [WHAT ARE THE CRITERIA?] Its ecological footprint must be reduced..."

🔑 Rule from Chapter 16 still applies, with extension: Connectors bridge clauses. Rhetorical questions bridge PARAGRAPHS or ARGUMENT LAYERS.

Pitfall 2: The "Sounds Smart But Doesn't Connect" Trap

What happens: An option uses impressive vocabulary that seems appropriate for the topic, but creates no logical bridge.

Example: Q39A — "Un sentiment qui passe par une reconquête de la citoyenneté." This sounds intellectually sophisticated. "Reconquering citizenship" seems relevant to urban life. But it doesn't create a logical CAUSE for "donc, transport is a major axis."

Why it fails: The exam tests LOGIC, not vocabulary recognition. An option can sound perfect in isolation and fail completely in context.

🔑 Rule: Always test the option WITHIN the sentence, not as a standalone statement. Insert it, read the full passage, and ask: "Does the logic flow?"

Pitfall 3: Forgetting to Check What "Mais" Nuances

What happens: You pick a moderate or positive option for the gap before "Mais," creating a contradiction.

Example: If you picked Q40C ("La mobilité écologique peine à se faire une place"), then "Mais doit-on la rejeter?" would mean "Should we reject ecological mobility?" — which contradicts the article's pro-sustainability stance.

Why it fails: "Mais" introduces a COUNTER-POSITION. The gap before "Mais" must state something STRONG or NEGATIVE that "Mais" then softens.

STRONG NEGATIVE (gap) → Mais → NUANCE
"The car can't be the backbone" → "But should we reject it entirely?"

If the gap is already moderate or positive, "Mais" has nothing to counter.

🔑 Rule: Before "Mais," insert something ASSERTIVE. "Mais" exists to TEMPER an extreme position.

Pitfall 4: Breaking the Argument Chain Across Multiple Gaps

What happens: You solve each gap independently and end up with a text that contradicts itself.

Example: If you chose Q38A ("dilemme") and Q39D ("réappropriation des rues"), the text would read: "It's the dilemma of all metropolises: ensuring inhabitants thrive. This involves reclaiming streets." — The first sentence frames it as a DILEMMA (unresolvable tension), but the second offers a SOLUTION. Dilemmas don't have solutions by definition.

🔑 Rule: After solving all five gaps, read the ENTIRE reconstructed text from beginning to end. Check that the tone, logic, and argument build consistently. If one gap feels off, reconsider it.
