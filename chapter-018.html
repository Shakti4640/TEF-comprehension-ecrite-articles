Welcome to Phase 4: Texte à Trous

Everything changes now. For 17 chapters, you've been reading COMPLETE texts and answering questions ABOUT them. Starting with this chapter, you read texts WITH HOLES and must FILL those holes.

This is fundamentally different:

PHASES 1-3 (Chapters 1-17):
  COMPLETE TEXT → Questions about meaning
  Skill: UNDERSTAND what the text says
  
PHASE 4 (Chapters 18-24):
  TEXT WITH GAPS → Fill in missing sentences
  Skill: COMPLETE what the text needs

The Texte à Trous Method — Your New Framework

Gap-fill questions test textual coherence — whether you understand how sentences connect to each other. Each gap has exactly ONE correct answer because only ONE sentence maintains logical, grammatical, and thematic continuity.

THE ANCHOR PRINCIPLE:

Every gap is surrounded by two anchors — the sentence BEFORE and the sentence AFTER:

SENTENCE BEFORE THE GAP ← ANCHOR 1
                          
     ...(GAP)...          ← What goes here?

SENTENCE AFTER THE GAP  ← ANCHOR 2

The correct answer must:

  1. Connect logically to Anchor 1 (what came before)
  2. Lead naturally to Anchor 2 (what comes after)
  3. Maintain thematic coherence with the overall paragraph
  4. Match grammatically (pronouns, tense, connectors)

THE FOUR TESTS for each gap:

TEST 1: LOGICAL FLOW
   Does this sentence make sense BETWEEN the two anchors?
   Does it follow logically from what came before?
   Does what comes after follow logically from it?

TEST 2: PRONOUN REFERENCE
   Do the pronouns in the candidate sentence correctly refer
   to nouns in the surrounding text?
   "Ces derniers" → who are "ces derniers"?
   "Ceux-ci" → who are "ceux-ci"?

TEST 3: CONNECTOR COHERENCE
   Does the logical connector (en effet, pourtant, quant à, 
   c'est pourquoi) match the relationship between sentences?

TEST 4: STATISTICAL/FACTUAL CONSISTENCY
   Do the numbers and facts remain consistent?
   If the text says "34 millions travel," the gap can't say
   "most French people stay home."

🚨 GOLDEN RULE #31: In gap-fill (texte à trous), every gap has TWO ANCHORS — the sentence before and the sentence after. The correct answer must connect smoothly to BOTH anchors. Test each candidate sentence by reading: [Anchor 1] + [Candidate] + [Anchor 2] as a continuous passage. Only ONE will flow naturally.

How to read a Texte à Trous

STEP 1: Read the ENTIRE text first, including the gaps, to get the overall theme and flow.

STEP 2: For each gap, identify the TWO ANCHORS clearly.

STEP 3: Before looking at the answer choices, PREDICT what the gap should say based on the anchors.

STEP 4: Then check which answer choice matches your prediction.

STEP 5: Verify by reading [Anchor 1] + [Choice] + [Anchor 2] as a continuous passage.

Let's apply this method to every gap.

🔬 PRONOUN REFERENCE — The Key to Gap 21

The critical skill here is pronoun reference tracking:

"Ces derniers" → refers to the LAST mentioned group
              → the 34 million who travel
              → "Ces derniers partent au moins une fois par an" ✓

"Ceux-ci"     → refers to the NEAREST mentioned group
              → also the 34 million who travel
              → "Ceux-ci aiment rester à la maison" ✗ (contradiction!)

Both "ces derniers" and "ceux-ci" refer to the 34 million travelers. But only D says something CONSISTENT with being travelers (they leave at least once a year). C says something CONTRADICTORY (they like staying home).

🚨 GOLDEN RULE #32: In gap-fill, pronouns like "ces derniers" (these latter ones), "ceux-ci" (these ones), "ceux-là" (those ones), "les uns... les autres" (some... others) must refer to the correct noun. Always trace the pronoun back to its ANTECEDENT — the noun it replaces. If the pronoun + verb creates a contradiction with the antecedent, the answer is wrong.

Analyzing the anchors — THE CONNECTOR IS KEY:

Anchor 2 begins with "En effet" (indeed / in fact). This is a CONFIRMATION connector — it confirms or supports what was just said.

"En effet" = "Indeed" / "In fact"
PURPOSE: To CONFIRM or PROVIDE EVIDENCE for the previous statement

So the gap (22) must make a STATEMENT that Anchor 2 CONFIRMS with evidence.

Anchor 2 says: "37% of over-70s travel." This is EVIDENCE that elderly people DO travel. What kind of statement would this confirm?

Prediction: The gap should say something like "it's not just young people who stay home" or "the non-travelers aren't just old people" — something that Anchor 2 then CONFIRMS by showing that even 37% of over-70s travel.

🔬 THE "EN EFFET" CONNECTOR — Confirmation Logic

The connector "en effet" is the KEY to this gap. It tells you that Anchor 2 CONFIRMS whatever the gap says:

GAP 22: [Statement X]
ANCHOR 2: "EN EFFET, 37% des plus de 70 ans voyagent"
           = INDEED, 37% of 70+ travel

LOGIC: Anchor 2 must CONFIRM Statement X
       
If X = "not only elderly stay home" (B)
Then "Indeed, 37% of elderly travel" CONFIRMS this
Because: if many elderly travel, then staying home ≠ only elderly ✓

If X = "only old people stay home" (A)
Then "Indeed, 37% of elderly travel" CONTRADICTS this
Because: if 37% of elderly travel, staying home ≠ only old people ✗

🚨 GOLDEN RULE #33: In gap-fill, logical connectors in the ANCHOR sentences are your most powerful clues. Each connector dictates a specific RELATIONSHIP between the gap and the anchor:

pourtant / cependantcontrastThe gap states something that the anchor CONTRADICTS
c'est pourquoi / par conséquentconsequenceThe gap states a cause; the anchor states the effect

de plus / en outreadditionThe gap adds to what came before; the anchor adds more

The colon at the end of Anchor 1 is a powerful signal:

🔬 THE "PARAGRAPH COHERENCE" PRINCIPLE

This gap tests whether the answer stays within the SAME TOPIC as its paragraph:

PARAGRAPH TOPIC: What sedentary people enjoy doing at home/locally
  → "profiter de leur jardin" (enjoy their garden)
  → "recevoir leurs amis" (host friends)
  → GAP 24 must be: another enjoyable local activity

WRONG answers introduce the WRONG TOPIC:
  A: "voyager" → travel (opposite topic)
  C: "circuits touristiques" → tourism (wrong group)
  D: "craignent de s'ennuyer" → negative feeling (wrong tone)

🚨 NEW CONCEPT: Paragraph Coherence. Each gap must maintain the TOPIC and TONE of its surrounding paragraph. If the paragraph discusses home activities with a positive tone, the gap must also describe a home activity positively. Answers that introduce a different topic or opposite tone break paragraph coherence.

🔬 THE "QUANTIFICATION BRIDGE" TECHNIQUE

Gap 25 works as a BRIDGE between a qualitative statement and a quantitative one:

ANCHOR 1: "de plus en plus courts" (qualitative: increasingly SHORT)
GAP 25:   "au maximum vingt jours" (quantitative: max 20 DAYS)
ANCHOR 2: "juillet et août = 4/5" (quantitative: specific months)

The gap QUANTIFIES the qualitative statement:
  "short" → "maximum 20 days"
  
Then Anchor 2 adds MORE specific data:
  "20 days" → "mostly in July and August"

This creates a narrowing funnel: general trend → specific number → specific months.

         □ Connector coherent?
         □ Facts/statistics consistent?

              "Ceux-ci" = the 34 million travelers
              "aiment rester à la maison" = like staying home
              → TRAVELERS like STAYING HOME? → CONTRADICTION

How to avoid it: Always trace the pronoun to its antecedent. "Ceux-ci" after "34 millions [qui se déplacent]" = the travelers. Check if the verb makes sense with the antecedent: travelers + staying home = contradiction.

PITFALL #66: The "Connector Mismatch" Trap 🔄

GAP 22: [Statement]
ANCHOR 2: "EN EFFET, 37% des plus de 70 ans voyagent"
          "En effet" = CONFIRMS what came before

TRAP A: "Parmi eux, il n'y a QUE de vieilles personnes" (only old people)
        → "En effet, 37% of elderly travel" → CONTRADICTS "only old people stay home"
        → "En effet" should CONFIRM, but it CONTRADICTS
        → CONNECTOR MISMATCH

How to avoid it: When Anchor 2 uses "en effet" (confirmation), the gap must state something that the anchor's evidence SUPPORTS. When Anchor 2 uses "pourtant" (contrast), the gap must state something the anchor OPPOSES.

PITFALL #67: The "Opposite Trend" Trap 📉📈

DATA: 1988 = 20 million → today = 34 million = INCREASE

TRAP A (Gap 23): "Une DIMINUTION importante" = significant DECREASE
TRAP D (Gap 23): "Un RETOUR aux vacances sédentaires" = return to staying home

Both describe OPPOSITE trends to what the data shows.

How to avoid it: Always check: does the trend in the answer match the trend in the data? If numbers go UP, the phenomenon must be an INCREASE. If numbers go DOWN, it's a DECREASE. Don't accept opposite trends.

PITFALL #68: The "Topic Intrusion" Trap 📌

PARAGRAPH TOPIC: What sedentary people enjoy at home
  → Garden, friends...

TRAP A (Gap 24): "Ils en profitent pour VOYAGER" = they travel
                 → TRAVEL intrudes into a HOME paragraph

TRAP C (Gap 24): "circuits TOURISTIQUES" = tourist circuits
                 → TOURISM intrudes into a HOME paragraph

How to avoid it: Before accepting an answer, check: "Does this answer belong to the same TOPIC as the surrounding paragraph?" If the paragraph discusses home activities, the gap must also discuss home activities.

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY

What Chapter 18 taught you — the FOUNDATION of gap-fill:

NEW RULE #31: Every gap has TWO ANCHORS (before and after).
              The correct answer must connect smoothly to BOTH.
              Read [Anchor 1] + [Choice] + [Anchor 2] as continuous text.

NEW RULE #32: Pronouns ("ces derniers," "ceux-ci") must refer to
              the correct antecedent. Trace the pronoun back to its
              noun. If pronoun + verb contradicts the antecedent,
              the answer is wrong.

NEW RULE #33: Logical connectors in Anchor 2 dictate what the gap
              must contain:
              "En effet" → gap is CONFIRMED by anchor
              "Pourtant" → gap is CONTRASTED by anchor
              "C'est pourquoi" → gap is the CAUSE, anchor is effect
              "Quant à" → gap ends previous topic

NEW CONCEPT: The Texte à Trous Method
             1. Read entire text first
             2. Identify both anchors for each gap
             3. Analyze connectors
             4. Predict content
             5. Test each choice
             6. Verify with four tests (logic, pronouns, connectors, facts)

NEW CONCEPT: Paragraph Coherence
             Each gap must maintain the TOPIC and TONE of its
             surrounding paragraph. Don't introduce wrong topics.

NEW CONCEPT: Quantification Bridge
             Gaps can bridge qualitative statements ("short") to
             quantitative data ("maximum 20 days")

NEW TRAP TYPES:

  #67: Opposite Trend (increase vs. decrease mismatch)
  #68: Topic Intrusion (wrong subject for the paragraph)
