CHAPTER 2: BIRTH OF THE FÊTE DE LA MUSIQUE

📰 Texte 85 — Cultural Event | Phase 1: Foundation Thinking

📄 THE COMPLETE ARTICLE

La fête de la musique

En 1982, Jack Lang, alors ministre de la Culture, Christian Dupavillon, membre de son cabinet et Maurice Fleuret, directeur de la musique, décident d'organiser la première Fête de la musique. L'idée leur est venue suite à une enquête révélant que 37 % des Français disposaient d'un instrument de musique et qu'un jeune sur deux, de moins de 19 ans, en jouait régulièrement. Pour faire découvrir le panorama musical de la France, les trois hommes décident de tout mettre en œuvre pour organiser cette manifestation. Le rendez-vous est fixé au 21 juin, jour symbolique du solstice d'été, jour le plus long de l'année et ancienne fête de la Saint-Jean. La préparation se déroule dans une grande précipitation, une vingtaine de jours à peine après son annonce officielle, sans véritables moyens financiers ni logistiques. En dépit de la diligence et du caractère spontané de l'événement, de nombreuses initiatives se mettent en place dans toute la France. Des ensembles professionnels comme l'Opéra de Paris ou l'Orchestre philharmonique de Strasbourg finissent par accepter de jouer gratuitement.

  • B — faire connaître les talents musicaux des Français.
  • C — mettre en valeur la chanson française dans le monde.

Question 19 : La première Fête de la musique

  • A — a été plus ou moins improvisée.
  • B — n'a pas été annoncée officiellement.
  • C — a été organisée très longtemps à l'avance.
  • D — a nécessité un apport financier important.

🧠 HOW TO APPROACH IT

What's NEW in this chapter

In Chapter 1, answers were almost word-for-word copies of the text. Here, something changes dramatically:

The correct answer uses DIFFERENT words to say the SAME thing as the text.

This is called paraphrase — and it's the #1 skill that separates students who score 70% from those who score 95%.

Applying the method from Chapter 1

STEP 1: Read all questions first (Rule #1 from Chapter 1)

Q17: "L'idée est née..." → I need to find: THE ORIGIN / WHAT TRIGGERED THE IDEA
Q18: "Elle a été créée pour..." → I need to find: THE PURPOSE / THE GOAL

Three search targets loaded. Notice how they follow a logical chain:

Q17: WHERE did the idea come from?     → ORIGIN
Q18: WHY was it created?               → PURPOSE
Q19: HOW was the first edition organized? → CONDITIONS

This is typical of cultural/historical texts — questions follow the chronological logic of the story: origin → purpose → execution.

Simple, descriptive. The article will explain the event. No surprise here.

✅ HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT

QUESTION 17: L'idée de la fête de la musique est née...

The key phrase in the text:

"L'idée leur est venue suite à une enquête révélant que 37 % des Français disposaient d'un instrument de musique"

Let's decode the critical vocabulary:

FrenchMeaning
suite àfollowing / as a result of
une enquêtea survey / an investigation
révélant querevealing that
disposaient d'un instrumenthad (owned) an instrument

The logic: The idea was born AFTER a survey showed that many French people played music.

Now let's test each answer:

ChoiceAnalysisVerdict
A — après avoir effectué un sondage"un sondage" = a survey/poll. "une enquête" = a survey/investigation. These are SYNONYMS. "Après avoir effectué" = after having carried out. "Suite à une enquête" = following a survey. SAME MEANING, DIFFERENT WORDS.✅ CORRECT
B — à la demande de 37% des FrançaisThe 37% is a STATISTIC from the survey. These people didn't ASK for anything. The survey REVEALED a fact about them. "Demande" (request) ≠ "révélant" (revealing).❌ Distorts the role of the 37%
C — d'une rencontre entre le ministre et les jeunesThe text mentions no meeting with young people. The three men decided among themselves.❌ Invented information
D — sur l'initiative de musiciens de moins de 19 ansThe text says "un jeune sur deux, de moins de 19 ans, en jouait." This is a STATISTIC, not an initiative. The young people didn't propose anything.❌ Confuses a statistic with an action

✅ Answer: A

🔬 THE PARAPHRASE PRINCIPLE — Your new superpower

Look at what just happened:

TEXT says:        "suite à une enquête"
CORRECT answer:   "après avoir effectué un sondage"

Not a single word is the same. Yet they mean the EXACT same thing.

suite à         →  après avoir effectué
une enquête     →  un sondage

Meanwhile, look at the WRONG answers:

Answer B contains: "37%" — a number COPIED from the text
Answer D contains: "de moins de 19 ans" — a phrase COPIED from the text

This confirms and deepens Rule #2 from Chapter 1:

🚨 GOLDEN RULE #2 (EXPANDED): Words copied from the text often appear in WRONG answers. The CORRECT answer often paraphrases the text using completely different vocabulary.

This is the Paraphrase Principle — and it will save you in every single exam from now on.

QUESTION 18: Elle a été créée pour...

The key phrase in the text:

"Pour faire découvrir le panorama musical de la France"

Let's decode this:

FrenchMeaning
pourin order to (signals PURPOSE — directly answers "créée pour")
faire découvrirto make people discover / to showcase
le panorama musicalthe musical landscape / diversity
de la Franceof France

The purpose: To showcase the musical diversity/talent of France to the French people.

Now let's test each answer:

ChoiceAnalysisVerdict
A — financer les musiciens professionnels débutantsThe text says professionals played "gratuitement" (for free). There's no mention of financing anyone.❌ Invented
B — faire connaître les talents musicaux des Français"Faire connaître" = to make known ≈ "faire découvrir" = to make discover. "Les talents musicaux des Français" ≈ "le panorama musical de la France." PARAPHRASE!✅ CORRECT
C — mettre en valeur la chanson française dans le monde"Dans le monde" (in the world) — the text says "de la France" (of France). The event was FOR France, not for the world. Also "la chanson française" is too narrow — the text says "panorama musical" which includes all music, not just songs.❌ Two errors: scope (world) + type (songs only)
D — inciter les jeunes à jouer d'un instrument de musiqueThe survey FOUND that young people already played. The festival wasn't created to MAKE them play — it was created to SHOWCASE existing talent.❌ Confuses the finding with the goal

✅ Answer: B

🔬 PARAPHRASE MAP FOR Q18

TEXT:              "faire découvrir le panorama musical de la France"
CORRECT ANSWER:    "faire connaître les talents musicaux des Français"

faire découvrir    →  faire connaître
le panorama musical →  les talents musicaux
de la France       →  des Français

Every single element was replaced with a synonym. Zero words in common. This is textbook paraphrase — and this is what the correct answer looks like in most TCF/TEF/DELF/DALF exams.

QUESTION 19: La première Fête de la musique...

The key phrases in the text (multiple!):

"La préparation se déroule dans une grande précipitation"
"une vingtaine de jours à peine après son annonce officielle"
"sans véritables moyens financiers ni logistiques"
"le caractère spontané de l'événement"

Let's build the complete picture:

Evidence from textWhat it means
"grande précipitation"great rush / hurry
"une vingtaine de jours à peine"barely twenty days (very short preparation)
"sans véritables moyens financiers"without real financial resources
"ni logistiques"nor logistical support
"caractère spontané"spontaneous nature

The conclusion: The first festival was thrown together quickly, without proper resources, in a rushed and spontaneous manner. In other words: it was improvised.

Now let's test each answer:

ChoiceAnalysisVerdict
A — a été plus ou moins improvisée"Plus ou moins improvisée" = more or less improvised. The text says: rushed, barely 20 days, no money, no logistics, spontaneous. That IS improvisation. PARAPHRASE of the entire description.✅ CORRECT
B — n'a pas été annoncée officiellementThe text EXPLICITLY says "son annonce officielle." It WAS officially announced. This answer directly CONTRADICTS the text.❌ Direct contradiction
C — a été organisée très longtemps à l'avance"Très longtemps à l'avance" = far in advance. The text says "une vingtaine de jours à peine" = barely 20 days. This is the OPPOSITE.❌ Opposite of text
D — a nécessité un apport financier importantThe text says "sans véritables moyens financiers." No real financial means = the OPPOSITE of "important financial contribution."❌ Opposite of text

✅ Answer: A

🔬 THE SYNTHESIS PARAPHRASE — A new level

Notice something special about Q19. Unlike Q17 and Q18, where one phrase matched one answer, here multiple phrases across the text combine into one single answer word: "improvisée."

"grande précipitation"          ─┐
"une vingtaine de jours à peine" ├──→  "plus ou moins improvisée"
"sans moyens financiers"         │
"caractère spontané"            ─┘

This is a synthesis paraphrase — the answer summarizes several scattered pieces of evidence into one concept. This is harder than simple paraphrase because you need to:

  1. Collect multiple clues across the text
  2. Understand what they ADD UP TO
  3. Match that sum to one answer choice

🚨 GOLDEN RULE #5: Sometimes the correct answer is a SUMMARY of multiple text elements, not a paraphrase of one single sentence. Look for the answer that captures the OVERALL picture.

🎯 WHAT COULD BE DONE

Three types of paraphrase you now know:

TYPE 1: Simple Synonym Swap (Q17)
   TEXT: "suite à une enquête"
   ANSWER: "après avoir effectué un sondage"
   → One phrase, replaced word-by-word with synonyms

TYPE 2: Full Phrase Replacement (Q18)
   TEXT: "faire découvrir le panorama musical de la France"
   ANSWER: "faire connaître les talents musicaux des Français"
   → Every element rephrased, but same structure

TYPE 3: Synthesis Paraphrase (Q19)
   TEXT: [multiple scattered clues]
   ANSWER: "plus ou moins improvisée"
   → Multiple text elements compressed into one concept

In real exams, Type 3 is the hardest and most common for difficult questions. You'll see it increasingly in later chapters.

Read the "purpose" sentence carefully

In any article about an event, organization, or initiative, there will almost always be a sentence starting with "pour" + infinitive that states the PURPOSE. This sentence is gold — it answers "why was it created?" questions directly.

"Pour faire découvrir le panorama musical de la France"

 This word signals: HERE IS THE PURPOSE

Check for contradictions systematically

In Q19, three out of four answers CONTRADICTED the text:

AnswerContradiction

❌ WHAT YOU SHOULD NOT DO

Bad habitExample from this chapterConsequence
Choosing answers that copy text wordsPicking B ("37%") or D ("moins de 19 ans") for Q17 because those numbers appear in the textYou fall into the copy-paste trap every time

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL #5: The "Number Trap" 🔢

Text mentions: "37%" and "de moins de 19 ans"
Wrong answers contain: "37% des Français" and "de moins de 19 ans"
Student thinks: "These numbers are in the text, so the answer must be right!"

Reality: Numbers and statistics from the text are favorite bait for wrong answers. The exam takes a real number and wraps it in a FALSE statement.

How to avoid it: When you see a number from the text in an answer, read the FULL sentence around it. Ask: "Is the number being used the SAME WAY in the text and in the answer?"

TEXT: "37% des Français disposaient d'un instrument" (had an instrument — FACT)
ANSWER B: "à la demande de 37% des Français" (at the request of — ACTION)
→ Same number, COMPLETELY different meaning

PITFALL #6: The "Cause-Effect Reversal" 🔄

CAUSE (what existed before):   Young people played music
EFFECT (what was created after): The festival was organized

Student reverses it:
"The festival was created TO MAKE young people play"

Reality: The playing caused the festival, not the other way around. The survey DISCOVERED that people played → this INSPIRED the creation of the festival.

How to avoid it: Ask yourself: "Which came first? The chicken or the egg?" In this text:

  1. First: People played music (CAUSE)
  2. Then: Survey discovered this (TRIGGER)
  3. Then: Festival was created (EFFECT)

PITFALL #7: The "Scope Expansion" Trap 🌍

TEXT: "le panorama musical de la France" (of France — national scope)
ANSWER C: "la chanson française dans le monde" (in the world — global scope)

The text talks about France discovering its OWN music. The wrong answer expands this to THE WHOLE WORLD. This is a common trick:

What text saysWhat wrong answer claims
National scopeInternational scope
All music typesOnly songs
For the publicFor professionals
To discoverTo finance

How to avoid it: Match the SCOPE. If the text says "France," the answer should say "France" or its equivalent — not "the world."
