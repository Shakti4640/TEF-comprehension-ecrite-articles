Chapter 6: BIEN RECYCLER POUR LIMITER LES DÉCHETS — THE GAP-FILL TEXT (TEXTE À TROUS)

♻️ Texte 52 | Phase 2: Easy | Core Patterns

📄 COMPLETE ARTICLE

On appelle ça la « responsabilité élargie des producteurs ». Le principe est simple : un industriel ou un distributeur qui vend un produit à son client doit le reprendre quand il est usé ou cassé afin de le recycler. ...(16)... C'est ainsi qu'ont été récoltés l'an dernier en Belgique plus de 800 millions d'euros.

La méthode fait florès avec aujourd'hui une dizaine de filières en activité. ...(17)... Les lampes et les textiles utilisent, eux, l'apport volontaire en magasin.

Des efforts restent à faire pour réduire drastiquement le volume de déchets. Chaque Belge produit 590 kilos d'ordures ménagères par an, soit deux fois plus qu'il y a quarante ans. ...(18)... Il s'agit de boire l'eau du robinet, de réparer ses appareils électriques, d'opter pour les piles rechargeables... Et enfin, donner les objets dont on n'a plus besoin pour qu'ils soient réutilisés.

THE THREE QUESTIONS (GAP-FILL FORMAT)

Question 16 :

  • A — Le principe sera mis en place d'ici la fin de l'année.
  • B — Le recyclage ne rapporte pas d'argent pour le moment.
  • C — Ainsi, elle ne finit pas dans une poubelle ou dans une déchèterie.
  • D — L'opération est financée par une taxe incluse dans le prix d'achat. ✅

Question 17 :

  • A — Les tissus et les éclairages ne sont pas encore recyclés.
  • B — Tous les objets sans exception sont collectés à domicile.
  • C — Les appareils sont repris lors de la livraison d'un appareil neuf. ✅
  • D — Les consommateurs peuvent déposer leurs appareils usés chez le vendeur.

Question 18 :

  • A — Aussi, les gestes les plus vertueux consistent à ne pas en produire. ✅
  • B — Seules des décisions prises en haut lieu pourraient inverser la tendance.
  • C — En cause, les habitudes néfastes de consommation des pays industrialisés.
  • D — Et malheureusement aucun indicateur ne donne l'espoir d'un changement.

🔍 HOW TO APPROACH IT

A completely new question format

In Projects 1-5, the format was always:

TEXT (complete) → QUESTION about the text → CHOOSE the right answer

Gap-fill reverses the logic:

TEXT (with holes) → CHOOSE the sentence that FILLS the hole correctly

This is fundamentally different. You're no longer asking "What does the text say?" You're asking "What does the text NEED?"

Why gap-fill is harder

In standard comprehension, you have ALL the information and must interpret it. In gap-fill, you have INCOMPLETE information and must RECONSTRUCT it. This requires three skills that standard questions don't test:

SkillWhat It MeansWhy It Matters
Backward coherenceThe inserted sentence must flow FROM the previous sentenceWhat comes BEFORE the gap constrains the answer
Forward coherenceThe inserted sentence must flow INTO the next sentenceWhat comes AFTER the gap constrains the answer
Thematic coherenceThe inserted sentence must fit the paragraph's TOPICA sentence about money can't go in a paragraph about methods

Step 1: Identify the text type

Quick scan reveals:

  • An economic/environmental topic (recycling, waste)
  • Structured in three clear paragraphs
  • Each paragraph has ONE gap
  • Factual, informational tone

Verdict: INFORMATIONAL TEXT WITH GAPS (texte informatif à trous)

Step 2: DON'T read the options first

This is the ONE exception to the "read questions first" rule from Project 1. Here's why:

In standard questions, reading the question first helps you SCAN for the answer. But in gap-fill, reading the options first contaminates your thinking — you'll try to make each option fit instead of determining what SHOULD fit.

The correct approach for gap-fill:

Step 1: Read the text WITHOUT looking at options
Step 2: At each gap, PREDICT what should go there
Step 3: THEN look at options and find your prediction

This is the "Predict Before You Peek" technique:

🏆 GOLDEN RULE #18: In gap-fill texts, read the surrounding sentences FIRST and predict what the gap needs BEFORE looking at the options. Your prediction acts as a filter — the correct option will match your prediction; the traps won't.

Step 3: The "Before-After Sandwich" technique (NEW)

For EVERY gap, examine exactly TWO things:

THE SANDWICH:
┌─────────────────────────┐
│  SENTENCE BEFORE (bread) │ ← What idea does this end with?
├─────────────────────────┤
│  GAP (filling)           │ ← What must bridge BEFORE and AFTER?
├─────────────────────────┤
│  SENTENCE AFTER (bread)  │ ← What idea does this start with?
└─────────────────────────┘

The gap must:

  1. Continue the idea from BEFORE
  2. Prepare the idea that comes AFTER
  3. Bridge both ideas logically

If an option connects to BEFORE but not AFTER → wrong.
If an option connects to AFTER but not BEFORE → wrong.
If an option connects to NEITHER → definitely wrong.
If an option connects to BOTH → correct.

🧩 HOW CAN WE SOLVE IT — Question by Question

QUESTION 16: First Gap

Step 1: Build the Sandwich

BEFORE the gap:

"un industriel ou un distributeur qui vend un produit à son client doit le reprendre quand il est usé ou cassé afin de le recycler."

The BEFORE sentence explains: producers MUST take back used products to recycle them.

Key information to carry forward:

  • The MECHANISM: take back → recycle
  • The OBLIGATION: "doit le reprendre" (must take it back)
  • The TOPIC: how the system works

AFTER the gap:

"C'est ainsi qu'ont été récoltés l'an dernier en Belgique plus de 800 millions d'euros."

The AFTER sentence says: "This is how" 800 million euros were collected.

Key information to connect to:

  • "C'est ainsi" = "This is how" → refers BACK to the gap. Whatever the gap says IS the method that generated money.
  • "800 millions d'euros" → MONEY was generated
  • "récoltés" = collected → money was collected through the mechanism described

Step 2: Predict what the gap needs

BEFORE: Products are taken back for recycling
GAP: [must explain HOW this generates money]
AFTER: "This is how" 800 million euros were collected

My prediction: The gap should explain the FINANCIAL MECHANISM — how taking back products turns into money. Probably a tax, fee, or funding system.

Step 3: Test each option against the Sandwich

Option A: "Le principe sera mis en place d'ici la fin de l'année."
(The principle will be implemented by year's end.)

Test BACKWARD (connects to BEFORE?):
BEFORE says the principle EXISTS ("le principe est simple"). Option A says it WILL BE implemented (future). If it will be implemented in the future, how can money have ALREADY been collected?

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER says money WAS collected ("ont été récoltés" — passé composé = past). If the system isn't in place yet (Option A), money couldn't have been collected.

TEMPORAL CONTRADICTION: Future implementation vs. past collection. The verb tenses clash.

This is Pitfall #8 from Project 3 (Temporal Misassignment) — but now applied to gap-fill. The tenses in the option must be COMPATIBLE with the tenses in the surrounding text.

❌ ELIMINATED — Temporal contradiction (future system vs. past revenue)

Option B: "Le recyclage ne rapporte pas d'argent pour le moment."
(Recycling doesn't generate money for now.)

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER says: "C'est ainsi qu'ont été récoltés...800 millions d'euros."

"C'est ainsi" = "This is how." If the gap says "recycling doesn't generate money," then the AFTER sentence would read: "Recycling doesn't generate money. This is how 800 million euros were collected."

This is a DIRECT LOGICAL CONTRADICTION. You cannot say "no money" and then immediately say "this is how money was collected."

❌ ELIMINATED — Direct logical contradiction with AFTER sentence

Option C: "Ainsi, elle ne finit pas dans une poubelle ou dans une déchèterie."
(Thus, it doesn't end up in a bin or a waste facility.)

Test BACKWARD (connects to BEFORE?):
BEFORE talks about taking back products to recycle them. Option C says "it doesn't end up in a bin." This connects reasonably — if the product is taken back, it doesn't end up as waste. ✓

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER says: "C'est ainsi qu'ont été récoltés...800 millions d'euros."

"Not ending up in a bin" → "This is how 800 million euros were collected"???

There is NO logical bridge between avoiding waste and collecting money. The AFTER sentence needs a FINANCIAL explanation, and Option C provides an ENVIRONMENTAL one.

Additionally: Option C starts with "Ainsi" (thus/therefore). The AFTER sentence starts with "C'est ainsi" (this is how). Having two consecutive sentences both starting with "ainsi" creates stylistic awkwardness — a subtle but real signal of incorrectness.

❌ ELIMINATED — No logical bridge to AFTER (environmental → financial gap) + stylistic repetition of "ainsi"

This introduces a new gap-fill specific tool:

🏆 GOLDEN RULE #19: In gap-fill texts, check for CONNECTOR COLLISIONS. If the gap option starts with a connector (ainsi, donc, cependant) and the AFTER sentence also starts with a similar connector, the option likely doesn't fit. Good writing avoids repeating the same logical connector in consecutive sentences.

Option D: "L'opération est financée par une taxe incluse dans le prix d'achat."
(The operation is financed by a tax included in the purchase price.)

Test BACKWARD (connects to BEFORE?):
BEFORE explains the take-back system. Option D explains HOW it's financed — a tax in the purchase price. This is the MECHANISM behind the system described. ✓

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER says: "C'est ainsi qu'ont été récoltés...800 millions d'euros."

A tax in the purchase price → "This is how" 800 million euros were collected.

PERFECT BRIDGE: Tax on every product sold → millions of products × tax = 800 million euros. The "C'est ainsi" directly refers to the tax mechanism described in Option D.

COMPLETE FLOW:
Products are taken back for recycling
         ↓
This is financed by a tax in the purchase price  [GAP FILLED]
         ↓
"This is how" 800 million euros were collected

Every element connects seamlessly.

✅ CORRECT — Bridges BEFORE (mechanism) and AFTER (revenue) perfectly

🎯 Answer: D — L'opération est financée par une taxe incluse dans le prix d'achat.

THE MASTER LESSON FROM GAP 16:

The key to solving this gap was the phrase "C'est ainsi" in the AFTER sentence. This phrase is a backward-pointing connector — it says "the thing I just described is HOW..."

This means: whatever fills the gap IS the thing that "C'est ainsi" refers to. The gap MUST contain the mechanism that generated 800 million euros.

🏆 GOLDEN RULE #20: In gap-fill texts, CONNECTORS in the AFTER sentence are your most powerful clues. They tell you exactly what ROLE the gap must play:

Connector in AFTERWhat the Gap Must Provide
"C'est ainsi que..." (This is how...)A MECHANISM or METHOD
"C'est pourquoi..." (That's why...)A CAUSE or REASON
"En revanche..." (On the other hand...)A CONTRASTING point
"Par exemple..." (For example...)A GENERAL STATEMENT (examples follow)
"Pourtant..." (However...)Something that will be CONTRADICTED
"En effet..." (Indeed...)Something that will be CONFIRMED

QUESTION 17: Second Gap

Step 1: Build the Sandwich

BEFORE the gap:

"La méthode fait florès avec aujourd'hui une dizaine de filières en activité."

"La méthode fait florès" = the method is flourishing/thriving
"Une dizaine de filières en activité" = about ten sectors/channels in operation

Key information: Multiple sectors use this recycling method. The gap should describe HOW one or more sectors operate.

AFTER the gap:

"Les lampes et les textiles utilisent, eux, l'apport volontaire en magasin."

"Les lampes et les textiles" = lamps and textiles
"Utilisent, eux" = use, for their part / they on the other hand
"L'apport volontaire en magasin" = voluntary drop-off in stores

Key information:

  • Lamps and textiles use IN-STORE VOLUNTARY DROP-OFF
  • The critical word is "eux" (set off by commas) = "for their part" / "they, on the other hand"

"Eux" is a CONTRAST MARKER. It signals that lamps/textiles use a DIFFERENT method from what was just described. Therefore, the gap must describe a DIFFERENT collection method.

BEFORE: Ten sectors are active
GAP: [must describe METHOD A — used by some items]
AFTER: Lamps and textiles, FOR THEIR PART, use METHOD B (voluntary store drop-off)
                                    ↑
                            CONTRAST with gap

Step 2: Predict what the gap needs

My prediction: The gap should describe a collection method that is DIFFERENT from voluntary in-store drop-off. Probably home collection, delivery-based pickup, or some other retrieval system.

Step 3: Test each option

Option A: "Les tissus et les éclairages ne sont pas encore recyclés."
(Textiles and lighting are not yet recycled.)

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER says lamps ("lampes") and textiles ("textiles") use voluntary store drop-off.

Option A says textiles ("tissus") and lighting ("éclairages") are NOT YET recycled.

But "tissus" = textiles and "éclairages" = lighting/lamps. Option A says they're NOT recycled. The AFTER sentence says they ARE recycled (via store drop-off).

DIRECT CONTRADICTION. Option A denies recycling for the very items that the AFTER sentence describes as being recycled.

This trap uses synonyms to disguise the contradiction:

Option A wordAFTER sentence wordSame thing?
"tissus""textiles"✅ YES — both mean fabrics
"éclairages""lampes"✅ YES — both mean lighting

The exam hopes you won't realize "tissus/éclairages" and "textiles/lampes" refer to the SAME items.

❌ ELIMINATED — Contradicts AFTER sentence (says items aren't recycled when they are)

This introduces a gap-fill specific trap:

Trap TypeHow It Works
🪤 Synonym Disguised ContradictionUses synonyms to hide the fact that the option contradicts the surrounding text

Option B: "Tous les objets sans exception sont collectés à domicile."
(All objects without exception are collected at home.)

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER says lamps and textiles use "l'apport volontaire en magasin" (voluntary drop-off in stores).

Option B says ALL objects are collected "à domicile" (at home).

If ALL objects are collected at home, then lamps and textiles would ALSO be collected at home. But the AFTER sentence says they use STORE drop-off. "All objects at home" contradicts "lamps and textiles in stores."

The word "sans exception" (without exception) is the killer. It leaves NO room for the AFTER sentence to describe a different method.

❌ ELIMINATED — "Sans exception" contradicts the different method described in AFTER

This teaches an important gap-fill principle:

ABSOLUTE STATEMENTS ("tous," "sans exception," "jamais," "toujours") in gap-fill options are almost always wrong because they leave no room for the nuance or contrast that the surrounding text provides.

Option C: "Les appareils sont repris lors de la livraison d'un appareil neuf."
(Appliances are taken back during delivery of a new appliance.)

Test BACKWARD (connects to BEFORE?):
BEFORE says ten sectors are active. Option C describes ONE specific sector (appliances) and its method. This gives a concrete example of one of those sectors. ✓

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER says: "Les lampes et les textiles utilisent, eux, l'apport volontaire en magasin."

Option C describes: appliances taken back during HOME DELIVERY of a new one.

Now the "eux" contrast works perfectly:

METHOD A (Gap = Option C):
APPLIANCES → taken back during HOME DELIVERY
         ↕ CONTRAST ("eux")
METHOD B (AFTER sentence):
LAMPS & TEXTILES → voluntary drop-off IN STORES

HOME DELIVERY pickup vs. IN-STORE drop-off = clear contrast in:

  • Location: HOME vs. STORE
  • Initiative: AUTOMATIC (during delivery) vs. VOLUNTARY (consumer brings)
  • Item type: LARGE appliances vs. SMALL items (lamps, textiles)

The "eux" now makes perfect sense — "lamps and textiles, for their part (unlike appliances), use a different method."

✅ CORRECT — Creates the contrast that "eux" demands

Option D: "Les consommateurs peuvent déposer leurs appareils usés chez le vendeur."
(Consumers can drop off their used appliances at the seller's.)

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER says lamps and textiles use "l'apport volontaire en magasin" (voluntary in-store drop-off).

Option D says consumers drop off appliances "chez le vendeur" (at the seller's = in-store).

Both describe essentially the SAME method: consumer brings item to store. If we insert Option D, the text would read:

"Consumers can drop off their appliances at the seller's. Lamps and textiles, for their part, use voluntary in-store drop-off."

The "eux" (for their part) demands CONTRAST. But Option D and the AFTER sentence describe the SAME method (in-store drop-off). There's nothing to contrast.

Option D methodAFTER methodContrast?
Drop off at seller's (in-store)Voluntary drop-off in store❌ SAME METHOD

❌ ELIMINATED — No contrast created, making "eux" meaningless

🎯 Answer: C — Les appareils sont repris lors de la livraison d'un appareil neuf.

THE MASTER LESSON FROM GAP 17:

The entire solution hinged on ONE WORD: "eux." This tiny pronoun, set off by commas, told us everything:

", eux," = CONTRAST MARKER
         ↓
The gap MUST describe something DIFFERENT from the AFTER sentence
         ↓
AFTER describes in-store voluntary drop-off
         ↓
The gap MUST describe a NON-store, NON-voluntary method
         ↓
Only Option C (home delivery pickup) qualifies

🏆 GOLDEN RULE #21: In gap-fill texts, PRONOUNS and EMPHASIS MARKERS in the surrounding text are critical clues. Words like "eux," "quant à," "en revanche," "de leur côté" signal that the gap must create a CONTRAST with what follows.

Here is a reference table of contrast markers you'll encounter:

French MarkerEnglish EquivalentSignal
", eux,""for their part"The gap describes a DIFFERENT approach
"en revanche""on the other hand"The gap states the OPPOSITE
"quant à""as for"The gap covers a different SUBJECT
"de leur côté""on their side"The gap describes a different ACTOR'S approach
"contrairement à""unlike"The gap provides what this contrasts WITH

QUESTION 18: Third Gap

Step 1: Build the Sandwich

BEFORE the gap:

"Chaque Belge produit 590 kilos d'ordures ménagères par an, soit deux fois plus qu'il y a quarante ans."

Key information: Waste production has DOUBLED in 40 years. This is a PROBLEM statement — things are getting WORSE.

AFTER the gap:

"Il s'agit de boire l'eau du robinet, de réparer ses appareils électriques, d'opter pour les piles rechargeables... Et enfin, donner les objets dont on n'a plus besoin pour qu'ils soient réutilisés."

Key information: A list of PRACTICAL ACTIONS individuals can take. "Il s'agit de..." = "It's about..." — introduces specific examples of something.

But examples of WHAT? The gap must state the GENERAL PRINCIPLE that these actions illustrate.

BEFORE: Waste has doubled (PROBLEM)
GAP: [must state a GENERAL PRINCIPLE / SOLUTION]
AFTER: Specific actions (drink tap water, repair appliances, rechargeable batteries...) = EXAMPLES of the principle

Step 2: Predict what the gap needs

My prediction: The gap should state that the best solution is to REDUCE waste at the source — to not produce waste in the first place. The AFTER sentence then gives specific examples of how to do this.

Step 3: Test each option

Option A: "Aussi, les gestes les plus vertueux consistent à ne pas en produire."
(Also, the most virtuous actions consist of not producing [waste] at all.)

Test BACKWARD (connects to BEFORE?):
BEFORE states the problem (waste has doubled). Option A says the solution is to not produce waste. "Aussi" (also/therefore) connects to the previous problem statement. ✓

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER lists specific actions: drink tap water (= no plastic bottles), repair appliances (= no new purchases), rechargeable batteries (= no disposable ones), give away objects (= no throwing away).

ALL of these actions are examples of NOT PRODUCING WASTE. Option A states the general principle; the AFTER sentence gives the examples.

GENERAL PRINCIPLE (Gap = Option A):
"The most virtuous actions = not producing waste"
         ↓
SPECIFIC EXAMPLES (AFTER):
├── Drink tap water (avoid bottle waste)
├── Repair appliances (avoid replacement waste)
├── Rechargeable batteries (avoid disposable waste)
└── Give away objects (avoid disposal waste)

PERFECT GENERAL-TO-SPECIFIC BRIDGE. ✓

Additionally: "En" in "ne pas en produire" refers back to "ordures ménagères" (household waste) from the BEFORE sentence. The pronoun "en" creates a grammatical link backward.

✅ CORRECT — States the principle that AFTER exemplifies, with grammatical link to BEFORE

Option B: "Seules des décisions prises en haut lieu pourraient inverser la tendance."
(Only decisions taken at the highest level could reverse the trend.)

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER lists INDIVIDUAL actions (drink tap water, repair appliances). These are things ORDINARY PEOPLE do, not "decisions taken at the highest level" (government/institutional decisions).

Option B says ONLY high-level decisions can help. The AFTER sentence describes PERSONAL actions. This creates a scope contradiction — institutional vs. individual.

If only government action could fix the problem, why would the text then list personal actions? The logic collapses.

❌ ELIMINATED — Scope contradiction (institutional scope vs. personal actions)

Option C: "En cause, les habitudes néfastes de consommation des pays industrialisés."
(To blame: the harmful consumption habits of industrialized countries.)

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER starts with "Il s'agit de..." (It's about...) and lists SOLUTIONS. Option C identifies a CAUSE (harmful habits).

CAUSE → SOLUTIONS can work logically. But the transition is abrupt. After blaming "industrialized countries" (a global, macro-level accusation), the text would jump to "drink tap water" (a micro-level personal tip). The scale mismatch is jarring.

More importantly: "Il s'agit de" introduces examples of a PRINCIPLE or METHOD. It does NOT introduce examples of "harmful habits." The actions listed (repair, recharge, give away) are GOOD habits, not bad ones. Option C talks about BAD habits; the AFTER sentence describes GOOD ones.

❌ ELIMINATED — "Il s'agit de" introduces positive actions, not examples of harmful habits

Option D: "Et malheureusement aucun indicateur ne donne l'espoir d'un changement."
(And unfortunately no indicator gives hope for change.)

Test FORWARD (connects to AFTER?):
AFTER lists concrete actions people CAN take. These are HOPEFUL, PRACTICAL solutions.

Option D says there's NO hope for change. Then the AFTER sentence gives specific ways to change. This is a TONAL CONTRADICTION — despair followed by practical optimism.

Option D: "No hope for change" (PESSIMISTIC)
         ↓ ❌ contradicts
AFTER: "Here's what you can do..." (OPTIMISTIC/PRACTICAL)

❌ ELIMINATED — Tonal contradiction (despair vs. practical solutions)

This introduces a gap-fill specific principle:

🏆 GOLDEN RULE #22: In gap-fill texts, the TONE of the inserted sentence must match the tone of the surrounding text. A pessimistic sentence cannot precede an optimistic list of solutions. A hopeful sentence cannot follow a definitive condemnation. Check for TONAL COHERENCE.

🎯 Answer: A — Aussi, les gestes les plus vertueux consistent à ne pas en produire.

THE MASTER LESSON FROM GAP 18:

This gap tested the GENERAL → SPECIFIC relationship. The gap provides the PRINCIPLE; the AFTER sentence provides the EXAMPLES.

This is one of the most common gap-fill patterns:

COMMON GAP-FILL PATTERNS:

Pattern 1: GENERAL → SPECIFIC
Gap = states a principle
After = gives examples ("Il s'agit de...", "Par exemple...", "Comme...")

Pattern 2: CAUSE → CONSEQUENCE
Gap = explains why
After = describes the result ("C'est pourquoi...", "C'est ainsi que...")

Pattern 3: CONTRAST
Gap = states one side
After = states the other ("En revanche...", ", eux,", "Pourtant...")

Pattern 4: BRIDGE
Gap = connects two different subtopics
Before = Topic A / After = Topic B / Gap = transition

Gap 16 was Pattern 2 (financial mechanism → revenue result).
Gap 17 was Pattern 3 (home pickup → contrasted with store drop-off).
Gap 18 was Pattern 1 (general principle → specific examples).

✅ WHAT YOU SHOULD DO

For Gap-Fill Texts Specifically:

1. ALWAYS read BEFORE and AFTER before looking at options.
This is Golden Rule #18. Your prediction is your most powerful weapon.

2. Pay obsessive attention to CONNECTORS.
The connector in the AFTER sentence tells you WHAT ROLE the gap plays:

AFTER starts with...The gap must provide...
"C'est ainsi que..."A mechanism/method
"C'est pourquoi..."A cause/reason
"Par exemple..."A general statement
"Il s'agit de..."A principle (examples follow)
"En revanche..." / ", eux,"Something to contrast WITH
"Pourtant..." / "Cependant..."Something that will be nuanced/contradicted
"En effet..."Something that will be confirmed/supported

3. Check PRONOUN references.
If the AFTER sentence contains "en," "y," "le," "eux," "cela," these pronouns REFER to something. Either they refer to BEFORE (the gap adds new info) or they refer to the GAP (the gap introduces the referent).

In Gap 18, "en" in "ne pas en produire" refers back to "ordures ménagères" in BEFORE. This grammatical link confirmed the answer.

4. Read the COMPLETE FLOW aloud (mentally).
After choosing, insert the option and read BEFORE + GAP + AFTER as one continuous paragraph. If it sounds natural, you're right. If there's a bump, reconsider.

5. Check tense compatibility.
BEFORE uses past tense? The gap should be compatible. AFTER uses future? The gap shouldn't be in a contradictory past.

⚠️ COMMON PITFALLS AND FAILURE MODES

PITFALL 17: The Forward-Only Trap (NEW)

First introduced here. Will reappear in Project 7.

What it is: An option that connects beautifully to the BEFORE sentence but contradicts the AFTER sentence. You choose it because you only checked backward.

How to detect: ALWAYS check BOTH directions. The Sandwich test is non-negotiable.

In this text: Gap 16, Option C connects to BEFORE (product doesn't end up in bin = result of take-back) but has no bridge to AFTER (800 million euros).

PITFALL 18: The Tonal Mismatch (NEW)

First introduced here. Will reappear in Projects 7, 21.

What it is: An option whose emotional tone clashes with the surrounding text — pessimism in an optimistic context, or vice versa.

How to detect: After checking logic, check FEELING. Does the option's mood match the paragraph's mood?

In this text: Gap 18, Option D is hopeless/pessimistic, but the AFTER sentence lists practical, hopeful actions.

PITFALL 19: The Missing Contrast (NEW)

First introduced here. Will reappear in Project 7.

What it is: A gap that requires CONTRAST (signaled by "eux," "en revanche," etc.) but the chosen option provides SIMILARITY instead.

How to detect: When the AFTER sentence contains a contrast marker, the gap MUST provide the other side of the contrast.

In this text: Gap 17, "eux" demands contrast. Option D (in-store drop-off) provides the same method as the AFTER sentence (in-store drop-off) — no contrast.

🌉 BRIDGE TO Chapter 7

Project 6 introduced gap-fill with a factual/informational text where the logic was concrete: money, methods, actions. The connectors were clear ("C'est ainsi," "eux") and the contradictions were detectable through facts and tenses.

Project 7 (Baudelaire — Chronological Gap-Fill) raises the stakes: the text is a biographical narrative where the gaps must be filled based on chronological sequence and life events.

You will learn:

  • How to track a biographical timeline and detect when a gap disrupts the sequence
  • How pronoun reference chains ("il," "son," "ce père") constrain which sentence can fit
  • Why emotional/biographical coherence matters as much as logical coherence
  • How to handle a gap-fill text where the "Before-After Sandwich" must account for a PERSON'S LIFE STORY

The difficulty shifts from "filling logical gaps" to "filling narrative gaps."
